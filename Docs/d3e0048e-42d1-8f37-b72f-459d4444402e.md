# Net Metering

## What is Net Metering?
The net metering initiative may be used by electricity consumers in Ontario who generate some of their own energy from a renewable resource. Net metering enables you to send surplus electricity from renewable resources to the distribution system for a credit towards your electricity bill. For a successive 12-month period, excess generation credits can be carried forward to offset future electricity bills. Customers with net meters can now combine energy storage with renewable energy systems.

## How Do I Get Started?
1.  **Find out more about the process:** Contact Oakville Hydro and the Electrical Safety Authority of Ontario (ESA). Oakville Hydro will provide technical specifications, the approval process, and how to get your net metering contract. The ESA must inspect and approve your system for grid connection.
2.  **Investigate your alternatives for renewable energy:** Renewable energy organizations, advisors, and suppliers can provide information. For urban clients, a solar system may be the best choice; wind or water systems may require ample space and face zoning regulations. In rural areas, wind, water, or agricultural biomass energy might be options.
3.  **Determine the size of the generation unit:** Use your prior bills to tailor your system to your individual energy demand. Since credits can only be carried forward for 12 months, oversizing a generation system does not increase the benefits.

## Application Process By Generation Classes

### MICRO GENERATION ( 10 KW)
*A micro generation plant is described as a system for generating electricity with a capability of 10 kW or less. The [latest Net Metering rules](https://www.ontario.ca/laws/regulation/050541) can be found on the Ontario Energy Board (OEB) website.*

**Application process:**
1.  **Information request:**
    *   Contact Oakville Hydro and ESA. Fill out a [Preliminary Consultation Information Request (PCIR)](/preliminary-consultation-information-request-distributed-energy-resource-der-connections).
2.  **Fill out the online application:**
    *   Complete the online application to Oakville Hydro including nameplate rated capacity, fuel type, technology type, and location.
3.  **ESA electrical permit application & Oakville Hydro review:**
    *   Submit plans to ESA for permit. Oakville Hydro will review the application.
4.  **Provision of information/offer to connect:**
    *   Oakville Hydro will provide connection process information, approvals, technical requirements, contractual requirements, and application forms. If requirements are met, an Offer to Connect and a Connection Agreement will be issued.
5.  **Customer develops a plan:**
    *   Review information from Oakville Hydro and ESA to develop an installation plan.
6.  **The decision to proceed and install:**
    *   If proceeding, issue payment for any fees outlined in the Offer to Connect and return a signed Connection Agreement.
7.  **Authorization to connect:**
    *   Receive authorization after ESA inspection. Oakville Hydro will then install a new meter.

### SMALL GENERATION (> 10 kW to < 1 MW)
*Small generation facilities have a capacity greater than 10 kW and up to 999 kW when connected to a distribution system. The [latest Net Metering rules](https://www.ontario.ca/laws/regulation/050541) can be found on the Ontario Energy Board (OEB) website.*

**Application process:**
1.  **Information request:**
    *   Contact Oakville Hydro and ESA. Fill out a [Preliminary Consultation Information Request (PCIR)](https://www.oakvillehydro.com/sites/oakvillehydro/files/2025-06/DERCP%20-%20PCIR%20and%20PCR.xlsx) and email to [engineering@oakvillehydro.com](mailto:engineering@oakvillehydro.com).
2.  **Provision of information:**
    *   Oakville Hydro will provide connection process information, approvals, technical requirements, contractual requirements, and application forms.
3.  **Customer develops a plan:**
    *   Review information from Oakville Hydro and ESA, develop an installation plan, prepare detailed engineering drawings, and submit to ESA and Oakville Hydro for design review.
4.  **Connection impact assessment:**
    *   Submit a [Connection Impact Assessment (CIA)](/media/4216/download?attachment) application and payment to Oakville Hydro. Oakville Hydro performs the CIA. A Study agreement will detail requirements and cost.
    *   **Simplified CIA qualification factors:**
        *   **Single-phase DER:** > 10kW and  30kW
        *   **Three-phase DER:** > 10kW and  50kW for <15kV; > 10kW and  100kW for  15kV
        *   A Hydro One Connection Impact Assessment (CIA) will be performed, if required.
5.  **The decision to proceed and install:**
    *   Upon successful CIA, choose to continue installation. A site visit will be scheduled. An offer to connect detailing costs and requirements will be sent. After payment and commitment, Oakville Hydro initiates work.
6.  **Commissioning and verification:**
    *   Submit Oakville Hydro pre-commissioning checklist and ESA inspection. Arrange for generator commissioning and testing. Oakville Hydro will send witnesses and complete its commissioning portion.
7.  **Connection agreement:**
    *   Agree to and sign the connection agreement. A letter of equivalency from an independent Professional Engineer is required.

## CIA Fee Schedule

| Project Type                     | Fees (Plus HST) |
| :------------------------------- | :--------------: |
| Small Generation (Simplified CIA)* | $6,000          |
| Small Generation (Full CIA)      | $8,000          |
| Mid & Large Generation (Full CIA)+ | $17,000         |

*Simplified CIA cost qualification factors:
1.  **Total site capacity:**
    *   **Single-phase DER:** >10kW and  30kW
    *   **Three-phase DER:** >10kW and  50kW for <15kV; >10kW and  100kW for  15kV
2.  No upstream assessment or upgrade required
3.  Feeder does not have a significant embedded generation impact on annual peak

+Generation > 1MW will require an additional CIA with the Transmitter (Hydro One) and projects > 10MW will need an SIA with the IESO.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does the process take?
The process is dependent on size. For small and mid-sized projects it may take anywhere from 3 to 6 months.

### How much does the process cost?
Prices can vary depending on project scope. Please submit an application for pricing.

### Am I eligible for generation?
Please submit an application to determine eligibility.

### How many credits do I have?
Please refer to your monthly hydro bill as it will indicate your current credit balance.

### How much does a generation system cost?
Please contact a renewable generation installation company for costs.
