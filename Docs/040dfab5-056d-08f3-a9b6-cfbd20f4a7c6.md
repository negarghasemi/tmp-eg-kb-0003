# Parking and the ENERGY STAR Score in the United States and Canada

*This document provides technical details on how the ENERGY STAR score accounts for parking areas in property energy performance assessments.* 

## Overview

The **ENERGY STAR score** assesses a property's energy performance relative to its peers, considering factors like climate, weather, and business activities. Parking areas are **not eligible** for their own ENERGY STAR score but are accommodated as common amenities in other commercial building types (e.g., offices, hotels).

*The goal is to rate the primary use of the building, not the parking.* 

### Key Aspects:

*   **Technical Approach**: An engineered model estimates energy use for parking. This estimate is subtracted from the building's actual energy use to evaluate the building's performance without the parking component.
*   **Property Types**: Parking areas can be entered for all property types. Adjustments are incorporated for eligible property types, including open parking lots, completely enclosed/underground garages, and partially enclosed garages. If parking energy consumption is separately metered, it doesn't need to be entered.
*   **Adjustments**: The parking adjustment model is based on engineered assumptions for basic energy requirements:
    *   **Lighting Energy**: Varies by parking type (power density and hours).
    *   **Ventilation Energy**: For fully enclosed structures lacking natural ventilation.
    *   **Heating Energy**: For enclosed garages in very cold climates.
*   **Release Date**: The model is periodically updated.
    *   **Most Recent Update**: August 2023
    *   **Previous Updates**: August 2018, July 2013
    *   **Original Release**: June 2001

## Theoretical Background

Energy use in a parking area primarily involves lighting, ventilation, and heating. Requirements depend on parking type and location.

### Lighting Energy

Lighting power density (watts per square foot) is the key metric, referenced from ASHRAE Energy Standard and building codes. Hours of operation are also considered.

#### 2023 Lighting Adjustment:

*   **Completely enclosed and partially enclosed parking garages**: **0.11 watts/ft** (general lighting), **0.95 watts/ft** (daylight transition zones).
*   **Open parking lots**: **0.037 watts/ft**.
*   **Hours of Operation**:
    *   Completely and partially enclosed garages: **24 hours**.
    *   Open parking lots: **12 hours**.

#### 2018 Lighting Adjustment:

*   **Completely enclosed and partially enclosed parking garages**: **0.30 watts/ft**.
*   **Open parking lots**: **0.15 watts/ft**.
*   **Hours of Operation**:
    *   Completely and partially enclosed garages: **24 hours**.
    *   Open parking lots: **16 hours**.

*Note: The 2023 lighting adjustment is immediately applicable for all Canadian property types. For the U.S., it's phased in, initially for vehicle dealership scores. Other property types (Data Centers, Hotels, K-12 Schools, Multifamily Housing, Offices, Residence Halls and Dormitories, Retail Stores and Supermarkets, Warehouses and Worship Facilities) will continue to use the 2018 adjustment until comprehensive model updates.* 

### Ventilation Energy (*2018 adjustment unchanged for 2023*)

*   Required for **completely enclosed parking garages** without natural ventilation.
*   **Maximum ventilation rate**: **1.0 cfm/ft**.
*   **Operation**: Fans operate at **full power for 6 hours/day** and in **setback mode (0.05 cfm/ft)** for **18 hours/day**.
*   *Ventilation is assumed unnecessary for partially enclosed garages and open parking lots.* 

### Heating Energy (*2018 adjustment unchanged for 2023*)

*   Typically used in **completely enclosed parking garages in very cold climates**.
*   **Primary heat load**: Due to ventilation (envelope loads ignored).
*   **Heating fuel**: Natural gas, with **80% system efficiency**.
*   **Target temperature**: **40F (4.4C)**.
*   Energy estimates are based on ventilation load multiplied by heating degree days with a base of 40F.

### Predicted Parking Source Energy (Example Equation - 2023 Adjustments)

*For an Auto Dealership in the United States with open, partially enclosed, and completely enclosed heated parking:*

= **0.4147** x (Area of Open Parking) x *source-site ratio for electricity*
+ **4.266** x (Area of Partially Enclosed Parking) x *source-site ratio for electricity*
+ (**4.297** + **2.391**) x (Area of Completely Enclosed Parking) x *source-site ratio for electricity*
+ **0.009354** x HDD_Base40F x (Area of Completely Enclosed and Heated Parking) x *source-site ratio for natural gas*

*Note: The ENERGY STAR score uses kBtu for the U.S. and Gigajoules (GJ) for Canada. Portfolio Manager can display results in either unit.* 

## Example Calculation (Office with Parking in the United States - using 2018 adjustments)

### 1. User enters building data into Portfolio Manager

*   **Energy Data Summary**:
    *   **Electricity**: **3,500,000 kWh**
    *   **Natural Gas**: **4,000 therms**

*   **Office Property Use Details Summary**:
    *   **Gross floor area**: **200,000 ft**
    *   **Weekly operating hours**: **80**
    *   **Workers on the main shift**: **300**
    *   **Number of computers**: **300**
    *   **Percent cooled**: **100%**
    *   **HDD (Heating Degree Days)**: **4937**
    *   **CDD (Cooling Degree Days)**: **1046**

*   **Parking Use Details Summary**:
    *   **Open Parking Lot Size**: **10,000 ft**
    *   **Partially Enclosed Parking Garage Size**: **20,000 ft**
    *   **Completely Enclosed Parking Garage Size**: **30,000 ft**
    *   **Supplemental Heating**: **No**

### 2. Portfolio Manager computes the actual source EUI

*   **Billed Source Energy Calculation**:

| Fuel        | Billing Units    | Site kBtu Multiplier | Site kBtu    | Source kBtu Multiplier | Source kBtu  |
| :---------- | :--------------- | :------------------- | :----------- | :--------------------- | :----------- |
| Electricity | 3,500,000 kWh    | 3.412                | 11,942,000   | 2.80                   | 33,437,600   |
| Natural gas | 4,000 therms     | 100                  | 400,000      | 1.05                   | 420,000      |
| **Total**   |                  |                      |              |                        | **33,857,600** |

*   **Predicted Parking Energy Calculation**:
    *   = (2.989  10,000  2.8) + (8.967  20,000  2.8) + (11.357  30,000  2.8) + 0
    *   **Predicted Parking Source Energy = 1,539,832 kBtu**

*   **Actual Source Energy (for ENERGY STAR score)**:
    *   33,857,600 kBtu (Total Billed Source Energy)  1,539,832 kBtu (Predicted Parking Source Energy)
    *   **Actual Source Energy = 32,317,768 kBtu**

*   **Actual Source EUI**:
    *   32,317,768 kBtu / 200,000 ft
    *   **Actual Source EUI = 161.6 kBtu/ft**

### 3. Portfolio Manager computes the predicted source EUI

*   *Based on property use details and regression equations.*

| Variable                           | Actual Building Value | Reference Centering Value | Building Centered Variable | Coefficient | Coefficient * Centered Variable |
| :--------------------------------- | :-------------------- | :------------------------ | :------------------------- | :---------- | :------------------------------ |
| Constant                           | -                     | -                         | -                          | 143.1       | 143.1                           |
| Square Foot (max value of 100,000) | 100,000               | 12,342                    | 87,658                     | 0.0006768   | 59.33                           |
| Weekly Operating Hours             | 80                    | 54.09                     | 25.91                      | 0.6130      | 15.88                           |
| Number of Workers per 1,000 ft    | 1.500                 | 2.056                     | -0.5560                    | 15.90       | -8.840                          |
| Number of Computers per 1,000 ft  | 1.500                 | 3.028                     | -1.528                     | 10.13       | -15.48                          |
| Percent Cooled  Ln (Cooling Degree Days) | 6.953                 | 6.332                     | 0.6210                     | 4.529       | 2.813                           |
| Small Bank                         | 0.0000                | NA                        | 0.0000                     | 82.87       | 0.0000                          |
| Heating Degree Days                | 4937                  | 924                       | 4013                       | 0.004693    | 18.83                           |
| **Total**                          |                       |                           |                            |             | **215.6**                       |

*   **Predicted Source EUI = 215.6 kBtu/ft**

### 4. Portfolio Manager computes the energy efficiency ratio

*   **Energy Efficiency Ratio** = Actual Source EUI (Step 2) / Predicted Source EUI (Step 3)
*   = 161.6 / 215.6
*   **Energy Efficiency Ratio = 0.7495**

### 5. Portfolio Manager uses the efficiency ratio to assign a score via a lookup table

*   A ratio of **0.7495** falls between 0.7406 and 0.7511 in the lookup table for offices.
*   **The ENERGY STAR score is 61**.
