# Energy Saving DIY Tips

Take a look below and start saving energy now!

## Begin With Simple Steps

### Air Filters
*Summary:* Regularly check and replace furnace filters per manufacturer's recommendation to maintain efficiency. Your furnace works harder with dirty filters. Hot water radiators need bleeding at least once or twice a year to work efficiently. Oil furnaces require annual maintenance, while natural gas and propane furnaces need servicing every two years. Ensure heat flows freely by not blocking vents and air grills with objects or furniture.

### Cold Air / Drafts
*Summary:* Drafts and cold air can enter through small openings. Electrical outlets on outside walls are common entry points; an inexpensive foam insulator can stop these drafts. Weatherstripping is an easy and effective way to prevent cold air from entering and warm air from leaving your house.

### LED Light Bulbs
*Summary:* Switching to LED light bulbs can lead to significant energy savings. These bulbs last for as much as 100,000 hours, or up to 14 years, and can save you a few hundred dollars a year if you convert all your lighting to LED.

### Window Coverings
*Summary:* Utilize window coverings for energy saving. Heavy fabric curtains or insulated blinds can significantly reduce cold, drafty air coming through your windows.

### Water Heater
*Summary:* Consider wrapping your water heater with a blanket, especially if it's an older model or located in a cold area like a basement. Water heater blankets are inexpensive (around $30-$40) and offer worthwhile cost savings.

### Drying
*Summary:* To save energy, skip the dryer and let your clothes dry naturally in the breeze and sunshine.

## Financial Help & Incentives

### Free or Low Cost Income-Qualified Programs

*   **Federal Oil to Heat Pump Affordability Program**
    *   **Description:** Eligible Canadian homeowners can receive up to $10,000 to switch from oil heating to a new energy-efficient heat pump.
    *   **Eligibility:** Home is heated by oil; household income is at or below the median household after-tax income level.
    *   **Eligible Measures:** Necessary electrical and mechanical upgrades for a new heat pump system, installation of outdoor piping loops for ground source heat pumps, safe removal of oil tank, installation of backup electrical heating (as required), and switching over other oil-using household systems (e.g., water heater).

*   **100% Free Cold Climate Air Source Heat Pumps (Save on Energy)**
    *   **Description:** Offered through the Energy Affordability Program for qualifying Ontario households living in electrically heated homes. Includes a two-year free maintenance warranty and a 10-year equipment and labour warranty.
    *   **Additional Benefits:** Qualifying households may also be eligible for other free energy efficiency upgrades, such as professionally installed replacement ENERGY STAR appliances (refrigerators, freezers, window air conditioners, lighting), weatherstripping, attic and basement insulation, and smart thermostats/power strips.

|*   **Home Winterproofing Program (HWP) | Enbridge Gas**
    *   **Description:** Streamlined with the Energy Affordability Program to offer free home energy upgrades. If your home and household income levels qualify, you may receive free insulation, draft proofing, a smart thermostat, Energy Star appliances, and more.
    *   *Note:* CCET is a community distribution partner with CLEAResult, the authorized delivery partner for this program. Contact CCET for a free Energy Saving Kit.

*   **Hydro One Low Income Energy Assistance Program (LEAP)**
    *   **Description:** Pays up to $1,000 (or up to $1,200 if your home is electrically heated) of the amount owing on your electricity bill for lower-income households.

*   **Save on Energy  Energy Affordability Program**
    *   **Description:** Provides expert advice and energy-saving home upgrades completely free of charge for income-eligible households (homeowners or renters who pay monthly electricity bills).
    *   **Eligibility:** Depends on household income level from the previous year, number of occupants, or receipt of a qualifying government/energy-support benefit. Residents of social housing may also be eligible.
    *   **Types of Support:** Comprehensive upgrades or Energy Saving Kits.

*   **Ontario Electricity Support Program (OESP)**
    *   **Description:** An Ontario Energy Board (OEB) program that lowers electricity bills for lower-income households with a monthly credit applied directly to eligible customers bills.
    *   **Credit Amount:** Depends on household income and household size.

### Rebates & Incentives

*   **Hydro One - Ontario Electricity Rebate (OER)**
    *   **Description:** Provides eligible customers with a 13.1% reduction on their electricity bill before HST (as of November 1, 2024).
    *   **Automatic Eligibility:** Residential and small commercial customers (billed under a Residential or General Service Energy rate class) using less than 50 kW; commercial accounts greater than 50 kW but using less than 250,000 kWh annually.

*   **Enbridge Fixed Incentive Program**
    *   **Description:** Helps offset the cost of upgrading to energy-efficient natural gas technology. Includes support from an Energy Solutions Advisor to identify opportunities, quantify savings, and provide incentives.

*   **Enbridge Business Programs and Incentives**
    *   **Description:** Offers rebates and incentives for equipment upgrades, new construction, retrofits, and energy assessments for businesses.

*   **Home Renovation Savings Program (Enbridge Gas and Save on Energy)**

        | Air Sealing                      | Up to $250           |
    *   **Rebates not requiring a Home Energy Assessment (for one or more upgrades):**
        | Upgrade Category                 | Rebate Amount        |

### Loans & Financing

*   **Canada Greener Homes Loan (CMHC)**
    *   **Description:** Offers up to $40,000 in interest-free loans for terms up to 10 years for eligible retrofits.

*   **Clean Energy Loan (RBC)**
    *   **Description:** Available to RBC customers for purchasing hybrid cars, ENERGY STAR appliances, and renewable energy projects. Offers either a 1% interest rate reduction or a $100 rebate on a clean energy audit with the loan.

*   **Mortgage Insurance Loan Rebate (CMHC, Sagen, Canada Guaranty)**
    *   **Description:** A 25% mortgage loan insurance rebate offered when buying a certified green home (e.g., R-2000, ENERGY STAR, Built Green, LEED).

*   **Canada Green Affordable Housing Program**
    *   **Description:** Funds up to 100% of eligible retrofit costs to a maximum of $170,000 per unit (or forgivable loans of $85,000 per unit or 80% of eligible costs) for homeowners meeting affordability criteria. Requires pre-retrofit activities such as energy audits and energy modeling studies.
