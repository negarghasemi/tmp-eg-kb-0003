# ENERGY STAR Scores

## ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager Update
Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) announced two new ENERGY STAR scores for **Museums** and **Libraries** in Canada as part of the **February 2024** update of the ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager benchmarking tool. These scores provide an \"at-a-glance\" reference to assess building energy efficiency relative to similar buildings.

## What is the significance of the ENERGY STAR score?
An energy performance score serves as an external reference for energy managers to gauge how efficiently a building consumes energy compared to similar structures. It is a complex rating system that normalizes for various factors like local climate and occupancy, comparing a building's energy consumption against a peer group.

*   The ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager utilizes a **1-100 ENERGY STAR score**.
    *   A score of **50** indicates **median energy performance**.
    *   A score of **75 or more** signifies **top performance**.

Organizations can leverage these scores to:
*   Evaluate energy performance across their portfolio of buildings.
*   Compare performance against similar buildings nationwide.
*   Identify buildings with the greatest potential for energy improvement and recognition.

### Performance Score Features for Canadian Buildings:
*   **1-to-100 score** for specific building types (NRCan is actively working to expand this to more building types).
*   A score of **50** means the building is at the **50th percentile**; half of its peer group performs better, and half perform worse.
*   Includes **whole-building actual energy performance score** and **weather-normalized energy use intensity**.
*   Energy performance scores are derived from the **Survey of Commercial and Institutional Energy Use (SCIEU)**.
*   Data accounts for various factors such as weather, operating hours, number of occupants, and plug load.
*   **Energy use intensities** are available for almost all buildings, which can be used for monitoring and tracking energy performance even if a 1-100 ENERGY STAR score is not available.

## How is the ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager score calculated?
The Portfolio Manager's rating system estimates a building's energy intensity based on user-inputted information (e.g., size, location, occupancy, number of computers). It then compares this against actual energy data to rank the building relative to its peers.

*   **Basis of Calculation**: Calculations are based on **source energy**, which is considered the most equitable method for comparing building energy performance and correlates best with environmental impact and energy cost.
*   **Data Analysis**: To estimate energy use at different performance levels, Natural Resources Canada conducts statistical analysis using data from the **Survey of Commercial and Institutional Energy Use**, a national survey by Statistics Canada.
*   **Rigorous Analysis Process**: NRCan's analysis for eligible building types includes:
    *   Ensuring data quality and quantity supports a reliable performance score.
    *   Creating statistical models to correlate energy data with operational characteristics, identifying key energy drivers.
    *   Testing these models against real-world buildings.

## ENERGY STAR scores for specific building types
Natural Resources Canada currently offers **1-100 ENERGY STAR scores for seven specific building types** and is continuously working to provide scores for additional building types.
