# Combination Systems

## Overview

**Looking to heat water and space with a single system?** Some homes simply need less heating. This can be due to:

*   **Excellent insulation**
*   **Low air leakage**
*   **Efficient use of space**
*   **Location in a milder climate**

In such homes, a single appliance can perform double duty as a water heater and a space heater, known as a **combination system**.

## System Components and Functionality

Combination systems typically consist of:

*   A **fan coil** installed with ductwork.
*   A **small pump** that circulates water from the water heater through the fan coil.

Heated water can also be circulated through **in-floor piping** to provide floor and radiant heating. Some combination systems may use **boilers** instead of water heaters.

## Energy Efficiency and Regulations

*   Combination systems are **not currently regulated** under Canadas *Energy Efficiency Regulations*.

### Recommendations for Performance Evaluation

*   Look for a system that has been rated **as a system** using the new Canadian Standards Association standard **CSA P.9***Test method for determining the performance of combined space and water heating systems (combos)*.
*   This test method provides a **thermal performance factor** that consolidates measures for both space heating and water heating.
*   *Caution*: If a system only has an energy factor (EF) rating for the water heater, the energy performance picture is **incomplete**, as the water heater's performance will differ in space heating mode.

### Professional Credentials

*   When purchasing a combination system, verify that your contractor or designer has the appropriate credentials to ensure proper installation and performance.
*   For example, the Heating, Refrigeration and Air Conditioning Institute of Canada (**HRAI**) offers a certification in combination systems.
