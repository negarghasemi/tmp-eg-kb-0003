# Oil to Heat Pump Affordability Program

This program helps homeowners switch from oil heating to eligible electric heat pump systems, aiming to save thousands on heating bills and reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

## Program Scope and Eligibility by Region

*   **Information for applicants residing in:** Alberta, Northwest Territories, Quebec, and Saskatchewan.
    *   _Note: Details vary for residents of British Columbia, New Brunswick, Newfoundland and Labrador, Nova Scotia, Ontario, Prince Edward Island, or Yukon. Manitoba applicants should refer to their specific provincial page._
*   **Eligibility Requirement:** Your home must be connected to an integrated electricity grid.
*   **Ineligible Locations (due to grid requirements):** Nunavut, remote areas of Quebec, and other remote areas of Canada.
    *   _Contact OHPA-CAMT@nrcan-rncan.gc.ca if unsure about your location's eligibility._

## Eligibility Criteria Overview

To be eligible for an upfront payment of up to $10,000, applicants must meet the following conditions:

*   Be a **Canadian homeowner**.
*   **Heat your home with oil**.
*   Your **household income is at or below the median household after-tax income**.
*   Meet all other eligibility criteria.

## What is Available Through the Program (Up to $10,000 Upfront)

Eligible homeowners can receive an **upfront payment of up to $10,000** for the purchase and installation of eligible heat pump systems, including:

*   **Purchase and installation** of eligible heat pump systems.
*   **Necessary electrical and mechanical upgrades** for the new electric heat pump system.
*   **Safe removal of your oil tank** (_Note: While OHPA does not require removal, the cost is an eligible expense if your province/territory mandates it_).
*   **Installation of a supplemental electric heating system** (as required).
*   **Switching over other oil-using household systems** (e.g., an electric water heater, where necessary).
*   **Installation of outdoor piping loops** for ground source heat pumps.

_This funding can be combined with additional financial assistance from other existing federal, provincial, territorial, and utility programs._

### Detailed Eligibility Criteria

Applicants must meet all eligibility requirements throughout the program, including complying with mandatory technical criteria and conditions, and completing an attestation form. A home energy evaluation *may not be required* for the OHPA program.

## Application Process

*   **New Applicants:** Use the \"Check your eligibility\" tool.
*   **Indigenous Community Applications:** Learn more about specific application processes.
*   **Process Overview:** Understand the steps and required documents by reviewing \"How the process works.\"
*   **Ready to Apply:**
    *   **For British Columbia, Manitoba, New Brunswick, Newfoundland and Labrador, Nova Scotia, Ontario, Prince Edward Island, or Yukon:** You must refer to your provincial/territorial program to apply.
    *   **For all other provinces:** Ensure eligibility and have all required documents ready before applying.

## Important Warnings

*   **Be wary of high-pressure sales tactics:** NRCan has not approved any third parties to make unsolicited contact with homeowners to register or participate in the program.
*   **Homeowners must apply themselves:** Third parties (including service organizations, energy advisors, and contractors) may not apply for the homeowner or offer to apply on their behalf under any circumstances.
