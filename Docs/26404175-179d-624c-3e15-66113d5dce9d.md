# Eligible Retrofits and Grant Amounts - Canada Greener Homes Initiative

_**Note:** The Canada Greener Homes Grant and Loan programs are now closed to new applications. Requests submitted by the deadline will continue to be processed._

## Program Overview

As part of the Canada Greener Homes Initiative, participants had access to:

*   **Grants from $125 to $5,000** for eligible and recommended home retrofits.
*   **Up to $600** as a maximum contribution toward the total costs of pre- and post-retrofit EnerGuide evaluations.
*   **Interest-free loans of up to $40,000** with a repayment term of 10 years for major home retrofits (_applications for loans closed October 1, 2025_).

**Maximum Grant Amount:** A household could receive up to **$5,600**, including up to $5,000 for eligible retrofits and up to $600 for evaluations.

_If you live in Ontario, Quebec, Nova Scotia, or Northern and off-grid communities, refer to specific provincial/territorial web pages for details._

## Ineligible Retrofits

The Canada Greener Homes Initiative aims to improve the energy efficiency of existing homes. Additions to existing homes are generally not eligible. Renovations done as part of a rebuild may also have limited eligibility. It was highly recommended to contact the program for advice on eligibility before starting renovations.

**Retrofits that were not eligible:**

*   Retrofits done before a pre-retrofit evaluation.
*   Retrofits done before December 1, 2020.
*   Products installed in new home additions built between pre- and post-retrofit EnerGuide evaluations:
    *   Home insulation: _Portions for new areas (e.g., wall, attic insulation) were ineligible and could reduce the total grant amount._
    *   Windows and doors: _New windows and doors in new additions were ineligible._
    *   Components requiring 100% upgrades: _e.g., crawl space wall and header insulation, exposed floor insulation._
*   Leased or rented equipment.
*   Air conditioners.
*   Tankless or storage water heaters other than heat pump water heaters.
*   Skylights.
*   Furnaces (unless in northern or off-grid communities).
*   Boilers (unless in northern or off-grid communities).
*   Roofs.
*   Windows and doors in a garage.

### Home Insulation

Properly insulating your home reduces heating and cooling costs and improves comfort. The effectiveness is measured by R-value or RSI value (higher is better). All products must be purchased in Canada from a Canadian distributor.

#### Insulate your Roof Assemblies (Attic/Cathedral Ceiling/Flat Roof)

*   **Eligibility:** Add insulation to at least 20% of the total roof assembly area, increasing the insulation value as per the table below. Additional insulation must be added to the same location as existing insulation.
*   **Important:**
    *   Grant amount is pro-rated based on the percentage of the total roof assembly insulated.
    *   The total grant for any combination of roof insulation cannot exceed **$1,800**.

| Level of insulation at pre-retrofit evaluation | Attics (Insulation level to reach) | Attics (Maximum Grant) | Cathedral Ceilings & Flat Roofs (Insulation level to reach) | Cathedral Ceilings & Flat Roofs (Maximum Grant) |
| :--------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------- | :--------------------- | :---------------------------------------- | :------------------------------ |
| No insulation                                  | RSI 8.81 (R-50)                  | **$1,800**             | RSI 3.52 (R-20)                           | **$600**                        |
| <= RSI 2.11 (R-12)                             | RSI 8.81 (R-50)                  | **$1,800**             | RSI 4.93 (R-28)                           | **$600**                        |
| > RSI 2.11 (R-12) and <= RSI 4.40 (R-25)       | RSI 8.81 (R-50)                  | **$600**               | RSI 4.93 (R-28)                           | **$250**                        |
| > RSI 4.40 (R-25) and <= RSI 6.16 (R-35)       | RSI 8.81 (R-50)                  | **$250**               | N/A                                       | **N/A**                         |

#### Exterior Wall Insulation

*   **Eligibility:** Add insulation to at least 20% of your exterior wall area, excluding foundation walls.
*   **Important:**
    *   Grant amount is pro-rated based on the total percentage of walls insulated.
    *   Semi-detached/end unit row houses: Max 75% of listed amounts.
    *   Middle unit row houses: Max 50% of listed amounts.
    *   No grants for insulating walls between individual units.

| Percentage of wall area insulated | From RSI 1.32 (R-7.5) to RSI 2.11 (R-12) | > RSI 2.11 (R-12) to RSI 3.52 (R-20) | > RSI 3.52 (R-20) |
| :-------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------- | :---------------- |
| 20%                               | $660                                     | $760                                 | $1,000            |
| 100%                              | $3,300                                   | $3,800                               | $5,000            |

#### Exposed Floor Insulation

*   **Eligibility:** Insulate the entire exposed floor area (minimum 11 square metres or 120 square feet). This includes floors above attached garages or areas exposed to the outdoors. Crawl spaces are excluded.

| Option                                   | Grant Amount |
| :--------------------------------------- | :----------- |
| Minimum additional insulation, RSI 3.52 (R-20) | $350         |

#### Basement Insulation

*   **Eligibility:**
    *   Add insulation to at least 20% of the total foundation wall area.
    *   Seal and insulate a minimum 80% of your entire basement header area.
    *   Seal and insulate a minimum 50% of your entire basement slab area.
*   **Important:**
    *   Combined grants for basement walls, headers, and slabs are possible.
    *   Semi-detached/end unit row houses: Max 75% of listed amounts.
    *   Middle unit row houses: Max 50% of listed amounts.
    *   No grants for insulating walls/headers between adjacent units.
    *   If both basement and crawl space are present, grants are pro-rated to a maximum of $1,500 based on total foundation wall area and insulation added.

| Seal and insulate at least:                                      | Grant Amount |
| :--------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------- |
| 80% of entire basement header area (min. RSI 3.52 / R-20)        | $240         |
| 50% of entire basement slab (min. RSI 0.62 / R-3.5)              | $400         |

| Percentage of wall area insulated | From RSI 1.76 (R-10) to RSI 3.87 (R-22) | > RSI 3.87 (R-22) |
| :-------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------- | :---------------- |
| 20%                               | $210                                    | $300              |
| 100%                              | $1,050                                  | $1,500            |

#### Crawl Space Insulation

*   **Eligibility:**
    *   Add insulation to crawl space walls and headers, or ceiling.
    *   If both crawl space and basement are present, must insulate a minimum of 20% of the total foundation wall area.
    *   If also insulating basement walls, maximum combined grant for both areas is $1,500.
*   **Important:**
    *   Semi-detached/end unit row houses: Max 75% of listed amounts.
    *   Middle unit row houses: Max 50% of listed amounts.
    *   If both walls/headers and ceiling are upgraded, only the larger grant amount is received.
    *   If both basement and crawl space are present, grants are pro-rated to a maximum of $1,500.

| Option                                                         | From RSI 1.76 (R-10) to RSI 3.87 (R-22) | > RSI 3.87 (R-22) | > RSI 4.23 (R-24) |
| :------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------- | :---------------- | :---------------- |
| Insulate 100% of crawl spaces exterior wall area (incl. header) | $1,040                                  | $1,300            | $1,300            |

### Air Sealing

Improving airtightness is a crucial retrofit. Your Renovation Upgrade Report provides a target for improvement. Achieving these levels typically requires professional assistance.

| Options                                              | Grant Amount |
| :--------------------------------------------------- | :----------- |
| Meet the target in your Renovation Upgrade Report    | $550         |
| Reach 10% better than target (0.9 x target value)    | $810         |
| Reach 20% better than target (0.8 x target value)    | $1,000       |

_**Note:** Improving the building envelope or air sealing may lead to inadequate ventilation or increased radon levels. A balanced mechanical ventilation system (HRV/ERV) may help. Consult your energy advisor and a qualified ventilation contractor._

### Windows and Doors

Upgrading to high-performance ENERGY STAR certified windows and doors can improve comfort and save energy.

*   **Eligibility:**
    *   All equipment must be purchased in Canada from a Canadian distributor.
    *   Equipment must be on eligible product lists.
    *   Keep ENERGY STAR labels until after post-retrofit evaluation.
*   **Important:**
    *   A completely new window or door and frame can be inserted into an existing frame. Replacements of only glass, sash, or door without a frame are not eligible.
    *   Each rough opening is eligible for a grant of up to $250, to a maximum of $5,000.
    *   _A rough opening is a structurally stable opening for one or more windows/doors. The grant is per rough opening, not per number of units. Skylights are not eligible._

| Options                                                      | Grant Amount (per rough opening) |
| :----------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------- |
| Replace windows or sliding glass doors with ENERGY STAR certified models | $125                             |
| Replace windows or sliding glass doors with ENERGY STAR Most Efficient models | $250                             |
| Replace hinged doors (with/without sidelites or transoms) with ENERGY STAR certified models | $125                             |

### Thermostat

Smart or programmable thermostats improve comfort, save energy, and reduce your carbon footprint by allowing automated temperature adjustments.

*   **Eligibility:**
    *   Must be combined with another energy efficiency retrofit from the Canada Greener Homes Initiative.
    *   _Exclusions: New heat pumps include new thermostats and cannot be combined. Resiliency measures and thermostats must be combined with another energy efficiency measure._
    *   All equipment must be purchased in Canada from a Canadian distributor.

| Option                                                  | Grant Amount |
| :------------------------------------------------------ | :----------- |
| Replace a manual thermostat with a programmable or smart/adaptive thermostat | $50          |

### Space and Water Heating

Furnaces and boilers are generally not eligible, except for northern and off-grid communities. Space heating accounts for over 60% of energy use in Canadian homes. Switching to energy-efficient heating equipment like heat pumps can save energy and reduce utility bills.

_A heat pump transfers environmental energy for heating or cooling, offering higher energy efficiency than traditional systems. Cold-climate heat pumps are designed for Canadian winters. Ground source heat pumps are effective in all climates._

_**Note:** Your new heat pump must be installed by a licensed and trained professional and be on an eligible product list. All equipment must be purchased in Canada from a Canadian distributor._

#### Ground Source Heat Pumps

*   **Eligibility:**
    *   Unit must be installed by a licensed and trained professional (proof of license recommended).
    *   Attestation recommended confirming installation according to CSA standard ANSI/CSA/IGSHPA C448 SERIES.
    *   New pump must be on the list of eligible products. Direct expansion (DX), water-to-water, and brine-to-water systems are not eligible.
    *   All equipment must be purchased in Canada from a Canadian distributor.
    *   Must meet Canada's Energy Efficiency Regulations technical specifications.

| Equipment                                                            | Grant Amount |
| :------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------- |
| Install new or replace existing full ground source heat pump system (indoor & outdoor components) | $5,000       |
| Replace ground source heat pump unit only (indoor component only)    | $3,000       |

#### Air Source Heat Pumps (ASHP) and Cold Climate Air Source Heat Pumps (ccASHP)

*   **Eligibility:**
    *   Unit must be installed by a licensed and trained professional (proof of license recommended).
    *   Installation must include all components (indoor and outdoor units, and furnace/air handler if applicable).
    *   Heat pump system must be capable of distributing heat throughout the entire conditioned space, including the basement (if 1.8m or higher ceiling joist distance).
    *   Attestation recommended confirming heat distribution capability.
    *   New pump must be on the list of eligible products. All components must be compatible and listed together.
    *   All equipment must be purchased in Canada from a Canadian distributor.
*   **Important:** Air-to-water heat pump systems are **not eligible**.

### Renewable Energy Systems

Renewable energy is derived from natural processes that are replenished at a rate equal to or faster than consumption (e.g., solar energy).

#### Solar Photovoltaic (PV) System (including solar panels and inverters)

*   **Eligibility:**
    *   All equipment must be purchased in Canada from a Canadian distributor.
    *   System must comprise one or more PV panels and inverter(s) certified to CSA Standards.
    *   Total system peak power capacity must be equal to or greater than 1.0 kW DC.
    *   For grid-connected systems, a copy of the approval for interconnection from the local authority is required.
    *   Solar panels must be permanently mounted (on house or ground) on the property.
*   **Additional Information:**
    *   No national/provincial/territorial certifications for solar PV system designers/installers. Homeowners can retain a contractor or self-install (personal labor costs are not eligible).
    *   Eligible for first-time installation or addition to an existing system.
    *   System must be designed and installed according to local building and electrical requirements.
    *   Recommended to have a qualified solar PV professional assess the home as Solar Ready before pre-retrofit evaluation.
    *   For rooftop installations, CSA *SPE-900-13 Solar photovoltaic rooftop-installation best practices guideline* is recommended.
    *   Building permits may be required.

| Option                                                              | Grant Amount |
| :------------------------------------------------------------------ | :----------- |
| Install a solar photovoltaic system (incl. panels & inverters) with capacity >= 1.0 kW DC | $1,000 per kW |

_**Note:** The grant amount is pro-rated based on the added capacity (e.g., 3.5 kW PV capacity = $3,500 grant)._

### Resiliency Measures

Measures to protect your home from extreme weather events. These must be combined with an energy efficiency retrofit from the Canada Greener Homes Initiative.

#### Batteries connected to solar photovoltaic (PV) systems

*   **Eligibility:**
    *   Battery system must be connected to a permanently mounted photovoltaic system.
    *   Can be for new system, replacement, or supplement to existing system.
    *   Batteries must be rated for deep cycle (any technology).
    *   Inverter and charge controller purchase/installation costs can be included.
    *   Batteries must be permanently installed.
*   **Ineligible:** Portable batteries and electric vehicles.
*   **Best Practices:**
    *   Consider inverter with true sinewave output, min. 1200 Watts (W) continuous, min. 2500 W surge capability.
    *   Consider battery system with min. 4500 Watt hours total capacity at 20 hours.
    *   Inverter and charge controller should be certified to CSA C22.2 107.1 or CSA C22.2 62109.
    *   Installation or inspection by a licensed and trained professional.
    *   Electrical permit may be required.

| Option                                    | Grant Amount |
| :---------------------------------------- | :----------- |
| Batteries connected to photovoltaic systems | $1,000       |

#### Roofing Membrane - Self-adhering roofing underlayment applied to entire roof

*   **Eligibility:**
    *   Underlayment must be self-adhering.
    *   Applied to the entire surface area of the roof covering enclosed spaces (incl. attached garages).
    *   Certified to ASTM D1970 / D1970M or CSA A123.22.
*   **Best Practices:**
    *   Underlayment should be compatible with roof type.
    *   Ensure adequate roof venting to prevent condensation.
    *   Torched on membranes and flat roofs are not eligible.

| Option                                                              | Grant Amount |
| :------------------------------------------------------------------ | :----------- |
| Roofing membraneself-adhering roofing underlayment applied to entire roof | $150         |

#### Basement Wall Waterproofing

*   **Eligibility:**
    *   Waterproofing on the exterior side of below-grade basement wall with rubberized or polymer membranes (waterproof-rated spray, trowel-on, roll-on, sheet materials).
    *   Minimum 80% of below-grade wall area must be waterproofed (applies to all exterior facing walls, not party walls).
*   **Ineligible:** Crawl space and slab-on-grade foundations. Damp/moisture-proofing membranes (e.g., simple paint brush or roller applied interior sealants).
*   **Best Practices:**
    *   Waterproof 100% of below-grade wall area.
    *   Seal all existing cracks, holes, and penetrations.
    *   Ensure proper drainage (e.g., air gap drainage membrane, drainage board, weeping tile).
    *   Install exterior drainage membranes.
    *   Add insulation to the exterior side of the wall when waterproofing.
    *   Do-it-yourself waterproofing is acceptable with evidence (pictures, invoices).
    *   Building permit may be required.

| Option                       | Grant Amount |
| :--------------------------- | :----------- |
| Basement wall waterproofing  | $875         |

#### Moisture Proofing Crawl Space Floor, Walls and Headers

*   **Eligibility:**
    *   100% of floors, walls, and headers of all crawl spaces must be covered by a continuous moisture barrier (e.g., minimum 6 mil polyethylene barrier on floor, closed-cell foam on walls/headers).
    *   All seams, edges, and penetrations must be sealed.
*   **Best Practices:**
    *   Address all water infiltration and insect/vermin issues first.
    *   If attached to a basement, the crawl spaces moisture barrier must be sealed to the basement foundation junction.
    *   Continuous moisture barrier should be 0.75 perms or less.
    *   Encapsulation can be performed by homeowner or contractor.
    *   Verify with a heating professional if a combustion appliance is in the crawl space.
    *   Building permit may be required.

| Option                                              | Grant Amount |
| :-------------------------------------------------- | :----------- |
| Moisture proofing of 100% of crawl space floor, walls and headers | $600         |

### Multi-unit Residential Buildings (MURBs)

A MURB contains two or more units. To be eligible for the Canada Greener Homes Initiative, it must meet specific requirements:

*   Three or fewer storeys above ground.
*   Building area does not exceed 600 m.
*   Between 2 and 100 units.
*   Units are fully or partially stacked or joined by a common space (side-by-side for secondary suites).
*   At least 50% of total floor area must be residential living space.
*   Habitable year-round.

_High-rise condos are outside the scope of the program. Townhomes and row houses are considered individual houses, not MURBs. Retirement homes are not eligible._

**MURBs are eligible for grants for:**

*   Insulation
*   Air sealing
*   Windows and doors
*   Renewable energy
*   Resiliency measures

**MURBs (excluding two-unit MURBs and houses with secondary suites) are NOT eligible for grants related to:**

*   Heat pumps and heat pump water heaters
*   Thermostats
*   Furnaces and boilers (in northern and off-grid communities)
