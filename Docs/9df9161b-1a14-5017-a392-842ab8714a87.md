# Electric and gas ranges (cooking products)

*November 2024*

## Technical bulletin on amending the standards

In **April 2024**, the Office of Energy Efficiency at Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) updated its [Forward Regulatory Plan](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/node/21709#msdynttrid=gPrvBKD4QnHLNydHNDucp-nQokQOZTaBRjIp6V7eiWk), signaling its intention to amend Canadas *Energy Efficiency Regulations*. The aim is to **harmonize the scope, energy efficiency, and testing standards for electric and gas ranges with those of the United States for conventional cooking products**.

NRCan is considering:
*   Consolidating energy-using product names for **electric** and **gas ranges** under **cooking products**.
*   Removing current energy efficiency standards for **electric coil cooktops** and for **electric oven on-mode** to align with the United States.
*   Adopting a new prescriptive standard for **ovens** to harmonize with the United States, expecting negligible energy implications.
*   Updating the energy efficiency standard for **gas cooktops** to introduce an annual energy consumption requirement, replacing the current prescriptive requirement of no standing pilot.

This technical bulletin initiates the consultation process. Input will inform future amendments. NRCan will also conduct a cost-benefit analysis.

The Government of Canada plans to pre-publish a regulatory proposal in the *Canada Gazette*, Part I, in **2025**.

## Background

*   Household electric and gas ranges manufactured from **February 3, 1995**, are subject to the Regulations. Induction cooking products are currently unregulated.
*   Energy efficiency standards for cooking products reduce energy consumption, lower costs, and decrease energy-related greenhouse gas emissions.
*   In **2021**, NRCan and the U.S. Department of Energy (U.S. DOE) signed a [Memorandum of Understanding](https://nrcan.canada.ca/energy/resources/international-energy-cooperation/memorandum-understanding/23749) to collaborate on energy efficiency standards.
*   On **February 14, 2024**, the U.S. DOE published a [Direct Final Rule](https://www.regulations.gov/document/EERE-2014-BT-STD-0005-12825) strengthening standards for gas, induction, and electric resistance cooktops (excluding electric coil cooktops). Compliance is required on or after **January 31, 2028**.
*   On **August 8, 2022**, the U.S. DOE published a [Final Rule](https://www.regulations.gov/document/EERE-2021-BT-TP-0023-0024) for cooking product testing standards, with [corrections](https://www.regulations.gov/document/EERE-2021-BT-TP-0023-0026) published on **February 7, 2023**. Mandatory use in the U.S. since **February 20, 2023**.
*   NRCan aims to harmonize with U.S. DOE standards, scope, testing standards, and compliance dates.
*   The Government of Canada is committed to improving energy efficiency standards to support [net-zero emissions by 2050](https://www.canada.ca/en/services/environment/weather/climatechange/climate-plan/net-zero-emissions-2050.html).

## Product description

Currently, **electric ranges** and **gas ranges** are defined separately. NRCan intends to consolidate these under a new term:

*   **Cooking product**: means a cooktop, an oven, or a combined cooking product.
*   **Cooktop**: a household cooking appliance with a horizontal surface containing one or more surface units (gas flame, electric resistance, or inductive heating). Includes cooktop components of combined products.
*   **Oven**: a household cooking appliance with one or more compartments for cooking/heating food via gas flame or electric resistance heating. Excludes microwave ovens and portable/countertop electric resistance ovens designed for 120 V.
*   **Combined cooking product**: a household cooking appliance combining a cooking product with other functionalities.

## Testing standard

NRCan is considering incorporating by reference an NRCan technical standards document that would reproduce the following U.S. testing standard:
*   **Appendix I1 to Subpart B, Part 430 of Title 10** to the U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, entitled *Uniform Test Method for Measuring the Energy Consumption of Conventional Cooking Products*.

## Energy efficiency standards

The proposed energy efficiency standards, harmonized with subparagraphs [430.32(j)(1)(iii)](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-10/part-430/section-430.32#p-430.32(j)(1)(iii)) and [430.32(j)(2)(ii)](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-10/part-430/section-430.32#p-430.32(j)(2)(ii)) of Subpart C, Part 430 of Title 10 to the United States *Code of Federal Regulations*, include:

*   **Electric smooth element cooktops (resistance and induction)**: Maximum Integrated Annual Energy Consumption (IAEC) of **207 kWh/year**.
*   **Gas cooktops**: Maximum IAEC of **519 kWh/year or 1770 kBtu/year**.
*   **Oven control systems**: Shall not include a linear power supply.

*IAEC excludes any downdraft venting system energy consumption.

## Important dates

*   Changes to the Regulations would come into force **six months** after publication in the *Canada Gazette*, Part II.
*   NRCan is considering applying updated standards to cooking products manufactured on or after **January 31, 2028**, to align with the U.S. compliance date.
*   Voluntary compliance with considered changes would be permitted upon publication in the *Canada Gazette*, Part II.

## Regulatory pathway

NRCan is considering amending the Regulations through Governor in Council amendment, with pre-publication in the *Canada Gazette*, Part I, followed by a public comment period.

## Labelling requirements

*   An **EnerGuide label** is currently required on electric ranges.
*   As the U.S. does not require labelling for cooking products, NRCan is considering **phasing out the current labelling requirement**.
*   If the U.S. Federal Trade Commission introduces an EnergyGuide label for cooking products, NRCan would reconsider an EnerGuide label for these products.

## Verification requirements

No change is being considered regarding verification requirements.

## Reporting requirements

### Energy efficiency report

The energy efficiency report for cooking products would require the following information:

*   **Product type**: Cooktop, oven(s), or combined cooking product.
*   **Combined product components**: Cooktop, oven, and/or microwave oven (if applicable).
*   **Fuel(s) used**: Electricity, gas, or both.
*   **Cooktop surface technology**: Resistive coil, resistive smooth, or induction (if cooktop or combined with cooktop).
*   **Oven control system**: Whether it includes a linear power supply (if oven or combined with oven).
*   **IAEC**: In kWh/year (for electric cooktops/ovens) or kBtu/year (for gas cooktops), if applicable.

This report must be submitted to NRCan by the dealer **before** the product is imported into Canada or traded inter-provincially for the first time.

### Import report

No change is being considered regarding import reporting.

## Comments invited

NRCan invites feedback on the following specific implications:

*   Removing energy efficiency standards for **electric coil cooktops**.
*   Removing on-mode energy efficiency standards for **electric ovens**.
*   Removing the **EnerGuide label** for ranges (electric cooktops and ovens).
