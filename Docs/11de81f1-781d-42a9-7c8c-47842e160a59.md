# NetZero: future building standards

*Building Science. Exceptional Energy Performance. Government of Canada Certification. Industry Leadership.*

## Overview

Launched in **2013**, the R-2000 Net Zero Energy Pilot aimed to recognize builders and homes achieving net zero energy performance in Canada. The pilot also allowed rating net zero energy houses using Natural Resources Canada's (NRCan) EnerGuide Rating System (ERS) and labeling them under the R-2000 Standard.

By **Fall 2016**, **twenty-three net zero energy homes** were built by **six builders** in **three different provinces**.

A **net zero energy home** is so energy efficient, it only uses as much energy as it can produce from on-site renewable energy.

## Objectives

### 1. Build on national programs

*   **R-2000** is an established national standard providing training, certification, house-testing, inspections, and overall quality assurance essential for net zero energy housing.
*   Minimum envelope requirements of the **Draft 2014 R-2000 Standard** were applied, focusing on the building envelope before renewable energy technologies.
*   All houses were certified and labeled with a **zero gigajoule (0 GJ) rating** under NRCans **ERS Version 15**.
*   Using R-2000 and ERS ensures continuity and comparability with over one million Canadian homes already labeled.

### 2. Focus on energy performance and commoditization of net zero energy homes

*   Aimed at advancing the commoditization of net zero energy homes.
*   Focused on homes buildable today using market-available technologies meeting industry standards and regulations.
*   Technologies were limited to pre-engineered products and systems to ensure accurate modeling and long-term performance in Canada's climate.

### 3. Disseminate NRCan research

*   NRCans Office of Energy Efficiency led the Pilot in collaboration with **CanmetENERGY**, NRCans research arm.
*   Canmet demonstrated that an R-2000 home can achieve net zero energy performance using off-the-shelf technologies and methods.
*   Provided net zero energy cost optimization models as guidelines for Pilot participants.

The Pilot included two types of participants: (i) those invited through an open request for proposals (RFP) and (ii) those participating under the Net Zero Energy Housing Community Demonstration Project funded through the ecoENERGY Innovation Initiative (ecoEII).

### ecoEII: Net Zero Energy Housing Community Demonstration Project

*   **$1.9 million** in funds were provided to **Owens Corning Canada** by NRCans Innovation and Energy Technology Sector.
*   Objective: increase market adoption of net zero energy homes by demonstrating feasibility on a community scale.
*   **Five homebuilders** in **four provinces** built a total of **25 net zero energy and net zero energy ready homes**.
*   These builders used the same standards, methodologies, and tools as the RFP participants.
*   More information on the ecoEII net zero energy demonstration project: [https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy/funding/current-funding-programs/eii/16140](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy/funding/current-funding-programs/eii/16140)

## R-2000 NZE Pilot Results

*   A total of **23 net zero energy houses** were built under the Pilot by **six builders** (two from the RFP and four under the ecoEII project):
    *   Construction Voyer
    *   Habitat Studio & Workshop Ltd.
    *   Mattamy Homes
    *   Minto Communities Canada
    *   Reids Heritage Homes
    *   Sloot Construction Ltd.
*   All these builders became new (or were re-licensed as) **R-2000 builders**.
*   The Pilot facilitated building a range of archetypes, including **detached (single family) dwellings, four townhomes, and a six-plex multi-unit residential building**.
*   Some projects were not approved due to missing the final labeling deadline, using ineligible technologies, or not installing the required PV system. These provided valuable insights and lessons learned.
*   NRCan acknowledges the contribution of all builders, energy advisors, and industry partners.
