# Energy Affordability Program FAQs

*Find answers to the most asked Energy Affordability Program questions.*

## General

### **What is the Energy Affordability Program?**
The **Save on Energy Energy Affordability Program (EAP)** is an electricity conservation program offered by the Independent Electricity System Operator (IESO). It helps income-eligible electricity customers reduce energy costs and increase home comfort by providing no-cost energy-efficient upgrades.

**Two levels of support are available:**
-   **Comprehensive Support**: For those most in need, includes a free in-home energy assessment by an energy expert to identify upgrades like replacement appliances and weather stripping.
-   **Energy Saving Kit**: For moderate income-level participants, includes items for self-installation such as energy-saving LED lighting, timers, and a clothes drying line.

### **Is everything really offered free of charge?**
Yes! The Energy Affordability Program provides all energy-saving upgrades completely free of charge, overseen by the IESO.

### **Who can participate in the Energy Affordability Program?**
Eligibility is for Ontario residents responsible for paying their electricity bill (homeowners or renters), including residents of social housing. Eligibility is based on **household income** and **number of occupants**, or receipt of a **qualifying eligible benefit**. *Residents served by Cornwall Electric are not eligible as they are not connected to the IESO-controlled grid.* More details at [SaveonEnergy.ca/EAP](https://www.saveonenergy.ca/For-Your-Home/Energy-Affordability-Program).

### **How do I know if the Energy Affordability Program is right for me?**
This program is beneficial if you are income-eligible or have received a qualifying assistance benefit in the past 12 months, and have inefficient products (e.g., light bulbs, appliances) in your home. For direct financial assistance with electricity bills, visit the Ontario Energy Board at [www.oeb.ca/rates-and-your-bill/help-low-income-consumers](https://www.oeb.ca/consumer-information-and-protection/bill-assistance-programs).

### **I rent my home. What information is shared with my landlord?**
If the assessment identifies landlord-owned appliances or home insulation for upgrade, your landlord will be offered the upgrade. The EAP **does not share personal financial information** about your qualification.

### **Are your delivery agents and contactors authorized?**
Yes, delivery agents are authorized to assist participants. They include:
-   **Postal codes starting with K**: EnviroCentre
-   **Postal codes starting with L, M or P**: CLEAResult
-   **Postal codes starting with N**: The Home Inspectors Group

### **Where can I get more information?**
Call **1-844-770-3148** or visit [SaveonEnergy.ca/EAP](https://www.saveonenergy.ca/For-Your-Home/Energy-Affordability-Program).

### **Is the Home Assistance Program still available?**
The Home Assistance Program has been replaced by the new **Save on Energy Energy Affordability Program (EAP)**. The EAP offers similar free in-home energy assessments and energy-saving products with no-cost installation, with expanded eligibility and an option for moderate-income participants to receive an Energy Saving Kit.

### **Im in need of urgent electricity-bill support. Who can I contact?**
Visit the [OEB website](https://www.oeb.ca/rates-and-your-bill/help-low-income-consumers) for information on electricity-bill support programs and eligibility. Income-eligible EAP applicants will be contacted for assessments, or waitlisted if regional restrictions apply.

### **I am currently participating in the Energy Affordability Program and am waiting on delivery of my appliance upgrades and/or installation of weatherization products  when will these activities be completed?**
Program participants will be contacted in the coming weeks to schedule installations, as public health measures permit. Some product deliveries may be affected by supply chain delays.

## Comprehensive Support FAQs

### **What happens during my in-home energy assessment?**
An energy expert will:
-   Verify program eligibility documents.
-   Install qualifying measures like **ENERGY STAR certified LED lightbulbs, efficient showerheads and faucet aerators, and a clothes drying rack**.
-   Provide information on reducing electricity use.
-   Assess the age and efficiency of appliances, insulation, and draft levels.
-   Take photographs of appliances and home interior/exterior.
-   *A second home visit may be scheduled for additional energy-saving equipment installation if you qualify.*

### **What happens after my in-home energy assessment?**
The EAP delivery partner will review assessment information to determine if your home qualifies for **new, ENERGY STAR certified appliances** or, for electrically heated homes, **increased insulation (attic, walls, basement)** and **comprehensive draft proofing**. You will be notified of results and can choose to accept or decline offered upgrades.

### **What upgrades can I receive as a result of my in-home energy assessment?**
Free upgrades include:
-   **ENERGY STAR certified light bulbs (LEDs)**
-   **Energy-efficient shower heads and faucet aerators**
-   **Clothes drying rack**
-   **Power bar with a timer**

Based on assessment, you may also qualify for:
-   **ENERGY STAR certified replacement appliances**: fridge, freezer, window air conditioner, and/or dehumidifier.
-   *For electrically heated homes*: **Insulation** (walls, attic, basement) and **comprehensive home draft-proofing**.

### **What information do you collect about my home?**
With your consent, the EAP collects information on your home's **efficiency, age, heat-fuel type (electricity, natural gas), appliances, heating system equipment, electronics, and lighting**. This helps determine appropriate energy-efficient upgrades. You may also be referred to other no-cost energy-affordability programs.

### **Will the Energy Affordability Program contact me after my assessment?**
Yes, you will receive communications, including a customer survey, from the IESO and the program delivery partner.

### **What happens to my old appliances?**
If you qualify for new appliances, your old, inefficient appliance will be removed and responsibly disposed of. Harmful substances are handled safely, and other materials are recycled.

## Energy Saving Kits FAQs

### **How will the Energy Affordability Program delivery partner verify that I am eligible to receive an Energy Saving Kit for self installation?**
You may need to provide proof of eligibility (e.g., Notice of Assessment or proof of qualifying support benefit). This documentation will be securely destroyed after verification.

### **What will be included in my Energy Saving Kit?**
Your kit may include free energy-saving measures for self-installation such as:
-   **Energy-saving light bulbs**
-   **Efficient shower heads**
-   **Faucet aerators**
-   **Clothes drying line**
-   **Weather-stripping**

### **How is the Energy Saving Kit customized for me and my home?**
The kit is customized based on the **number of inefficient light bulbs** and your home's **primary heating fuel source** (especially if electric) and **hot water source**. You may also receive a **block heater timer** if applicable.

### **What happens after I receive my Energy Saving Kit?**
The EAP delivery partner will follow up by phone or email to verify that the energy-saving measures have been installed and are working. The IESO may also invite you to participate in a survey.

### **What if I have trouble installing the items in the Energy Saving Kit?**
-   If your kit was received in **2021**: Contact GreenSaver at **1-888-855-3106**.
-   If your kit was received in **2022**: Contact Ecofitt at **1-888-341-9778**.
