# Step 2A. Pre-retrofit EnerGuide evaluation

## Canada Greener Homes Grant Status
**Closed:** The Canada Greener Homes Grant closed on **December 31, 2025**. This was the last day to submit documents. Requests submitted by the deadline will continue to be processed.

## Purpose of Pre-retrofit EnerGuide Evaluation
Pre-retrofit EnerGuide evaluations provide expert advice on what retrofits make the most sense for a home and how to improve its energy performance. For example, it explains how to conserve more renewable energy if you were considering installing solar panels.

*It was recommended that homeowners ensure their energy advisor evaluate the potential energy efficiency improvements listed on the eligible retrofits page.*

## 1. Schedule Your Pre-retrofit EnerGuide Evaluation
Homeowners selected a **service organization** registered with Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) to conduct the pre-retrofit EnerGuide evaluation. A representative from this organization would contact the homeowner to schedule the evaluation.

* **What is a service organization?** Service organizations are companies that deliver EnerGuide evaluations and work with energy advisors registered with NRCan.

### Cost of Your EnerGuide Evaluation
*   Homeowners paid upfront for both pre- and post-retrofit EnerGuide evaluations.
*   Prices varied across Canada depending on factors like location and home size.
*   Homeowners could receive up to **$600** as a maximum contribution toward the total cost of their pre- and post-retrofit EnerGuide evaluations.
*   In some cases, the maximum grant amount did not cover the full cost.
*   **Recommendation:** Program participants are advised to keep their documents and receipts until **March 31, 2028**.
*   *Note: Homeowners were required to complete at least one eligible retrofit and the full grant process to receive the contribution towards the evaluation fees.*

### If You Did Not Hear From Your Service Organization
Due to high demand, response times from NRCan and service organizations could vary. Homeowners were advised to follow up with their service organization if they had not been contacted within a few weeks.

## 2. Review the Results of the Pre-retrofit EnerGuide Evaluation
Upon completion of the pre-retrofit EnerGuide evaluation, the energy advisor provided:
*   An **EnerGuide home rating and a label**
*   A **Homeowner Information Sheet** (describing assessed building components, equipment, and energy usage)
*   A **Renovation Upgrade Report** (with recommended retrofits for improving energy performance)

This information helped homeowners decide which retrofits to undertake.

*   **Delivery Time:** Energy advisors were required to send these reports (by mail or email) within a minimum of **14 days** after the evaluation.
*   **Portal Access:** Service organizations were required to submit reports to the online Canada Greener Homes portal. Access to reports via the portal could take a minimum of **30 days** after the evaluation.
*   **Further Information:** Learn how to interpret the results of your home evaluation at: [After your EnerGuide home evaluation](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/product-energy-ratings/energuide/after-your-energuide-home-evaluation).
