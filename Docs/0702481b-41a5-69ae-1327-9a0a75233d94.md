# Inspection Guidance for Regulated Parties - Compliance Operations, Office of Energy Efficiency

## 1. Purpose
This guidance document aims to **inform stakeholders about Natural Resources Canadas (NRCan) inspection strategy** for energy-using products regulated under the *Energy Efficiency Act* (Act) and *Energy Efficiency Regulations* (Regulations). It outlines the **inspection process** used to assess compliance.

## 2. Scope
This guidance applies to **dealers of regulated energy-using products**. A dealer is defined as a person engaged in:
- **Manufacturing** energy-using products in Canada
- **Importing** energy-using products into Canada
- **Selling or leasing** energy-using products obtained directly or indirectly from a manufacturer or importer.

Locations with regulated products owned by or on the premises of a dealer or consignee may be inspected.

## 3. Background
The *Energy Efficiency Act* and *Regulations* are administered by **Natural Resources Canada (NRCan)**. The Regulations apply to energy-using products that must meet federal energy efficiency standards, testing, marking, and labelling requirements.

NRCan's Compliance Operations conduct regulatory activities, including inspections, under the authority of **section 10 of the Act**, with inspectors designated under **section 9**.

## 4. Compliance and Enforcement Activities
NRCan's approach to compliance and enforcement is detailed in the *Compliance and enforcement policy*. Activities include **promoting and monitoring compliance** and undertaking **enforcement actions**.

### 4.1 Inspections
The primary objective of inspections is to **verify compliance** with the Act and Regulations. This includes verification of:
- **Energy efficiency verification mark requirements**
- **Required labels** (if applicable)
- **Energy efficiency standards**

Inspections can be part of scheduled monitoring or in response to information received by Compliance Operations.

#### 4.1.1 Responsibilities of the Regulated Party
Dealers are expected to:
- **Understand** the Act and Regulations and their obligations.
- **Ensure products comply** with requirements.
- **Keep records** about energy-using products for **six years**.
- **Provide models** of a product for examination and testing.

During an inspection, owners or persons in charge must provide **all reasonable assistance** and necessary information to NRCan inspectors. Preventing an inspection or providing false information is a violation of **section 12(2) of the Act**.

#### 4.1.2 Site Selection
Compliance Operations uses a **risk-based approach** considering criteria such as:
- **Presence and quantity** of regulated products.
- Products identified as **high risk** or for **routine inspection**.
- **Compliance and inspection history**.
- **Import data** and **energy efficiency report data**.
- **Tips and complaints**.

Inspections can be proactive or reactive, with frequency potentially changing based on non-compliance information.

#### 4.1.3 Inspection Activity
Inspections of various regulated product categories are conducted annually across Canada, sometimes including follow-up inspections. They serve to **verify compliance** and offer opportunities for **compliance promotion and education**.

An inspection site is any place where an inspector reasonably believes there is a regulated energy-using product or related documents/records.

#### 4.1.4 Before an Inspection
Inspectors may provide **advance notice** for planned inspections to request documents or address access concerns. They may also conduct inspections without advance notice if it better assesses compliance.

An inspection is typically **two to four (2-4) hours long** and conducted by a team of **two designated inspectors**.

#### 4.1.5 During an Inspection
Inspectors will:
- Show their **certification of designation**.
- State their identity and the **purpose of the inspection**.
- Explain their **authority** and may provide guidance documents.
- Inform the person in charge of their **obligation to provide assistance**.
- **Examine products and records**, and may **take photos**.
- Request **import records**, **product quantity information**, and **proof of energy efficiency standard compliance**.

Designated inspectors have the authority to:
- **Enter** any place with regulated energy-using products.
- **Examine products**, open packages, and examine documents.
- **Make copies** of records.
- **Conduct tests** or **take measurements**.
- **Use computer systems** and request printouts.

#### 4.1.6 Completion of the Inspection
Inspectors will notify the regulated party when the inspection is concluded and invite questions. A **follow-up communication and report** will present the results and any required **corrective actions**. Corrective actions must be completed by the given deadline to avoid further compliance actions.

All records obtained are handled according to NRCan policies.

#### 4.1.7 Review of Inspection Results
Regulated parties who disagree with inspection results should first **contact the lead inspector**. If unresolved, the **Manager of Compliance Operations** may be contacted.

### 4.2 Enforcement
NRCan emphasizes **self-monitoring, reporting, and collaboration**. However, enforcement measures may be used for violations. The choice of enforcement depends on:
- **Nature of the violation** (circumstances, gravity, tolerance levels).
- **Culpability** (intent, compliance history, motive, concealment attempts).
- **Effectiveness** of the measure in achieving compliance.
- **Consistency** in application.

*Refer to NRCans Compliance and Enforcement Policy for more information about enforcement.*

## Glossary
- **Compliance**: The state of conformity of a regulated party or product with a legislative or regulatory requirement.
- **Dealer**: Defined in the *Energy Efficiency Act* as a person engaged in manufacturing, importing, or selling/leasing energy-using products.
- **Inspection**: Entering and inspecting a place to verify compliance with the *Energy Efficiency Act* and Regulations, including examining products, records, conducting tests, and making copies.
- **Inspection site**: A place where an inspector reasonably believes there is a regulated energy-using product or relevant documents/records owned by or on the premises of a dealer or consignee.
- **Inspector**: A person designated under section 9(1) of the *Energy Efficiency Act*.
