# Ceiling fans

An **ENERGY STAR certified ceiling fan uses 60% less energy**, on average, than a standard model. Ceiling fans that have earned the [ENERGY STAR Most Efficient](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/energy-star/products/energy-star-most-efficient) designation are the best of the best  theyll save you more energy than standard models. Saving energy saves money and reduces your carbon footprint.

## Key features

*   Use improved motors and blade designs.
*   Feature the ability to reverse the motor and airflow direction. Operate your ceiling fan counterclockwise in the summer and clockwise in the winter to keep you comfortable.
*   Component(s) are warrantied for at least three years.
*   Some ENERGY STAR certified ceiling fans offer connected functionality, so you can control it with your smartphone or voice assistant!

## Helpful tips

*   Switch it off when you leave! Fans dont actually cool a roomthey cool the occupants by circulating air. Turn fans and lights off on your way out and reap the savings.
*   Reduce your energy bills by setting your thermostat higher and using ceiling fans to circulate cool air.

## The ENERGY STAR Promise

All ENERGY STAR certified products are tested to meet strict [efficiency specifications](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/energy-star/technical-specifications) and are certified by an independent third party. They perform the same as or better than standard products without compromising performance in any way.

Start your research [using the ENERGY STAR Product Finder to find and compare](https://www.energystar.gov/productfinder/product/certified-ceiling-fans/results?SetLanguage=English&NRCAN=on) certified ceiling fans (_You will be redirected to the U.S. ENERGY STAR website_).

## Regulations set the energy efficiency minimum

Ceiling fans are subject to Canada's Energy Efficiency Regulations, which set a minimum performance standard for their energy efficiency. Find details in the [Guide to the Regulations](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/industry-energy-efficiency/energy-efficiency-regulations-standards-industry/guide-canada-s-energy-efficiency-regulations).
