# Eligible Property Types for Energy Efficiency Programs

**Note:** The Canada Greener Homes Grant closed on **December 31, 2025**. Requests submitted by this deadline will continue to be processed.

## General Eligibility Requirements
Regardless of property type, the building must have met the following requirements to be eligible for program funding:

*   **Storeys:** It has **three or fewer storeys** above ground.
*   **Building Area:** The building area does not exceed **600 m**.
*   **Habitability:** The building must be **habitable year-round**.

### General Ineligibility
*   **New homes** (6 months or newer based on the date of occupancy by the first homeowner) and **new additions** to existing homes are **not eligible** for the Canada Greener Homes Grant or the Oil to Heat Pump Affordability program.

*In the following definitions, a house contains only one dwelling unit, while a multi-unit residential building (MURB) contains two or more units (dwelling unit or non-residential unit).*

## Eligible Property Types

### Single and Semi-detached Houses
*   **Single detached:** A dwelling unit with walls and roofs independent of any other building. If two houses are connected only through a garage or an unheated space, both houses are considered to be detached.
*   **Semi-detached:** Part of a set of two dwelling units separated from each other by one vertical division (a party wall). Each dwelling unit is identified as semi-detached and evaluated separately.

### Townhomes and Row Housing
*   **Definition:** Each townhome or row house shares one or more walls with adjacent properties but has its own entrance. A townhome or row house may be connected to adjacent properties on one side (end unit), both sides (middle unit), or back-to-back.

### Mobile Homes (on a permanent foundation)
*   **Definition:** A movable dwelling unit designed and constructed to be transported by road on its own chassis to a site and placed on a permanent foundation such as blocks, posts, or a pad.

### Permanently Moored Floating Homes
*   **Definition:** A structure floating in water that incorporates a flotation system that maintains the building envelope above the waterline (typically greater than 400 mm [15.75 in.]) under normal load conditions. The floating home is not primarily intended for, or usable in, navigation and does not include a watercraft designed or intended for navigation.

### Low-rise Multi-unit Residential Buildings (MURBs)
*   **Definition:** A building with **three or fewer storeys** above ground that has a footprint of **600 m or less** and contains between **2 and 100 units**. The units must be either fully or partially stacked (up/down) or joined by a common space if not stacked. Each unit must have a private entrance, either from the outside of the building or from a common space.
    *Examples:* A low-rise apartment building, triplex, duplex, or house with a secondary suite is a MURB.
*   **Ineligible for Oil to Heat Pump Affordability program:** Low-rise MURBs with **more than 2 units** are not eligible.

### Mixed-use Buildings (residential portion only)
*   **Definition:** A building that has a mix of residential and non-residential occupancies (e.g., a house or MURB that contains one or more dwelling units as well as commercial or retail space).
    *   At least **50% of the total floor area** must be residential living space (areas used for dwelling units and common spaces).
    *   The combined total floor area of the non-residential spaces must be **less than that of the residential spaces** and must **not exceed 300 m**, excluding parking garages that serve residential occupancies.
*   **Ineligible for Oil to Heat Pump Affordability program:** Mixed-use buildings with **more than 2 units** (1 residential and 1 commercial) are not eligible. (Only the residential portion may be eligible for an electric heat pump system under this program.)

## Footnotes

*   **Footnote 1:** The Canada Greener Homes Initiative is based on the EnerGuide Rating System, which is limited to houses and low-rise MURBs governed by Part 9 of the National Building Code of Canada. High-rise condos are considered complex structures outside the scope of both the EnerGuide Rating System and the Canada Greener Homes Initiative. Homeowners of non-eligible property types may still be eligible for provincial and territorial renovation programs.
*   **Footnote 2:** A dwelling unit is a building or part of a building operated as a housekeeping unit; is used or intended to be used by one or more people; and usually contains cooking, eating, living, sleeping, and sanitary facilities.
*   **Footnote 3:** MURBs higher than three storeys above ground or with a footprint of over 600 m or containing more than 100 units are not eligible for the Canada Greener Homes Initiative.
*   **Footnote 4:** In a house with a secondary suite, the units may be located side-by-side, fully or partially stacked, or joined by a common space.
