# Pool pump motors

*May 2024*

## Technical Bulletin Overview

This technical bulletin from the Office of Energy Efficiency at Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) announces the intention to amend Canadas *Energy Efficiency Regulations*. The amendments will introduce **pool pump motors** as a new energy-using product, including energy efficiency and testing standards harmonized with the United States.

This bulletin serves to initiate the consultation process and gather stakeholder feedback on the proposed requirements. NRCan will conduct a cost-benefit analysis using Canadian market data to assess economic and environmental impacts. A regulatory proposal is expected to be pre-published in the *Canada Gazette*, Part I, in 2025.

## Background

Currently, pool pump motors are not defined as energy-using products in the Regulations. Implementing energy efficiency standards for these motors is expected to: 
- **Save energy** and **reduce energy costs** for Canadians.
- **Reduce energy-related greenhouse gas emissions**.
- Encourage manufacturers to adopt **efficient product designs**.

Canada is harmonizing with the U.S. Department of Energy (U.S. DOE) regulations, following a Memorandum of Understanding signed in 2021. The U.S. DOE published a final rule for a test method in July 2021 and energy efficiency standards in September 2023. U.S. manufacturers must comply with these new requirements starting in 2025 or 2027, depending on motor size.

## Product Description

A **pool pump motor** is defined as an appliance designed specifically for driving a swimming pool pump. This includes motors for:
- Self-priming pool filter pumps
- Non-self-priming pool filter pumps
- Waterfall pumps
- Pressure cleaner booster pumps
- Integral sand-filter pool pumps
- Integral-cartridge filter pool pumps
- Storable electric spa pumps
- Rigid electric spa pumps

A **variable speed motor** is a pool pump motor that meets the definition of variable-speed control dedicated purpose pool pump motor as defined by UL 1004-10:2022.

### Testing Standard

NRCan is considering incorporating by reference one of the following testing standards:
- **CSA C747-09** *Energy efficiency test methods for small motors* (currently incorporated by the U.S. Code of Federal Regulations).
- **CSA C747-22** *Energy efficiency test methods for small motors*, if incorporated into the U.S. Code of Federal Regulations before the next amendment to the Regulations.

### Energy Efficiency Standard

The proposed standards align with section 431.485 of Subpart Z to Part 431, Title 10 of the U.S. Code of Federal Regulations:
- A pool pump motor with a total horsepower of less than **0.5 hp (0.373 kW)** must have a full-load efficiency of not less than **69 percent**.
- A pool pump motor with a total horsepower greater than or equal to **0.5 hp (0.373 kW)** must be a **variable speed control motor**.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: Modifications to the Regulations would come into force **six months** after publication in the *Canada Gazette*, Part II.
- **Manufacturing Dates for New Standards**:
    - **September 29, 2025**, for motors with **THP <0.5**, or **1.15  THP  5**.
    - **September 28, 2027**, for motors with **0.5  THP <1.15**.

### Regulatory Pathway

NRCan plans to amend the Regulations through a Governor in Council amendment. Proposed changes will be pre-published in the *Canada Gazette*, Part I, for public comment. Final publication in the *Canada Gazette*, Part II, is subject to comments received and typically occurs within a year of the initial public comment period. All changes are subject to decision-making authorities under the *Energy Efficiency Act*.

## Labelling Requirements

NRCan is **not considering labelling requirements** for pool pump motors.

## Verification Requirements

Pool pump motors would require a **third-party verification mark** indicating compliance with energy efficiency standards and verification of reported information. This mark must be from a Standards Council of Canada accredited certification body that operates an energy efficiency certification program for the product.

## Reporting Requirements

### Energy Efficiency Report

This report must be submitted by the dealer to NRCan **before** the product is imported into Canada or traded inter-provincially for the first time. It would include:
- **Name of product**: Pool pump motor
- **Brand name**
- **Model number**
- **Name of the manufacturer**
- **Pool pump nominal motor power**, in kW (hp)
- **Pool pump motor service factor**
- **Total horsepower** of the pool pump motor, in kW (hp)
- **Efficiency**, in percent (if required)
- **Name of the certification body** whose verification mark is affixed
- Indication of whether a **mathematical model** was used to generate product-specific information

### Import Report

A dealer importing these products into Canada would include the following on the customs release document:
- **Name of product**
- **Model number**
- **Brand name**
- **Address of the dealer** importing the product
- **Purpose for import**: (e.g., for sale/lease in Canada without modification; for sale/lease after modification; for use as a component in an exported product)

## Comments Invited

NRCan invites comments on the proposed requirements for pool pump motors, specifically seeking feedback on:
- **Pool pump motor products manufactured within Canada**.
- **Canada-specific sales or shipment data** for pool pump motors and by motor size.

Comments are welcome by **September 13, 2024**.
