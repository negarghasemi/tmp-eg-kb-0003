# Central Air Conditioners

## Energy Efficiency Overview
An **ENERGY STAR certified** central air conditioner (A/C or central air) **uses 8% less energy**, on average, than a standard model. Models with the **ENERGY STAR Most Efficient** designation offer even greater savings.

Replacing an air conditioner that is over 10 years old can lead to **upwards of 30% energy savings**. Saving energy not only saves money but also reduces your carbon footprint.

## Types of Central Air Conditioning Systems

There are two main types of central air conditioning systems:

1.  **Split systems:**
    *   Consist of an outdoor cabinet (compressor/condenser) and an indoor cabinet (expander/evaporator).
    *   Most economical to install if your home already has a furnace.
2.  **Packaged central air conditioning system:**
    *   All components are in one cabinet, typically placed on a roof or a slab next to the house.
    *   Often include electric heating coils or a natural gas furnace, eliminating the need for a separate indoor furnace.

To find an ENERGY STAR certified product, you can:

*   Ask your **retailer, installer, or service delivery agent**.
*   Use the **advanced version of ENERGY STAR Product Finder** to access the ENERGY STAR Certified Non-AHRI (Air-Conditioning, Heating and Refrigeration Institute) Central Air Conditioner directory. *(Note: This redirects to an English-only U.S. ENERGY STAR website.)*
*   Use the **Consortium for Energy Efficiency / Air-Conditioning, Heating and Refrigeration Institute directory** (English-only). Make sure to select either Split or Packaged under product type and Yes under labeled ENERGY STAR.

*Heat pumps with a reverse-cycle feature can also be used for air conditioning and space heating.*

## Helpful Tips for Energy Savings

*   **Sizing:** Bigger is not always better. An oversized air conditioner is less effective and wastes energy. Ensure your unit is **properly sized by a certified air conditioning contractor**.
*   **Efficiency Ratings:** Look for the **Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio (SEER)** and the **Energy Efficiency Ratio (EER)**. Higher numbers indicate better efficiency.
*   **Temperature Setting:** Aim for comfort, not chill. The recommended temperature is between **25 and 27C**.
*   **Maintenance:** Regularly **clean filters, coils, and ductwork**.
*   **Professional Service:** Get your central air serviced **every year by a qualified HVAC contractor** to maintain optimal performance.
*   **Resource:** Consult the booklet, *[Air Conditioning Your Home](http://www.nrcan.gc.ca/energy/publications/efficiency/residential/air-conditioning/6051)*.

## Installation Tips

*   **Professional Installation:** Hire a qualified air conditioning contractor to assess cooling requirements and determine the right unit size based on factors like home size, insulation, existing ductwork, and window orientation.
*   **Location:** Ensure adequate air circulation. Place the outdoor unit away from shrubbery and direct sunlight, and in a location where noise will be least disruptive.
*   **Leveling:** In new construction, consider installing the unit on a frame mounted to the home to ensure it remains level as the foundation settles.
*   **Refrigerant Lines:** Keep them as short as possible. Seal gaps around lines passing through exterior walls with resilient material to prevent noise, vibration, and air leaks.
*   **Coil Matching:** When replacing a unit, **always match the indoor coil to the new outdoor unit**. Failing to do so will prevent the new unit from operating at its rated efficiency.

## Energy Labels

Central air conditioners in Canada may feature two labels:

*   **ENERGY STAR symbol:** Indicates the model is highly energy efficient.
*   **EnerGuide label:** Shows the models energy consumption and how it compares to similar models. [Learn more about EnerGuide labels](/energy-efficiency/product-energy-ratings/energuide/central-air-conditioners-energuide).

## The ENERGY STAR Promise

All ENERGY STAR certified products are tested to meet strict efficiency specifications and are certified by an independent third party. They offer equivalent or superior performance compared to standard products without compromise.

## Energy Efficiency Regulations

Central air, room, and large commercial air conditioners are subject to Canada's *Energy Efficiency Regulations*, which establish minimum performance standards. Details can be found in the [Guide to the Regulations](/energy-efficiency/energy-efficiency-regulations/guide-canada-s-energy-efficiency-regulations).
