# Home Energy Retrofits

Home energy retrofits are designed to provide residents with the most effective energy-saving installations and upgrades that immediately deliver greater comfort and savings on utility bills while reducing GHG emissions. CCETs Energy Coaches offer a **FREE** call (up to 45 minutes) to guide you through the process.

## Benefits of Home Energy Retrofits

-   **Greater comfort** in both heating and cooling seasons
-   **Lower utility bills**
-   **Reduction of harmful GHG emissions**
-   **Increased home value**

## What to Expect from a Home Energy Retrofit

A visit from an *NrCan (Natural Resources Canada) Registered Energy Advisor* will identify the most impactful retrofits for comfort, energy, and cost savings.

### Common Retrofit Suggestions:

-   **Insulation**: Installing insulation in attics, basement walls, and crawl spaces for immediate warmth in winter and comfort in summer.
-   **Blower Door Test**: Uses a machine to measure and locate air leakage in homes, creating negative air pressure to identify problem areas.
-   **Weather Stripping**: Simple installation around doors, windows, and ductwork to reduce drafts and save energy.
-   **Air Source Heat Pump (ASHP)**: Can save up to $1,000 per year in heating and cooling costs compared to conventional gas furnaces and standard AC units (*according to Ontario Clean Air Alliance*).
-   **LED Lighting**: Switching to LED bulbs can save an average household about $225 in energy costs per year. Lighting accounts for approximately 15% of total electric costs.
-   **New Windows**: Installing Energy Star rated windows can save as much as 45% on energy bills, though it's an expensive investment best considered during construction.
-   **Water Conservation**: Canadians use significantly more water than many European countries. Installing low-flow toilets can save up to $200 per year. Low-flow showerheads and sink aerators also contribute to substantial water savings.

## Deep Retrofits: Get to Net Zero

**What is Net Zero?**

Net zero means a home produces as much energy as it consumes, ideally from clean sources like solar and wind. These homes are typically up to 80% more energy-efficient than conventionally built homes and integrate energy efficiency, conservation, efficient water management, a substantial thermal building envelope, and healthy indoor air quality.

**Why is it Important?**

Achieving a Net Zero Home leads to significantly lower utility bills year-round, a reduced carbon footprint, and contributes to Canada's GHG emission goals.

### Key Components of Deep Retrofits:

-   **Building Envelope**: Ensure the home has high-quality, high R-value insulation and a tight building envelope. This is fundamental for the entire system's efficiency.
-   **Conservation**: Utilize high-efficiency HVAC systems (like Air Source Heat Pumps) and appliances. Orient windows for passive heating to maximize sun warmth and light.
-   **Renewables**: Incorporate clean, renewable energy sources. A \"Net Zero Ready\" house is designed for easy installation of renewables.

## Financial Help & Programs

### Free or Low-Cost Income-Qualified Programs

-   **Federal Oil to Heat Pump Affordability Program**
    -   **Payment**: Up to $10,000 for eligible Canadian homeowners.
    -   **Eligibility**: Home heated by oil; household income at or below median after-tax income.
    -   **Eligible Measures**: Electrical/mechanical upgrades for heat pump, ground source heat pump outdoor piping, safe oil tank removal, backup electrical heating (if required), switching other oil-using systems (e.g., water heater).
-   **100% Free Cold Climate Air Source Heat Pumps (Save on Energy  Energy Affordability Program)**
    -   **Eligibility**: Qualifying Ontario households in electrically heated homes.
    -   **Benefits**: Two-year free maintenance warranty; 10-year equipment and labor warranty.
    -   **Other Free Upgrades (if eligible)**: Professionally installed ENERGY STAR appliances (refrigerators, freezers, window ACs, lighting), weatherstripping, attic/basement insulation, smart thermostats, power strips.
|-   **Home Winterproofing Program (HWP) | Enbridge Gas**
    -   **Description**: Streamlined with the Energy Affordability Program for free home energy upgrades.
    -   **Eligibility**: If home and household income levels qualify.
    -   **Benefits**: Free insulation, draft proofing, smart thermostat, Energy Star appliances, and more.
-   **Hydro One Low Income Energy Assistance Program (LEAP)**
    -   **Payment**: Up to $1,000 (up to $1,200 if electrically heated) on your bill.
-   **Save on Energy  Energy Affordability Program (IESO)**
    -   **Description**: Expert advice and free energy-saving home upgrades for income-eligible households.
    -   **Eligibility**: Responsible for electricity bill (homeowner or renter); depends on household income and size, or receipt of qualifying government/energy-support benefit. Social housing residents may also be eligible.
    -   **Types of Support**: Comprehensive upgrades or Energy Saving Kits.
-   **Ontario Electricity Support Program (OESP)**
    -   **Description**: Ontario Energy Board program lowering electricity bills for lower-income households.
    -   **Benefits**: Monthly credit applied directly to bills, amount based on household income and size.

### Rebates & Incentives

-   **Hydro One - Ontario Electricity Rebate (OER)**
    -   **Reduction**: 13.1% reduction on bill amount before HST (as of November 1, 2024).
    -   **Automatic Eligibility**: Residential and Small Commercial customers using less than 50 kW; Commercial accounts >50 kW but <250,000 kWh annually.
-   **Enbridge Fixed Incentive Program**: Offsets cost of upgrading to energy-efficient natural gas technology; Energy Solutions Advisor helps identify savings and provides incentives.
-   **Enbridge Business Programs and Incentives**: Rebates and incentives for equipment upgrades, new construction, retrofits, and energy assessments.
-   **Home Renovation Savings Program (Enbridge Gas & Save on Energy)**
    -   **Rebates (require Home Energy Assessment, for 2+ upgrades)**:
        -   Heat Pump Water Heaters: $500
        -   Windows and Doors: $100 per rough opening
        -   Insulation (attic, wall, foundation, exposed floor): Up to $8,900
        -   Air Sealing: Up to $250
    -   **Rebates (do not require home energy assessment, for 1+ upgrades)**:
        -   Heat Pumps: Up to $12,000
        -   ASHP: Up to $7,500 (must be on Natural Resources Canada's qualified list)
        -   Solar Panels and Battery Storage: Up to $10,000
        -   Smart Thermostats: $75
    -   **ASHP Eligibility**: Enbridge Gas customer with active account and primarily heats with natural gas furnace/boiler OR connected to Ontario electricity grid and heats electrically.

### Loans & Financing

-   **Canada Greener Homes Loan**: Up to $40,000 in interest-free loans for terms up to 10 years. Details at Natural Resources Canada site.
-   **Clean Energy Loan (RBC Energy Saver Loan)**: For RBC customers purchasing hybrid cars, ENERGY STAR appliances, renewable energy projects. Offers 1% interest rate reduction or $100 rebate on a clean energy audit.
-   **Mortgage Insurance Loan Rebate**: Up to 25% rebate for certified green homes (R-2000, ENERGY STAR, Built Green, LEED) from CMHC, Sagen, and Canada Guaranty.
-   **Canada Green Affordable Housing Program**: Funds up to 100% of eligible retrofit costs (max $170,000 per unit) for homeowners meeting affordability criteria. Forgivable loans up to $85,000 per unit or 80% of costs.
-   **Steps to Consider**: Show bankers your research, seek other loan/grant sources.

## Handy at Home? Quick Fix DIY Tips!

Many quick fixes can be done today at little or no cost, providing immediate comfort and cost savings.

### DIY Energy-Saving Tips

-   **Cold Air**: Electrical outlets on outside walls can be sealed with inexpensive foam insulators. Weather stripping is an easy way to stop drafts.
-   **Air Filters & Maintenance**:
    -   Replace furnace filters regularly per manufacturer's recommendations.
    -   Bleed hot water radiators at least once or twice a year.
    -   Oil furnaces need annual maintenance; natural gas and propane furnaces every two years.
    -   Keep heat flowing by not blocking vents and air grills.
-   **Curtains**: Use heavy fabric curtains to reduce cold, drafty air from windows.
-   **Light Bulbs**: Switch to LED light bulbs; they last up to 100,000 hours (14 years) and offer significant energy savings.
-   **Water Heater**: Insulate older water heaters in cold areas or basements with a water heater blanket (inexpensive, saves money).
