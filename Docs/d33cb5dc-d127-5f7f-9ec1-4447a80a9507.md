# EnerGuide Home Evaluation and Energy Efficiency Programs

## What to Expect from an EnerGuide Home Visit & Audit

Follow these steps for a successful EnerGuide Audit. More detailed information can be found on the Natural Resources Canada page.

### EnerGuide Auditor

The evaluation is conducted by a **certified and registered energy advisor** selected by you.

### Prepare Your Home

*   Remove ashes from wood-burning appliances.
*   Ensure clear access to the furnace, water heater, and other appliances.

### Consent Form and Scope of Work

*   Your Energy Advisor will review terms and scope of work.
*   They will collect photos of the exterior, mechanical systems, attic, and insulation. *All photos and data are strictly protected within privacy legislation.*

### Energy Efficiency Priorities

*   Prepare a list of desired energy efficiency upgrades and prioritize the retrofits you'd like to install.

### The Home Evaluation

*   The Energy Advisor collects data, typically starting with the exterior (including thermal imaging) and moving indoors.
*   This data is fed into energy performance software to calculate your home's **EnerGuide rating**.
*   **Data collected may include:**
    *   Air tightness, insulation levels, and types of windows and doors.
    *   Heating system sizes and ratings, water heaters, ventilation (if any), and air conditioning.
    *   Confirmation and locations of all heating and cooling units.
    *   *All wood-fired and fuel-fired heating must be turned off prior to the evaluation and will be returned to pre-test settings afterward.*

### Important Information

*   Have your **property tax information** readily available.
*   Understand the terms of any rebates and carefully follow guidelines if applying for the **Greener Homes Loan**.
*   **No retrofit work can be performed prior to the energy audit** and only after the initial report with recommended upgrades is complete.

## What is EnerGuide?

**EnerGuide** is the official mark of the Government of Canada for its energy performance rating and labeling program, covering houses, light-duty vehicles, and certain energy-using products.

### EnerGuide Labeling (Mandatory for Appliances)

EnerGuide labeling indicates energy efficiency levels and is mandatory for the following appliances:

*   Clothes dryers
*   Clothes washers (including integrated washer-dryers)
*   Dishwashers
*   Freezers
*   Electric ranges, cooktops, and ovens
*   Miscellaneous refrigeration products
*   Refrigerators and refrigerator-freezers
*   Room air conditioners

### The EnerGuide Home Evaluation Report

This report provides **recommendations and tips to improve your home's energy efficiency**. After the home inspection, the Energy Advisor will issue an **EnerGuide Renovation Upgrade Report**, detailing suggested upgrades, retrofits, and additional efficiency tips.

### Report May Include Recommendations For:

*   Increasing the home building envelope with upgraded insulation in attics and basements.
*   Weatherization stripping to reduce drafts around windows and doors.
*   Upgrading heating and cooling systems, such as **Air Source Heat Pumps** for year-round energy savings.
*   Replacing all lights with **LED bulbs**.
*   Replacing aging appliances with **EnerGuide-rated or Energy Star Label** products.
*   Implementing simple behavioral changes like drying clothes on a line or using heavier curtains on windows.

## Canada Supports Energy Efficiency

### Free or Low-Cost Income-Qualified Programs

*   **Federal Oil to Heat Pump Affordability Program**
    *   **Benefit**: Up to **$10,000** for switching from oil heating to an energy-efficient heat pump.
    *   **Eligibility**: Home heated by oil; household income at or below median after-tax income.
    *   **Eligible Measures**: Electrical/mechanical upgrades for heat pump, outdoor piping for ground source heat pump, oil tank removal, backup electrical heating, switching other oil-using systems (e.g., water heater).
*   **100% Free Cold Climate Air Source Heat Pumps (Save on Energy  Energy Affordability Program)**
    *   **Eligibility**: Qualifying Ontario households with electrically heated homes.
    *   **Benefits**: Two-year free maintenance warranty, 10-year equipment and labor warranty. May also qualify for other free upgrades (ENERGY STAR appliances, weatherstripping, attic/basement insulation, smart thermostats, power strips).
|*   **Home Winterproofing Program (HWP) | Enbridge Gas**
    *   Streamlined with Energy Affordability Program.
    *   **Eligibility**: Qualifying home and household income levels.
    *   **Benefits**: Free insulation, draft proofing, smart thermostat, Energy Star appliances, and more.
    *   **Note**: CCET is a community distribution partner with CLEAResult; contact for a free Energy Saving Kit.
*   **Hydro One Low Income Energy Assistance Program (LEAP)**
    *   **Benefit**: Pays up to **$1,000** (up to **$1,200** for electrically heated homes) on your bill.
*   **Ontario Electricity Support Program (OESP)**
    *   Lowers electricity bills for lower-income households with a monthly credit based on income and household size.

### Rebates & Incentives (Home Renovations Savings Program)

*   **Hydro One - Ontario Electricity Rebate (OER)**
    *   **Benefit**: 13.1% reduction on the amount of your electricity bill (before HST), as of November 1, 2024.
    *   **Automatic Eligibility**: Residential & Small Commercial customers (under 50 kW); Commercial accounts (over 50 kW but under 250,000 kWh annually).
*   **Enbridge Fixed Incentive Program**
    *   Offsets the cost of upgrading to energy-efficient natural gas technology. An Energy Solutions Advisor helps identify opportunities, quantify savings, and provides incentives.
*   **Enbridge Business Programs and Incentives**
    *   Offers rebates and incentives for equipment upgrades, new construction, retrofits, and energy assessments for businesses.
*   **Home Renovation Savings Program (Enbridge Gas and Save on Energy)**
    *   **Benefit**: Up to **$5,000** in rebates for home energy efficiency improvements.
    *   **Rebates requiring a Home Energy Assessment**:
        *   **Heat Pump Water Heaters**: **$500**
        *   **Windows and Doors**: **$100** per rough opening
        *   **Insulation** (attic, wall, foundation, exposed floor): Up to **$8,900**
        *   **Air Sealing**: Up to **$250**
    *   **Rebates not requiring a Home Energy Assessment**:
        *   **Heat Pumps**: Up to **$12,000**
        *   **ASHP (Air Source Heat Pump)**: Up to **$7,500**
            *   *Eligibility for ASHP*: Must be an Enbridge Gas customer primarily heating with natural gas or connected to the Ontario electricity grid and heating electrically; installed heat pump must be on Natural Resources Canadas qualified products list.
        *   **Solar Panels and Battery Storage**: Up to **$10,000**
        *   **Smart Thermostats**: **$75**

### Loans & Financing

*   **Canada Greener Homes Loan (CMHC)**
    *   **Benefit**: Up to **$40,000** in interest-free loans for terms up to 10 years for eligible retrofits.
*   **Clean Energy Loan (RBC Energy Saver Loan)**
    *   Available to RBC customers for purchasing hybrid cars, ENERGY STAR appliances, and renewable energy projects.
    *   Offers a **1% interest rate reduction** or a **$100 rebate** on a clean energy audit with the loan.
*   **Mortgage Insurance Loan Rebate (CMHC, Sagen, Canada Guaranty)**
    *   **Benefit**: 25% rebate on mortgage loan insurance when buying a certified green home (R-2000, ENERGY STAR, Built Green, LEED, etc.).
*   **Canada Green Affordable Housing Program (CMHC)**
    *   **Eligibility**: Homeowners meeting affordability criteria.
    *   **Benefit**: Funding up to 100% of eligible retrofit costs (maximum **$170,000** per unit); forgivable loans up to **$85,000** per unit or 80% of eligible costs.
    *   **Process**: Requires application for funding and pre-retrofit activities (energy audits, energy modeling).

## DIY Energy Saving Tips

*   **Water Heater**:
    *   Insulate older water heaters located in cold areas (like basements) with inexpensive blankets (**$30-$40**) for significant cost savings.
*   **Curtains**:
    *   Use heavy fabric curtains to reduce cold, drafty air entering through windows.
*   **Light Bulbs**:
    *   Switch to **LED light bulbs**; they last up to 100,000 hours (14 years) and offer significant energy savings (hundreds of dollars per year).
*   **Cold Air Drafts**:
    *   Seal electrical outlets on outside walls with inexpensive foam insulators.
    *   Apply weather stripping around doors and windows to prevent air leakage.
*   **Heating System Maintenance**:
    *   Regularly replace furnace filters as per manufacturer's recommendations.
    *   Bleed hot water radiators at least once or twice a year for efficient operation.
    *   Ensure annual maintenance for oil furnaces and biennial servicing for natural gas and propane furnaces.
    *   Keep vents and air grills clear of obstructions.
