# Make Small Changes That Add Up
_Save energy and money by making modest investments and minor changes around your home._

## Opportunities for Improvement

### Kitchen
- **Microwave**: Most energy-efficient for reheating food.
- **Dishwasher**:
    - Buy an **ENERGY STAR certified dishwasher**: uses **12% less energy**.
    - Scrape dishes, dont rinse.
    - Use the air dry feature.
    - Assess each load for efficient cleaning.
- **Range hood**: Install an **ENERGY STAR certified range hood** to use **up to 70% less energy** than a standard one.
- **Fridge**:
    - Shop for an **ENERGY STAR certified fridge**: uses **9% less energy**.
    - Allow air to flow; dont overfill.
    - Keep away from heat sources (vent, oven, sunlight, etc.).
    - For kitchen faucets, install an aerator with a flow rate less than **7.0 L/min**.

### Bathroom
- **Shower head**: Install a water-saving shower head (flow rate less than **7.0 L/min**) to use less hot water. Take showers instead of baths.
- **Toilet**: Use a low-flow toilet that uses **4.8 litres or less per flush** to save water.
- **Ventilation fans**: Switch to **ENERGY STAR certified ventilation fans** to use **70% less energy** than standard models, and install a timer.
- **Faucet aerator**: Choose an aerator that delivers less than **4.7 L/min** of water.

## Living Room, Dining Room and Office

### Living Room
- **TV**: Look for an **ENERGY STAR certified TV** to use **25% less energy**.
- **Cordless phone**: Find the **ENERGY STAR symbol** (40% savings).
- **Audio and video products**: Find the **ENERGY STAR symbol** (up to **70% savings**).
- **Recessed lighting fixtures (pot lights)**: Buy **ENERGY STAR certified recessed lighting fixtures** to use **up to 90% less energy** than traditional models with incandescent bulbs.
- **Smart power strip**: Use to turn off the power to devices not in use.
- **LED bulbs**: Choose **ENERGY STAR certified LED bulbs**; they last **15 times longer** than incandescent ones.

### Dining Room
- **Light fixtures**: Choose **ENERGY STAR certified light fixtures**.
- **Windows**: Use awnings, blinds or drapes to keep direct sunshine from overheating the room in the summer.
- **Window upgrades**: Read *Keeping the Heat In* to learn about options for upgrading your windows.
- **Lighting and Heating**: Use compatible dimmer switches with your **ENERGY STAR certified bulbs** to reduce light use. If your baseboards have individual thermostats, set each thermostat to the desired temperature (higher in summer, lower in winter) to save.

### Office
- **Computer**: Enable low power settings on your computer to save energy and help your battery last longer.
- **Smart power strip**: Use to reduce energy waste when equipment is on standby or off.
- **Light fixtures**: Buy **ENERGY STAR certified light fixtures** to use **up to 90% less energy** than traditional models with incandescent bulbs.
- **Air leakage**: Seal areas of air leakage around all window and door casings, mouldings, and baseboards to stop cool air from escaping in summer and warm air from escaping in winter. Read *Keeping the Heat In* to learn how.
- **Office equipment**: Purchase **ENERGY STAR certified office equipment** to use **25% to 40% less energy**.

## Bedrooms

### Primary Bedroom
- **Windows**: Install triple-glazed windows with a high insulation rating, especially in walls that receive little direct sunlight, to minimize unwanted heat transfer. Choose **ENERGY STAR Most Efficient windows** (up to **55% more energy-efficient**).
- **Computers/TVs**: Use **ENERGY STAR certified computers or TVs** to stream a movie and get **25% more energy savings**.
- **Ceiling fans**: Purchase **ENERGY STAR certified ceiling fans** to use **60% less energy**. Set to run counterclockwise in summer.
- **Radiators**: Empty (bleed) the air from radiators once or twice a year for efficiency.

### Children's Room
- **Electrical outlets**: Install foam insulation gaskets behind electrical outlet faceplates on exterior walls. Read *Keeping the Heat In*.
- **Light bulbs**: Switch to **ENERGY STAR certified light bulbs** and save **up to 90% more energy** than incandescent ones.
- **Air flow**: Keep air flowing by not obstructing vents or return air grills with furniture.
- **Computer monitors**: Use **ENERGY STAR certified computer monitors** to save **25% more energy** than standard models.
- **Air sealing**: Seal cracks and openings around penetrations (vents, pipes, etc.) in the walls, ceilings or floors to prevent air from escaping.

## Laundry Room and Hallway

### Laundry Room
- **Washer**:
    - Buy an **ENERGY STAR certified washer**: save **25% more energy**, use **33% less water** than a standard model.
    - Choose high-speed or extended spin to reduce drying time.
    - Fill but not too much: washers operate most efficiently when full.
    - Wash in warm or cold water to save energy needed to heat water.
    - Pre-soak stained clothes to avoid rewashing.
- **Dryer**:
    - Shop for an **ENERGY STAR certified dryer**: save **20% more energy** compared to other models.
    - Clean the lint trap after every load to increase air flow.
    - Dont dry too much; use the sensor setting or time it just right.
    - Dont fill too much to ensure airflow and reduce strain on the motor.
    - Sort by thickness (light items in one load, thick items in another).
- **Light fixtures**: Pick **ENERGY STAR certified light fixtures** to use **up to 90% less energy**.
- **Motion sensor**: Install to reduce your light use.
- **Air drying**: Try air drying clothes such as delicates and silks.

### Hallway
- **Exterior door**: Choose a more energy-efficient door (up to **25%**). An **ENERGY STAR exterior door** has better insulation.
- **Smart thermostat**: Install an **ENERGY STAR certified smart thermostat** to take control of your cooling.
- **Weatherstripping**: Apply around older exterior doors to prevent cool air from escaping in summer and warm air from escaping in winter.
- **Lighting**: Turn off lights when they are not in use.

## Mechanical Room
_**Important**: Consult the owners manual about the maintenance of all heating, cooling and ventilation equipment._

- **Hot water tank**:
    - Insulate hot- and cold-water pipes by covering the first two metres with foam sleeves or insulating wrap with a minimum of **RSI 0.7 (R-4)** insulation. Leave a 15-cm (6-inch) clearance between insulation and vent pipes on fuel-fired water heaters.
    - Buy an **ENERGY STAR certified gas-fired tankless water heater**: uses **30% less energy**.
    - Install a drain-water heat recovery unit and recover enough heat to save **15 to 20%** of the energy required for hot water.
- **Boiler**: Insulate the hot-water pipes to save on heating costs.
- **Heat recovery ventilator (HRV) or energy recovery ventilator (ERV)**:
    - Clean the filters every 3 months.
    - Clean the core annually.

- **Furnace**:
    - Choose an **ENERGY STAR certified furnace** (efficiency rating of at least **95% for gas**, **85% for oil**).
    - Look for an **ENERGY STAR Most Efficient gas furnace**.
    - Change or clean filters every 3 months or as they become dirty.
    - Clean and service regularly (oil-fired: annually, natural gas/propane: every two years).
    - Consider replacing your furnace with an electric heat pump once it reaches end of life (**2 to 4 times more energy-efficient**).
- **Ducts**:
    - Seal all supply duct work with aluminum foil duct tape, approved flexible plastic tape or water-based mastic.
    - Make sure heating, cooling and ventilation ducts running through basements, crawl spaces or unheated attics are sealed and insulated.
    - Ensure your heat, cooling and ventilation distribution system is balanced.

## Outdoors

### Front Yard
- **Doors**: Weatherstrip doors.
- **Landscaping/Awnings**: Use awnings or strategically positioned deciduous trees to block summer sun and allow winter sun.
- **Windows**: Seal the joints of window casings with flexible caulking.
- **Outdoor lighting**: Buy **ENERGY STAR certified outdoor light fixtures or bulbs**.
- **Insulation/Air sealing**: Upgrade the insulation and air seal your home.

### Backyard
_**Important**: Consult the owners manual for maintenance and details of all heating, cooling and ventilation equipment._

- **Air source heat pump (ASHP)**: Look into an **ENERGY STAR certified ASHP** (energy-efficient for heating and cooling). Keep clear of bushes or debris.
- **Solar energy system**: Install a safe, reliable, low-maintenance source of electricity.
- **HRV/ERV**: Inspect and clean outside hoods and screens.

### Garage
- **Lighting**: Install a motion sensor.
- **Transportation**: Walk, bike or take public transit to save costs. Drive fuel-efficiently.
- **Light fixtures/bulbs**: Look for **ENERGY STAR fixtures or bulbs**.
- **Vehicle**: Buy the most fuel-efficient vehicle that meets your needs.
