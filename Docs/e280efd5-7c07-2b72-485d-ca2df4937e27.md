# Heat pump basics

An electric heat pump is a heating and cooling system that is fully reversible. It can both heat and cool your home.

Electric heat pumps use less energy to heat and cool your home compared to traditional equipment, making them an excellent choice for new homes and retrofits.

## How does a heat pump work?

During cooler months, a heat pump extracts heat from the outside air, uses electricity to increase its temperature, and transfers it inside your home. Advanced technology allows it to extract heat even during cold Canadian winters. For cooling, it transfers heat from warm indoor air to the outside, similar to how an air conditioner works.

Heat pumps are energy-efficient because they *transfer* heat rather than *generate* it.

## Benefits of using an electric heat pump in your home

-   **Lower energy use**: Heat pumps consume less energy to heat a home.
-   **Financial incentives**: Federal, provincial, territorial, and municipal incentives are available to help with upfront costs.
-   **Both heating and cooling**: Provide efficient heating in winter and cooling in summer, crucial for rising temperatures and heat waves.
-   **Environmental**: Unlike traditional fossil-fuel systems, heat pumps produce no greenhouse gas emissions when heating the home.

## Types of heat pumps

### Air source heat pumps (ASHP)

These are the most common type in Canada and use **air as their heat source**.

-   **Compared to oil**:
    -   Heats space up to **2.5 times more efficiently** than an oil furnace or boiler.
    -   Reduces emissions by up to **10 tonnes of COe per year**.
-   **Compared to electrical resistance**:
    -   Heats space up to **2 times more efficiently** than electric furnaces or baseboards.
-   **Compared to gas**:
    -   Heats space up to **2.5 times more efficiently** than a gas furnace.
    -   Reduces emissions by up to **6 tonnes of COe per year**.

### Cold climate air source heat pumps (ccASHP)

A type of ASHP **better adapted to colder Canadian climates**, capable of operating in temperatures **down to -30C**.

-   **Compared to oil**:
    -   Heats space up to **3 times more efficiently** than an oil furnace or boiler.
    -   Reduces emissions by up to **10 tonnes of COe per year**.
-   **Compared to electrical resistance**:
    -   Heats space up to **2.5 times more efficiently** than electric furnaces or baseboards.
-   **Compared to gas**:
    -   Heats space up to **3.5 times more efficiently** than a gas furnace.
    -   Reduces emissions by up to **6 tonnes of COe per year**.

### Ground source heat pumps (GSHP)

These use the **earth and/or ground water as their heat source**. GSHPs can provide more consistent heat in winter because they are less affected by outdoor air temperature fluctuations.

-   **Compared to oil**:
    -   Heats space up to **3.5 times more efficiently** than an oil furnace or boiler.
    -   Reduces emissions by up to **10 tonnes of COe per year**.
-   **Compared to electrical resistance**:
    -   Heats space up to **3.5 times more efficiently** than electric furnaces or baseboards.
-   **Compared to gas**:
    -   Heats space up to **3.5 times more efficiently** than a gas furnace.
    -   Reduces emissions by up to **6 tonnes of COe per year**.

*Note: These efficiencies refer to average performance over the winter and account for temperature variations. Exact performance depends on location and previous heating system type.*

*Consider gas-heat pump hybrid systems if you have access to natural gas.*

## Which heat pump is right for you?

-   **Ducted**: Provides heating and cooling to the entire home via ductwork (similar to a central gas furnace).
-   **Ductless**:
    -   **Mini-split**: Provides heating and cooling to localized areas, typically a single room.
    -   **Multi-split**: Provides heat to the entire home using multiple indoor units supplied by a single outdoor unit.

## When to install a heat pump

It's recommended to install a heat pump proactively, rather than waiting for an emergency replacement. Electrical upgrades and other renovations for optimal performance can take up to a year.

## Working with contractors

### Use a skilled contractor

A skilled contractor can **design, install, and service** the equipment to meet your home's requirements and comply with local codes. Improper sizing or installation can lead to financial consequences and reduced efficiency.

### Questions to ask your contractor

-   What type of heat pump is best for my home?
-   How will you ensure the heat pump is properly sized for my home?
-   What maintenance schedule should be followed?
-   What is the equipment warranty? Is there a warranty certificate?

## Available grants and rebates

The Canada Greener Homes Initiative offers incentives for energy efficiency retrofits, including heat pumps. Check for additional rebates from your province, territory, municipality, or utility provider.
