# Heat Pumps

In Canada, more than half of the energy produced is lost. Reducing these energy losses through more efficient use is the most immediate and least costly means of achieving the Kyoto objectives. A recent report from the International Energy Agency indicates that **45% of the Kyoto objectives can be achieved through more intelligent use of energy**.

## Towards Better Energy Management Using Heat Pumps

Since **20% of the secondary energy used in Canada is for space and water heating in buildings**, this sector is an ideal target for energy reduction. Technologies that provide better management of energy for heating, cooling, and refrigeration will lead to significant energy reduction.

Achieving these objectives requires the use and management of multiple energy sources and efficient storage systems. **Heat pump (HP) systems** are identified as the most promising technology for heating and cooling buildings. With a projected **10% penetration rate by 2020**, heat pumps are expected to allow a reduction in:

*   **Energy consumption**: **120 PJ** annually
*   **Greenhouse gas emissions**: **5 Mt of CO equivalent** annually

## From Research to Demonstration

CanmetENERGY conducts research and development on heat pump systems to address technical, financial, and social limitations to their adoption. Their research activities focus on the following areas:

*   **Ground-source heat pumps**
*   **Ejectors**
*   **Cold Storage using Ice Slurry**
*   **Thermal heat pumps**

*This research is managed by **CanmetENERGY at the Varennes (Quebec) research centre**.*
