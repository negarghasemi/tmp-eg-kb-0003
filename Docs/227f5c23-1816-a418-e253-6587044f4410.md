# R-2000: Environmentally Friendly Homes

## Overview
R-2000 is a **voluntary housing standard** developed by Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) in collaboration with the energy efficiency sector. It sets a benchmark for homes designed and built to **high standards of energy efficiency**, focusing on environmental protection, clean air, and enhanced comfort.

## Energy Efficiency Rating
*   **R-Value**: A measure of how well insulation keeps in or keeps out heat.
*   **R-2000 Standard (2012 Update)**: Homes built to this standard are, on average, **50% more energy-efficient** than typical new homes built to code.

## Key Construction Features
In a typical R-2000 home, you will find:
*   **High insulation levels** in walls, ceilings, and basements.
*   **High-efficiency windows and doors**.
*   **High-efficiency heating and cooling systems**.
*   A **balanced, whole-house mechanical ventilation system**.
*   **Minimal air leaking** from the house.
*   **Water-conserving fixtures** such as taps and showerheads.

Many R-2000 home builders also use **less toxic and environmentally friendly building materials** (e.g., certified green carpet, water-based paints, air filtration systems).

## Certification Process
Every R-2000 home is:
*   Constructed by **licensed and specially trained builders**.
*   **Evaluated, inspected, and tested** by an independent third-party energy advisor.
*   **Certified by the Government of Canada**.

## R-2000 Certificate and Label
Every R-2000 homeowner receives a certificate and a label (to be placed on the electrical panel).

**R-2000 Certificate Information** typically includes:
*   Municipal address
*   Postal code
*   Lot number or legal description
*   Plan number or legal description
*   Certificate No.
*   Date and signature

**R-2000 Label Text**:
\"This home meets the R-2000 Standard. For further details, please refer to the R-2000 Home Certificate or contact your local R-2000 office or Natural Resources Canadas Office of Energy Efficiency at 1 800 387-2000 (toll-free). Please consult your R-2000 Homeowners Manual and ensure that you are familiar with how to operate and maintain your R-2000 home.\"

## Benefits of an R-2000 Home

### Energy Savings
*   **Lower heating and cooling costs** due to high insulation and an airtight building envelope.
*   Actual cost savings depend on local fuel costs, number of occupants, and lifestyle.

### Health Benefits
*   **Improved indoor air quality** through a balanced, whole-house mechanical ventilation system that removes stale air and delivers fresh air.
*   Reduced exposure to toxins by using eco-friendly building materials.
*   **Controlled indoor moisture levels** to prevent mould growth.
*   High-efficiency sealed-combustion heating systems prevent dangerous combustion products from entering the home.

### Enhanced Comfort
*   **Consistent indoor temperatures** and elimination of drafts due to airtightness, high-quality windows, and well-insulated walls.
*   Designed to **take advantage of solar energy** during winter, providing more light without increasing heating costs.

### Environmental Benefits
*   **Lower greenhouse gas emissions** by reducing the need to burn fossil fuels (average new home releases 5 tonnes of CO2 annually).
*   **Promotes a market for recycled products** as many R-2000 homes use materials made from recycled content (e.g., drywall, wood, steel studs, insulation, roofing, concrete, carpeting).
*   **Reduced water use** through water-saving fixtures, leading to less water purification and sewage treatment needs.

## R-2000 vs. ENERGY STAR Certified Homes
*   **ENERGY STAR certified new homes**: On average, 20% more energy efficient than homes built to code.
*   **R-2000 certified homes**: Considered \"best-in-class\" energy-efficient homes, offering **higher levels of energy efficiency** than ENERGY STAR certified new homes, along with additional clean air and environmental features.

## Cost Considerations
*   R-2000 homes typically have a **higher upfront cost** compared to standard homes.
*   This investment is offset by **significant savings on heating and cooling costs** over the long term, offering a return on investment.
