## About Electric Vehicles

### Vehicle Types
There are two main types of EVs on the market:

*   **Battery Electric Vehicle (BEV)**:
    *   Vehicles powered solely by a rechargeable battery pack that uses an electric motor to drive and must be plugged in to recharge the battery.
    *   BEVs have the largest battery capacities compared to other types of EVs.
    *   Produce zero tailpipe emissions.
    *   *Examples*: Tesla Model S, Nissan Leaf, Mitsubishi I-Miev, Smart Fortwo, and Ford Focus Electric.

*   **Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicle (PHEV)**:
    *   Vehicles that use both a rechargeable battery pack that powers an electric motor as well as an internal combustion engine.
    *   The internal combustion engine is used either in parallel with the electric motor or after the battery runs out of charge.
    *   *Examples*: Chevrolet Volt, Toyota Prius plug-in, BMW i3, and Ford C-Max Energi.

### Charging Equipment
Sometimes referred to as Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment (EVSE), this is the infrastructure that acts as the supply point to charge EVs.

*   **Level 1 (120V  15A)**:
    *   Typically included with the purchase of an EV.
    *   Can be connected to a normal 120V residential receptacle.
    *   Longest recharge time, typically **eight to 12 hours** depending on the EV's battery size.

*   **Level 2 (208 or 240V  30 to 100A)**:
    *   Requires a 208 or 240Vac supply with a higher current draw.
    *   Available as residential units and commercial charge stations.
    *   Most popular type of charging for public and residential locations.
    *   Typical charge time is in the range of **three to six hours**.

*   **Level 3 (DC Charge 208Vac input up to 120 kW DC output)**:
    *   A commercial charge unit.
    *   Can recharge a battery from **10% to 80% within 30 minutes** (time varies depending on the battery size and vehicle).

### Environmental Benefits
Road transportation in Canada is a large contributor to greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions. Since Ontarios electricity is mainly generated by hydroelectric and nuclear generation, the offset of carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases by driving electric is substantial. While lithium-ion battery production for EVs also produces GHG emissions, lifecycle analysis shows that battery materials only contribute a small percentage of emissions, and EVs show significant reductions in overall emissions when on the road compared with internal combustion engines.

### Financial Incentives
Although EVs may have a higher purchase price than most traditional vehicles, their use of low-cost electricity combined with higher fuel efficiency allows for great long-term financial savings.

*   **Electric motors** convert over **77%** of the chemical energy from batteries to power the wheels.
*   **Internal Combustion Engines** only convert about **12%30%** of the energy stored in gasoline.

*Check out the cost comparison calculator on the [CAA website](https://www.caa.ca/maintenance/car-cost-calculator.html#ev_new%EF%BF%BD?%20target=) to understand the savings of driving an EV.*

### Government Incentives
The Ministry of Transportation (MTO) promotes EVs with Ontario's Green Vehicle License Plate Program that allows the use of the HOV lane on major 400 series highways, even with one person in the vehicle.

*For more information, visit the Ministry of Transportation website [at this link](http://www.mto.gov.on.ca/english/vehicles/electric/green-licence-plate.shtml)*

### Installation Information

*   **Determine Charging Level**: Consider driving habits, EV battery size, and desired recharge time to choose the appropriate charging equipment.
*   **Wiring Requirements**: Check if the charging equipment requires a hardwired circuit or if it can be connected to a receptacle (e.g., some Level 2 equipment can use a 240V dryer receptacle).
*   **Electrical Panel Space**: If a hardwired circuit is required, ensure there is space on your homes electrical panel for an additional breaker. A panel upgrade may be needed if space is unavailable.
*   **Service Size Check**: If a panel upgrade is required, contact Oakville Hydro to confirm if your service size is rated for such an upgrade.
    *   *Email inquiries*: [engineering@oakvillehydro.com](mailto:engineering@oakvillehydro.com)
    *   *Phone*: 905-825-9400, extension 2266.
*   **Professional Installation**: Always hire a licensed electrical contractor to complete the wiring for any EV charging equipment. To find available contractors in your area, visit the [Electrical Safety Authority](http://findacontractor.esasafe.com/).
