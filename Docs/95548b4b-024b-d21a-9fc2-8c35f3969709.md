# TypesGround Source Heat Pump

There are three main types of ground-source heat pumps:

## Open Loop
*   This system uses a **lake, river, or another body of water** to exchange heat.
*   The unit **draws water directly to the heat exchanger**, where heat is extracted.
*   The system then **discharges the water** either to an above-ground body of water or back underground through a well.

## Closed Loop
*   Most closed-loop pumps circulate an **antifreeze solution** through a closed loop, typically made of plastic tubing.
*   This tubing is **buried in the ground or submerged in water**.
*   A heat exchanger transfers heat between the refrigerant in the heat pump and the antifreeze solution in the closed loop.
*   The loop can be configured in a **horizontal, vertical, or pond/lake arrangement**.

## Direct Exchange
*   In this system, a **refrigerant circulates** through a continuous loop of copper tubing placed directly in the ground.
*   It utilizes a **relatively shorter and smaller set of buried tubing**.
*   This design helps in **reducing installation costs**.
