# EnerGuide Energy Efficiency Home Evaluations

An energy-efficient home is **comfortable, healthy, environmentally friendly and cost-effective**. An EnerGuide home evaluation helps you understand your home's current energy consumption and identifies **retrofits to improve energy efficiency**.

An energy advisor will assess your home to provide an **EnerGuide rating** and an **energy efficiency report** with recommendations for upgrades.

## What to expect from your home evaluation

After booking, you may receive a pre-evaluation call or email from the service organization.

### Key Steps of the Evaluation:

-   The **energy advisor**, registered with Natural Resources Canada, will arrive at your home.
-   **Preparation**: Remove ashes from wood-burning appliances and ensure clear access to all areas (attic, crawl spaces, mechanical equipment). *(See more details in the \"Prepare your home\" section below)*
-   **Consent**: You will sign a \"Notice to Homeowner\" consent form.
-   **Review**: The advisor will explain the scope, intent, duration, and data collection process. All collected data and photos (exterior, mechanical systems, attic, wood-burning appliances) are protected by privacy legislation. One photo of the front of your house will be included in your report.
-   **Discussion**: You can discuss your plans and priorities for energy efficiency upgrades.
-   **Tools**: The advisor will explain the tools, including the **blower door testing equipment** used to determine your home's **airtightness**.
-   **Data Collection**: The evaluation typically starts outside and moves indoors. Data collected includes:
    -   **Airtightness level** (using a blower door test).
    -   **Insulation levels** of walls, ceilings, and basement.
    -   **Number, type, and location of windows and exterior doors**.
    -   **Size and efficiency ratings** of space heating, space cooling (air conditioning), and water heating equipment.
    -   Information about **ventilation equipment**.
    -   Any other relevant information about your homes energy performance.
-   **Access Confirmation**: The advisor will confirm locations of attic, basement/crawl space accesses, and mechanical equipment. Ensure safe and unobstructed access.
-   **Safety with Fuel-Fired Systems**: If you have fuel-fired space heating or water heating systems, they must be turned down or off during the blower door test to prevent safety issues. All equipment will be returned to pre-evaluation settings.
-   **Restrictions During Test**: Do not use fuel-fired or wood-fired appliances during the blower door test.
-   **Post-Evaluation Discussion**: The advisor may discuss program information and survey requests. Ask any questions you have.
-   **Required Items**: Prepare the following in advance:
    -   Your **municipal property tax roll number** (to confirm ownership).
    -   **Paid invoices and receipts** for energy efficiency upgrades (if participating in rebate/incentive programs requiring post-retrofit evidence).

## Prepare your home for the home evaluation

For a successful EnerGuide evaluation, your home must meet these conditions:

-   **Access**: Provide a clear path to all attic spaces, crawl spaces, mechanical equipment, and other hard-to-reach areas. Contact your service organization if unsure.
-   **Heating**: Your home must have a space heating system capable of maintaining **22 degrees Celsius** indoors.
-   **Windows and Doors**: All windows and doors must be in place. A maximum of one missing window or door is allowed if temporarily covered and air-sealed. Broken panes must be air-sealed (e.g., with taped polyethylene) for the blower door test. Ensure all are properly closed for accurate testing.
-   **Envelope**: The building envelope (ceilings, exterior walls, exposed floors, windows, doors) must be intact, including interior and exterior surfaces. If the exterior finish is not intact, the home cannot be evaluated. Renovations can only affect interior partitions and must not break the building envelope.
-   **Foundation**: Your home must be on its permanent foundation or be a permanently moored float home.
-   **Power**: Standard AC electrical power is needed for the blower door test equipment. If utility power is unavailable, arrange for an alternative supply and inform the service organization.
-   **Water and Sewer**: Potable water must be from the municipality, private wells, or cisterns. Sewer service must be from the municipality, a private septic system, or a holding tank (outhouses and bio-toilets are permitted).
-   **Operating Kitchen**: A kitchen space must include a sink, an operating stove (no portable devices), and at least **0.5 square metres (6 square feet)** of counter space (or evidence of future permanent counter installation).
-   **Wood-Burning Appliances**: Remove all ashes to prevent them from being drawn into the house during the blower door test.

<a id=\"the-day-of-your-evaluation\"></a>
## The day of your evaluation

-   **Safety**: Do not use fuel-fired space heating or water heating systems, or any wood-fired or solid-fuel fired appliances, as the blower door test could affect their safe operation. Return all equipment to normal settings after the test.
-   **Consent**: Read and sign the \"Notice to Homeowner\" consent form.
-   **Process Overview**: The energy advisor will review the scope, length, and purpose of the evaluation, and explain the data-collection process.
-   **Photos**: Photos will be taken of your homes exterior, mechanical systems, attic space, and any wood-burning appliances. All data and photos are protected by privacy legislation.
-   **Questions**: Feel free to ask the energy advisor about the tools and process.
-   **Assessment**: The advisor typically starts with the exterior and then moves to the interior, collecting data on:
    -   Your homes **airtightness** (blower door test).
    -   **Insulation levels** (walls, ceilings, basement).
    -   **Windows and exterior doors** (number, type, location).
    -   **Heating, cooling, and water heating equipment** (size, efficiency).
    -   **Ventilation equipment**.
    -   Other relevant **energy performance information**.
-   **Completion**: After the evaluation, the advisor will confirm all information and may discuss related programs or survey participation.
