# Home Renovation Savings - Go solar and save up to $5,000 back

## The Benefits of Solar
Adding rooftop solar panels and a battery energy storage system to your home can help you generate your own clean, renewable energy year-round. A home energy assessment is not required for these upgrades.

## Choose the Rebates That Are Right for You
Get rebates for rooftop solar panels on their own or combine them with a battery energy storage system.

### Available Rebates:

*   **Solar Panels:**
    *   **Up to $5,000 back**
    *   $1,000/kW, up to 50% of total costs

*   **Battery Energy Storage:**
    *   **Up to $5,000 back**
    *   $300/kWh, up to 50% of total costs

*Note*: Participants who receive an incentive for solar PV and battery storage projects through this program are **not eligible** to participate in a net-metering agreement with their Local Distribution Company (LDC). These incentives are for projects designed for load displacement only (electricity used exclusively to meet the homes energy consumption needs). Those contemplating solar PV and battery storage can choose either program incentives or net-metering.

Learn more about [net metering at the Ontario Energy Boards website](https://www.oeb.ca/consumer-information-and-protection/net-metering).

## How Does Solar Energy Work?
Solar photovoltaic (PV) technology converts sunlight directly into electricity. This can help you:
*   Manage energy costs
*   Add value to your home
*   Reduce greenhouse gas emissions
*   Help maintain a reliable electricity grid

**Pairing solar panels with battery energy storage** can make your home more resilient. Store excess solar power in your battery to use overnight, on cloudy days, in the event of a power outage, or at times of peak electricity demand.

*Eligibility for Battery Storage*: Battery storage systems must be paired with a new solar PV system and sized appropriately for the solar system being installed. They should be designed to primarily store electricity generated from the solar PV system, not from the electricity grid.

## How to Qualify for Solar and Storage Rebates
To receive solar and storage rebates, you must:
*   Be at least **18 years of age**.
*   Live in a **single detached, semi-detached, row house, townhome, or mobile home** on a permanent foundation.
*   **Own the home** where the solar and battery storage systems will be installed. If there are tenants, the owner must apply and be responsible for paying for the purchase and installation.
*   Be **connected to the Ontario electricity grid**.

*Note*: Cornwall Electric customers are not eligible as they are connected to the Hydro-Qubec electricity grid.

See detailed eligibility requirements on the [Terms and Conditions page](/terms-and-conditions).

## How to Get Your Rebates

1.  **Confirm your eligibility**
    See if your home qualifies for solar panel and storage rebates. You can complete a [quick form for guidance](/without-assessment/solar/solar-form).

2.  **Choose a contractor and obtain approval**
    Choose a contractor (refer to the [CanREA solar guide](https://renewablesassociation.ca/go-solar-guide/) for help). Your contractor will submit your [pre-installation form](/without-assessment/solar/solar-contractor) and assist with connection approval from your local electric utility.
    *Note*: We do not endorse or have any connection to contractors listed on these sites.

3.  **Complete upgrades**
    Once pre-approved, your contractor can install your solar and storage system, ensuring it meets program eligibility requirements. **Do not purchase or install equipment prior to receiving written pre-approval** from the Home Renovation Savings team and relevant local distribution companies.

4.  **Receive your rebate**
    After your contractor submits all required documentation and your application is approved, you will receive your rebate.
