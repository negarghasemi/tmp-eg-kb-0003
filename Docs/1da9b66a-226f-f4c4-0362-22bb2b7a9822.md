# Computer Room Air Conditioners

**Publication Date**: May 2024

## Technical Bulletin on Developing the Standards

- **Webinar**: Tuesday, June 11, 2024, 1:00-2:00 pm EDT

Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) has updated its Forward Regulatory Plan to include computer room air conditioners (CRACs) as a new energy-using product. This technical bulletin initiates the consultation process to gather stakeholder views on proposed requirements for CRACs, aiming to harmonize with United States standards.

- **Cost-Benefit Analysis**: NRCan will conduct a cost-benefit analysis using Canadian market data.
- **Regulatory Proposal**: The Government of Canada intends to pre-publish a regulatory proposal in the *Canada Gazette*, Part I, in **2025**.

## Background

CRACs are currently *not* defined as an energy-using product in Canadian Regulations.

- CRACs are commercial packaged or split system air-conditioners used in computer rooms or similar applications.
- **Benefits**: Energy efficiency standards for CRACs would save energy, reduce energy costs for Canadians, and decrease energy-related greenhouse gas emissions.
- **International Harmonization**: NRCan and the U.S. Department of Energy (U.S. DOE) signed a Memorandum of Understanding for cooperation on energy efficiency standards.
- **U.S. DOE Final Rule**: On June 02, 2023, the U.S. DOE published a final rule strengthening energy efficiency standards for floor-mounted and ceiling-mounted CRACs.
- **NRCan's Intent**: NRCan is considering amending its Regulations to harmonize with these U.S. energy efficiency and testing standards.

## Product Description

The definition of a CRAC would be consistent with **AHRI standards 1360-2022** and the **U.S. DOE**.

- **Computer Room Air Conditioner (CRAC)**: A computer and data processing room air conditioner that utilizes dedicated compressors and refrigerant cooling coils rather than chilled-water coils.
  - **Air-cooled CRAC**: CRAC refrigeration system with an air-cooled condenser heat exchanger.
  - **Glycol-cooled CRAC**: CRAC refrigeration system with a glycol-cooled condenser heat exchanger.
  - **Water-cooled CRAC**: CRAC refrigeration system with a water-cooled condenser heat exchanger.
- **Computer and Data Processing Room Air Conditioner (CDPR)**: An air conditioning unit specifically marketed for cooling data centers and information technology equipment (ITE).
  - **Ceiling-mounted CDPR**: Indoor unit marketed for installation above a dropped ceiling with horizontal airflow and multiple airflow connections.
  - **Floor-mounted CDPR**: Indoor unit marketed for installation on a raised floor, floor stand, or solid floor with two airflow configuration options:
    - **Flow direction options for floor-mounted CDPRs**:
      - **Downflow**: Return air enters above the cooling coil, discharge air leaves below.
      - **Horizontal-flow**: Neither downflow nor upflow.
      - **Upflow**: Return air enters below the cooling coil, discharge air leaves above.

*NRCan is not considering regulating **computer room air handlers**, which use chilled-water coils for cooling.* 

## Testing Standard

NRCan is considering incorporating by reference the following standard:

- **Appendix E to Subpart F of Part 431** to the U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, entitled *Uniform Test Method for Measuring the Energy Consumption of Computer Room Air Conditioners*.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date of Modifications**: Six months after publication in the *Canada Gazette*, Part II.
- **Application Date for New Standards**: CRACs manufactured on or after **May 28, 2024**.
- **Regulatory Pathway**: Governor in Council amendment.
- **Pre-publication**: Changes will be pre-published in the *Canada Gazette*, Part I, followed by a public comment period, with final publication typically within a year.

## Labelling Requirements

- NRCan is *not* considering labelling requirements for CRACs.

## Verification Requirements

- Products would require a **third-party verification mark** from a Standards Council of Canada accredited certification body.

## Reporting Requirements

### Energy Efficiency Report

The energy efficiency report for CRACs would include:

- **Mount & Flow**:
  - Floor mounted (downflow, upflow ducted, upflow non-ducted, horizontal flow)
  - Ceiling mounted (ducted, non-ducted)
- **Equipment Type**:
  - Air-cooled, air-cooled with fluid economizer, air-cooled with free air discharge condenser, air-cooled with free air discharge condenser and fluid economizer, air-cooled with ducted condenser, air-cooled with ducted condenser and fluid economizer, water-cooled, water-cooled with fluid economizer, glycol-cooled, or glycol-cooled with fluid economizer
- **Net sensible cooling capacity**
- **Flow**: Downflow, upflow ducted, upflow non-ducted, horizontal flow, ducted, non-ducted
- **NSenCOP efficiency**
- **Brand name**
- **Model number**
- **Name of the manufacturer**
- **Name of the certification body** whose verification mark would be affixed to the product

*This report must be submitted by the dealer to NRCan **before** the product is imported into Canada or traded inter-provincially for the first time.*

### Import Report

Dealers importing these products into Canada would include the following information on the customs release document:

- **Name of product**
- **Model number**
- **Brand name**
- **Address of the dealer** importing the product
- **Purpose of import** (e.g., for sale/lease in Canada without modification, for sale/lease after modification, or for use as a component in an exported product)
