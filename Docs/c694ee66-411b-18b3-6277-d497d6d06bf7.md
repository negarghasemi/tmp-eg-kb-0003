# Clothes Dryers: Energy Efficiency Overview

ENERGY STAR certified clothes dryers are designed to significantly reduce energy consumption, leading to cost savings and a smaller carbon footprint. Pairing them with an ENERGY STAR certified clothes washer can double these benefits.

## Key Benefits of ENERGY STAR Certified Clothes Dryers

-   **20% less energy** on average compared to standard models.
-   **Sensor technology** that automatically shuts off the dryer when clothes are dry, preventing over-drying and wear-and-tear.
-   **Extra-low heat settings** for energy-saving drying at lower temperatures.
-   **Heat pump dryers** offer even greater efficiency, saving an additional **20-60% more energy** than standard models. These models use and recirculate ambient heat for drying, effectively removing moisture. They are ideal for high-usage households or areas with high electricity rates and meet the criteria for **ENERGY STAR Most Efficient**.

## The ENERGY STAR Promise

-   All ENERGY STAR certified products undergo rigorous testing to meet **strict efficiency specifications**.
-   Certification is provided by an **independent third party**.
-   These products maintain **equal or superior performance** compared to standard alternatives.
-   *You can find and compare certified clothes dryers using the [ENERGY STAR Product Finder tool](https://www.energystar.gov/productfinder/product/certified-clothes-dryers/results?SetLanguage=English&NRCAN=on) (redirects to U.S. ENERGY STAR website).*

## Tips for Choosing an Energy-Efficient Clothes Dryer

-   **Check the EnerGuide label**: When comparing models, look for the EnerGuide label. **The lower the number, the better** the energy efficiency.
-   **Look for ENERGY STAR Most Efficient**: Products with this designation offer the highest energy savings.

## How to Use Your Clothes Dryer Efficiently

-   **Clean the lint trap** after every load to maintain optimal airflow.
-   **Regularly clean dryer vents** to improve airflow and reduce motor strain.
-   **Avoid overfilling** the dryer to ensure proper airflow and prevent motor overload.
-   **Scrub the lint filter monthly** if you use dryer sheets, as they can leave a film that reduces airflow. Use a toothbrush for thorough cleaning.
-   **Sort items by thickness**: Dry light, quick-drying clothes separately from heavier items like towels or jeans.
-   **Prevent over-drying**: Utilize the sensor setting or, if using timed drying, adjust the time to the minimum required for dry clothes.

## Regulations

Clothes dryers in Canada are subject to the country's **Energy Efficiency Regulations**, which establish minimum performance standards for energy efficiency.
