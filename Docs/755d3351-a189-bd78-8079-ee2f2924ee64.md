# ENERGY STAR Score for Multifamily Housing in Canada

## Overview

The ENERGY STAR score for Multifamily Housing applies to commercial housing with a common entrance and interior hallways. It assesses a propertys energy use against similar properties, considering climate, weather, and business activities. A statistical analysis identifies significant drivers of energy use and normalizes for these factors.

### Key Details:

- **Property types**: Commercial housing with at least 4 storeys above ground, or a horizontal footprint of at least 600m. The score applies to the entire Multifamily Housing, whether a single building or a campus.
- **Reference data**: Based on the Survey of Energy Consumption of Multi-Unit Residential Buildings (SECMURBs) for the year **2018**, commissioned by Natural Resources Canada (NRCan).
- **Adjustments for weather and business activity**: Includes adjustments for:
    - Number of units
    - Number of bedrooms
    - Percentage of units located in Mid-Rise and High-Rise buildings
    - Percent of the building that is heated
    - Percent of the building that is cooled
    - Weather and Climate (using heating degree days, retrieved based on postal code)
- **Release date**: This is the original release of the ENERGY STAR score for multifamily housing in Canada.

## Reference Data & Filters

The reference data for the peer property population comes from the **2018 SECMURBs survey**. Filters are applied to define the peer population and address technical limitations. These include Building Type Filters, Program Filters, Data Limitation Filters, and Analytical Filters.

## Variables Analysed

NRCan conducted a statistical analysis using weighted ordinary least squares regression to understand factors influencing energy use in multifamily housing.

### Dependent Variables

- **Source energy use intensity (source EUI)**: Total source energy use of the property divided by the gross floor area, expressed in annual gigajoules per square metre (GJ/m<sup>2</sup>).

### Independent Variables

Initially, numerous operational characteristics were considered. The final regression equation includes the following variables, which are significant drivers of source EUI:

- Number of bedrooms per 100m<sup>2</sup> (Bedroom Density, with a ceiling at 2.3)
- Number of residential units per 100m<sup>2</sup> (Unit Density)
- The percentage of residential units in a mid-rise or high-rise setting
- The percentage of the building that is cooled times the number of Cooling Degree Days (Percent Cooled x CDD)
- The percentage of the building that is heated times the number of Heating Degree Days (Percent Heated x HDD)

### Specific Analyses:

- **High, Mid, and Low-Rise Buildings**: Taller buildings tend to consume more energy due to factors like more common space. The 'Percent Units in Mid-Rise or High-Rise' variable accounts for this.
- **Laundry**: Due to insufficient variability in survey data (95% of properties had laundry facilities), a statistically significant difference could not be determined.
- **Climate Variables**: Both Heating Degree Days (HDD) and Cooling Degree Days (CDD) were found to be significant. An adjustment factor was applied for cooling energy to ensure a comprehensive model.

## Energy Star Score Lookup Table

The predicted source EUI is calculated using the regression equation. The *actual* source EUI is then divided by the *predicted* source EUI to determine an **Energy Efficiency Ratio**:

```
Energy Efficiency Ratio = Actual Source Energy Intensity / Predicted Source Energy Intensity
```

An efficiency ratio lower than one indicates greater efficiency. These ratios are sorted, and a smooth curve using a two-parameter gamma distribution (shape parameter alpha = 10.46, scale parameter beta = 0.0943) is fitted to assign a score from 1 to 100.

## Example Calculation

Heres an example of how an ENERGY STAR score is calculated for a multifamily housing property.

### Step 1: User enters building data into Portfolio Manager

- 12 months of energy use information for all energy types.
- Physical building information (size, location, etc.) and use details describing building activity.

#### Summary of Energy Data

| Energy Data | Value                 |
| :---------- | :-------------------- |
| Electricity | **300,000 kWh**       |
| Natural gas | **218,141 m<sup>3</sup>** |

#### Summary of Property Use Details

| Property Use Details                       | Value  |
| :----------------------------------------- | :----- |
| Gross floor area (m<sup>2</sup>)             | **9,000** |
| Total Number of Units                      | **180** |
| Total Number of Bedrooms                   | **250** |
| Units  Low Rise                           | **20**  |
| Units  Mid Rise                           | **0**   |
| Units  High Rise                          | **160** |
| Percent That Can Be Heated                 | **100%**|
| Percent That Can Be Cooled                 | **100%**|
| HDD (provided by Portfolio Manager)        | **5,751** |
| CDD (provided by Portfolio Manager)        | **117** |

### Step 2: Portfolio Manager computes the actual source EUI

- Total energy consumption for each fuel is converted into site energy and source energy.
- Source energy values are summed across all fuel types.
- Source energy is divided by gross floor area to determine actual source EUI.

#### Computing Actual Source EUI

| :---------- | :--------------------------- | :----------------- | :------ | :---------------- | :-------- |

| **Total Source Energy (GJ)** | _(Calculated)_ | _(Calculated)_ | _(Calculated)_ | _(Calculated)_ | **10,862** |
| **Source EUI (GJ/m<sup>2</sup>)** | _(Calculated)_ | _(Calculated)_ | _(Calculated)_ | _(Calculated)_ | **1.1207** |

### Step 3: Portfolio Manager computes the predicted source EUI

- Property use details are used to compute each building variable value in the regression equation.
- Centering values are subtracted to compute the centered variable for each operating parameter.
- Centered variables are multiplied by the coefficients from the multifamily housing regression equation to obtain a predicted source EUI.

#### Computing Predicted Source EUI

| Variable                               | Actual Building Value | Reference Centering Value | Building Centered Variable | Coefficient | Coefficient x Centered Variable |
| :------------------------------------- | :-------------------- | :------------------------ | :------------------------- | :---------- | :------------------------------ |
| Constant                               | -                     | -                         | -                          | 1.100       | 1.100                           |
| Bedroom Density\*                      | 2.300                 | 1.726                     | 0.574                      | 0.1130      | 6.52E-02                        |
| Unit Density                           | 2.000                 | 1.228                     | 0.772                      | 0.2420      | 0.187                           |

| Percent Cooled x CDD                   | 117.0                 | 113.1                     | 3.909                      | 2.000E-04   | 7.82E-04                        |
| Percent Heated x HDD                   | 5,751                 | 3,804                     | 1,947                      | 4.800E-05   | 9.35E-02                        |
| **Predicted Source EUI (GJ/m<sup>2</sup>)** | _(Calculated)_ | _(Calculated)_ | _(Calculated)_ | _(Calculated)_ | **1.476** |

### Step 4: Portfolio Manager computes the energy efficiency ratio

- The ratio equals the actual source EUI (Step 2) divided by the predicted source EUI (Step 3).
- Ratio = 1.1207 / 1.476 = **0.7593**

### Step 5: Portfolio Manager uses the efficiency ratio to assign a score via a lookup table

- The ratio from Step 4 is used to identify the score from the lookup table.
- A ratio of 0.7593 is greater than or equal to 0.7593 and less than 0.7675.
- **The ENERGY STAR score is 76**.
