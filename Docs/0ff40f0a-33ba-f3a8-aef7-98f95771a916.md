# ENERGY STAR for New Homes Standard

**ENERGY STAR for New Homes** is an initiative by the Office of Energy Efficiency of Natural Resources Canada. Its primary goal is to promote and encourage energy-efficient construction in new housing.

## Key Objectives and Benefits:

*   **Reduce greenhouse gas emissions**.
*   Ensure new homes are approximately **20% more energy efficient** than those built to Provincial or National Building Codes.
*   Lead to **reduced energy costs** for homeowners.

## Standard and Compliance:

The initiative is supported by the **ENERGY STAR for New Homes Standard**, which outlines the requirements for achieving this enhanced energy efficiency.

*   For a copy of the current standard, please contact the **ENERGY STAR for New Homes Team** at [esnhprogram-programmeesmn@nrcan-rncan.gc.ca](mailto:esnhprogram-programmeesmn@nrcan-rncan.gc.ca).

### Opaque Assemblies

*   The document **\"Tables for Calculating Effective Thermal Resistance of Opaque Assemblies\"** provides a method for calculating the effective thermal resistance of opaque assemblies.
*   This document is used to demonstrate **compliance** with the prescriptive approach under the ENERGY STAR for New Homes Standard.
*   It is also referenced in the National Building Code of Canada for calculating additional assemblies and configurations.
*   It offers easy-to-use look-up tables for the effective thermal resistance of assemblies containing both framing members and cavity insulation.
*   For a copy of these tables, please contact the **ENERGY STAR for New Homes Team** at [esnhprogram-programmeesmn@nrcan-rnrncan.gc.ca](mailto:esnhprogram-programmeesmn@nrcan-rncan.gc.ca).
