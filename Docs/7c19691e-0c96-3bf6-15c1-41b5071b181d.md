# Single package vertical heat pumps

## Energy Efficiency Regulations

### Regulatory Definition

**Single package vertical heat pump**: A single package commercial heat pump that is air-cooled, encased, and uses reverse cycle refrigeration as its primary heat source. The major components of both are arranged vertically and are intended for mounting through, or on either side of, an exterior wall.

### Compliance Date

**Manufactured on or after**: January 1, 2011

*For models manufactured prior, refer to the Regulations.*

### Compliant Product Models

**Single package vertical heat pumps** as listed on NRCans searchable database: [https://spl-lpi.nrcan-rncan.gc.ca/en-US/product/?product=HP.SinglePackageVertical&page=1](https://spl-lpi.nrcan-rncan.gc.ca/en-US/product/?product=HP.SinglePackageVertical&page=1)

### Testing Standard

**CSA C746-17**

### Energy Efficiency Standard

*Energy performance Levels standard for single package heat pumps (SPVHP)*

| Product type | Size                                                | Minimum efficiency requirement |
| :----------- | :-------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------- |
| **SPVHP**    | <19 kW (65,000 Btu/h)                               | EER =  11.0                   |
| **SPVHP**    |  19 kW (65,000 Btu/h) and < 39.5 kW (135,000 Btu/h) | EER =  10.0                   |
| **SPVHP**    |  39.5 kW (135,000 Btu/h) and < 70 kW (240,000 Btu/h) | EER =  10.0                   |

* **COP**: Coefficient of performance
* **EER**: Energy efficiency ratio
* **Btu/h**: British thermal units per hour
* **kW**: kilowatts
