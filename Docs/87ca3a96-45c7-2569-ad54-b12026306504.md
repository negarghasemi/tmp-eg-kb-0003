# Guide to ENERGY STAR Certified Homes

## Whats in an ENERGY STAR certified home?

Each ENERGY STAR certified home features a combination of common and flexible energy-saving elements.

### Common Features

-   **Energy-efficient windows**
-   **Heat Recovery Ventilator (HRV)**: *Recaptures lost heat and provides outdoor air.*
-   **400 kWh/year electrical savings**: *Typically achieved through ENERGY STAR certified lighting or appliances.*
-   **Airtightness**: *Must meet certain thresholds.*

### Flexible Features

The specific upgrades vary by home and builder's design choices, including:

-   **Level of insulation**
-   **Efficiency of the water heater**
-   **Extent of air tightness**

## The ENERGY STAR Label

*Image: An ENERGY STAR label*

### What does the ENERGY STAR Standard specify?

Developed by **Natural Resources Canada (NRCan)**, the ENERGY STAR for New Homes Standard (ESNH) outlines the technical requirements for a home to receive the ENERGY STAR label.

-   **Main Objective**: To be, on average, approximately **20% more efficient** than a home built to local building codes, primarily based on space and water heating.
-   **Updates**: The ESNH Standard is updated as regional building codes change. *During transition periods, homes may be 20% more efficient than the previous code, but not necessarily the new one.*

### How do builders meet the Standard?

Builders have two valid paths to certify their homes:

1.  **Prescriptive Path**:
    -   Builders follow predetermined specifications outlined in the Standard.
    -   They must meet a set of minimum requirements.
    -   They choose from a series of upgrade options, each with allocated points.
    -   The total points must meet a certain number, pre-determined by the province and climate zone.

2.  **Performance Path**:
    -   Builders aim to meet an energy performance target specified for their province and climate zone.
    -   They must still meet minimum requirements but have high flexibility in choosing features.
    -   The home is evaluated using energy simulation software to ensure it meets the required energy target.

*Both paths require on-site verification by an energy advisor.*

### Who are the different parties involved?

-   **Natural Resources Canada (NRCan)**: Administrator of the program, manages the ENERGY STAR mark, and develops/updates the ESNH Standard.
-   **Service Organizations and Energy Advisors**: Licensed to use ENERGY STAR trademarks and tools. They maintain networks of energy advisors who verify that homes meet the Standard before labeling. *They operate as independent businesses, not as agents or employees of NRCan.*
-   **Builders**: Licensed to use ENERGY STAR trademarks. They work with service organizations/energy advisors to construct certified homes.

**NRCan Disclaimers**:

### Questions to ask your builder:

-   Which energy efficiency features will be included in my ENERGY STAR certified home (e.g., insulation R-values, equipment efficiency, energy-efficient lighting or products)?
-   What should I do to maintain and operate my ENERGY STAR certified home?
-   To which version of the ENERGY STAR Standard will my home be built? Is the ENERGY STAR Standard you are using based on the current building code or the previous one?

### Questions about your Energy Star home? (Certification Review Process)

1.  **Contact your Service Organization**: First, contact the ENERGY STAR for New Homes service organization that oversaw your home's verification and labeling. Their contact information can be found on your ENERGY STAR for New Homes label or Certificate.
2.  **Service Organization's Role**: They may follow up with the energy advisor or builder to investigate concerns and determine if procedures and requirements were met at the time of evaluation and labeling.
3.  **Contact NRCan (if unresolved)**: If your concerns are not resolved, you may initiate a certification review process with NRCan by completing and submitting the [ENERGY STAR for New Homes Certification Review Form](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/19gxwXaxW6gmOzik1mf5dURZRJIw8C-yx?usp=sharing).
    -   **Form Requirements**: Clearly outline issues, actions taken (including contact with service organization), and include relevant supporting documentation (photos, emails).
    -   **Submission**: Send completed forms to [esnhprogram-programmeesmn@nrcan-rncan.gc.ca](mailto:esnhprogram-programmeesmn@nrcan-rncan.gc.ca).
