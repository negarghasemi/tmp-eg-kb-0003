# Central air conditionersEnerGuide

## The Voluntary EnerGuide Label

**Energy Efficiency Measurement:** The energy efficiency of a central air conditioner is measured by its **Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio (SEER)**. A higher SEER number indicates a more efficient model.

### EnerGuide Label Details

The voluntary EnerGuide label displays the **model type** and its **SEER**. It also includes a **pointer** to show where the model's SEER falls on a scale of energy efficiency compared to similar models.

*Note: In brochures referring to multiple models or sizes, there may be more than one pointer on the scale.*

EnerGuide labels may appear alongside the **ENERGY STAR symbol** for certified models.

### Key Items Displayed on EnerGuide Labels (for all types of central air conditioners):

Regardless of the specific type of central air conditioner, the EnerGuide label consistently displays three main items:

1.  **The Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio (SEER)** of the specific air conditioner model.
2.  **The range of SEERs available for similar models**, considering the same type and similar cooling capacity.
3.  **An indicator** that positions the model in comparison with the most and least efficient models within its class.
