# Room Air Conditioners: Energy Efficiency Overview

This report provides an overview of energy efficiency considerations for room air conditioners, focusing on ENERGY STAR certified products and related energy-saving tips.

## ENERGY STAR Certification

**ENERGY STAR certified room air conditioners** typically use **10% less energy** on average compared to standard models. The **ENERGY STAR Most Efficient** designation identifies the best-performing models, offering even greater energy savings.

*   *Saving energy helps reduce costs and your carbon footprint.*

*Note: Heat pumps with a reverse-cycle feature can also be used for air conditioning and space heating. More information is available for [air-source](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/energy-star/products/list-certified-products/air-source-heat-pumps) and [ground source](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/energy-star/products/list-certified-products/ground-source-heat-pumps) heat pumps.*

## Key Features for Energy Efficiency

*   **Improved Sealing and Insulation**: Certified units feature better materials and clearer instructions to minimize costly and uncomfortable air leaks around the unit.
*   **Smart Functionality**: Some models offer Wi-Fi enabled smart features, allowing control via connected devices like mobile phones or tablets.

## Helpful Energy-Saving Tips

*   **Proper Sizing**: Buying an oversized air conditioner does not provide better cooling; it is less effective and wastes energy.
*   **Professional Consultation**: Consult a qualified air conditioning contractor to select the correct unit size and ensure proper electrical circuitry.
*   **Optimal Placement**: Ensure adequate air circulation. Position the unit away from doors, corners, drapes, and furniture. Choose a wall or window with northern exposure and/or ample shade if possible.
*   **Maintenance and Storage**: Proper cleaning, maintenance, and removal/storage of the unit during winter extend its life and maintain efficiency, leading to long-term savings.
*   **Seal Gaps**: Seal large gaps with manufacturer-provided panels or side curtains. Use peelable caulking or other airtight sealant for remaining cracks to block air leaks and insects.
*   *For more tips, refer to the booklet: \"Air Conditioning Your Home\".*

## Energy Labels

Room air conditioners in Canada may carry two types of labels:

*   **ENERGY STAR Symbol**: Indicates the model is highly energy-efficient.
*   **EnerGuide Label**: Shows the model's energy consumption and how it compares to similar models. Learn more about [EnerGuide labels for room air conditioners](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/product-energy-ratings/energuide/room-air-conditioners-energuide).

### The ENERGY STAR Promise

All ENERGY STAR certified products are rigorously tested to meet strict efficiency specifications and are certified by an independent third party. They offer comparable or superior performance to standard products without compromise.

## Energy Efficiency Regulations

Room, central, and large commercial air conditioners are subject to **Canada's *Energy Efficiency Regulations***, which establish minimum performance standards for energy efficiency. Details can be found in the [Guide to the Regulations](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/energy-efficiency-regulations/guide-canada-s-energy-efficiency-regulations).
