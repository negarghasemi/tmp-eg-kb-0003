# Windows, doors and skylights  ENERGY STAR Canada technical specification

### Version 5.0  January 1, 2020 FINAL

This technical specification outlines the requirements for residential windows, doors, and skylights to be certified under the ENERGY STAR program in Canada. It is issued by Natural Resources Canada (NRCan), which is authorized by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) to administer the ENERGY STAR name and symbol in Canada. Products must meet this specification to be promoted as ENERGY STAR certified. Manufacturers are also required to sign a Participant Administrative Arrangement with NRCan.

## 1) Definitions

### Product types

-   **Window**: An assembled unit with a frame/sash and glazing for light/air, designed for vertical installation in residential building external walls.
-   **Door**: An assembled unit with a frame and one or more moving panels for ingress/egress, designed for vertical installation between conditioned and unconditioned space in residential buildings.
-   **Skylight**: A window for sloped or horizontal roof application in residential buildings, primarily for daylighting/ventilation. Tubular Daylighting Devices (TDDs) are considered skylights.

### Product subcategories

-   **Sliding door**: A door with one or more manually operated panels that slide horizontally within a common frame.
-   **Swinging door**: A door with a hinge attachment or a single, fixed vertical axis for rotation between open and closed positions.
-   **Sidelite**: A glazed frame or sash used as a companion product installed on one or both sides of a door.
-   **Transom**: A glazed frame or sash used as a companion product installed above a door or window. Transoms > 700 mm (27 in.) in height are windows; those  700 mm (27 in.) are transoms.
-   **Tubular daylighting device (TDD) or tubular skylight**: A non-operable device transmitting daylight from a roof to an interior ceiling via a tubular conduit. Includes an exterior glazed weathering surface, a reflective tube, and an interior sealing device. Considered skylights.
-   **Dynamic glazing product**: A fenestration product with fully reversible ability to change performance properties (U-factor, SHGC, VT, ER). This includes between-glazing shading systems and chromogenic glazing.
    -   **Chromogenic glazing**: Glazings that reversibly vary optical properties (e.g., electrochromic, Suspended Particle Device/SPD, photochromic, thermochromic).
    -   **Internal shading system**: Operable blinds or shades between glass panes in a window, door, or skylight.

### Performance metrics

-   **U-Factor**: Heat transfer per time per area per degree of temperature difference in **W/mK** (Btu/h ftF). *Conversion: U-factor in Btu/h ftF multiplied by 5.678263 converts to W/mK.*
-   **Solar heat gain coefficient (SHGC)**: Ratio of solar heat gain entering a space through the fenestration product to the incident solar radiation.
-   **Air leakage**: Flow of air through fenestration products in **L/s/m**. *Conversion: Air leakage in cfm/ft multiplied by 5.08 converts to L/s/m.*
-   **Energy rating (ER)**: A unitless value balancing heat loss (U-factor), air leakage loss, and passive solar gain for vertically installed fenestration systems in low-rise residential buildings.
    *   **ER Equation**: ER = (57.76  SHGCw)  (21.90  Uw)  (1.97  L75) + 40
        *   **SHGCw**: fenestration system solar heat gain coefficient
        *   **Uw**: fenestration system U-factor (W/mK)
        *   **L75**: fenestration system air leakage rate at 75 Pa, established per AAMA/WDMA/CSA 101/I.S.2/A440 (North American Fenestration Standard) in L/sm. *L75 is the average of infiltration and exfiltration measurements.*
-   **Decimal precision**: Values are rounded using the arithmetic method before calculation, to the decimal precision defined below:

| Value                     | Decimal precision |
| :------------------------ | :---------------- |
| Air leakage (infiltration) | 1                 |
| Air leakage (exfiltration) | 1                 |
| L75                       | 1                 |
| U-factor and SHGC         | 2                 |
| Energy Rating             | 0                 |

### Other definitions

-   **Residential**: Products designed for installation in buildings meeting Part 9 of the National Building Code of Canada (three stories or less, less than 600 square metres in area).
-   **Insulating glass unit (IGU)**: A preassembled unit of glass lites, sealed at edges and separated by dehydrated space(s).
-   **North American Fenestration Standard (NAFS)**: Common name for the AAMA/WDMA/CSA 101/I.S.2/A440 testing standard.
-   **Private labeller**: A company marketing products under its own name that are manufactured by another company.
-   **Standards Council of Canada (SCC)**: Canadian federal Crown corporation accrediting standards writing organizations, test laboratories, and certification agencies.
-   **U.S. National Fenestration Rating Council (NFRC)**: U.S. organization developing energy performance standards for fenestration products and providing third-party verification.

## 2) Scope

-   **Included products**: Residential windows, doors, or skylights as defined herein, except for excluded products.
-   **Excluded products**: Products assembled onsite (e.g., sash packs/kits); windows, doors, or skylights for non-residential buildings; and attachments not included in a products certified energy performance rating.

## 4) Test and certification requirements

-   All models energy performance shall be certified using:
    -   _CSA A440.2-14 or CSA A440.2-19 Fenestration Energy Performance_
    -   _ANSI/NFRC 100-14 or ANSI/NFRC 100-17 Procedure for Determining Fenestration Product U-factors_ and _ANSI/NFRC 200-14 or ANSI/NFRC 200-17 Procedure for Determining Fenestration Product Solar Heat Gain Coefficient and Visible Transmittance at Normal Incidence_
-   All models shall be tested to and meet the minimum requirements of:
    -   _AAMA/WDMA/CSA 101/I.S.2/A440-08 North American Fenestration Standard/Specification for windows, doors, and skylights (NAFS)-08_ and its Canadian supplement _CSA A440S1-09_
    -   _AAMA/WDMA/CSA 101/I.S.2/A440-11 North American Fenestration Standard/Specification for windows, doors, and skylights (NAFS)-11_ and its Canadian supplement _CSA A440S1-17_
    *Note: Certification to NAFS and the Canadian supplement is not a requirement.*
-   All Insulating Glass Units shall be certified using:
    -   _ASTM E2190-10 Standard Specification for Insulating Glass Unit Performance and Evaluation_
    -   _CAN/CGSB 12.8-17 Insulating Glass Units Standard_
-   The model selected for testing or simulation must be representative of the product intended for ENERGY STAR marketing and labelling.
-   Certification for energy performance must be conducted by an organization accredited by the SCC or by NFRC.
-   Testing to NAFS and the Canadian supplement must be conducted by an organization accredited by the SCC or other signatories to APLAC and/or ILAC Mutual Recognition Arrangement.

## 5) Model registration and labelling

-   Product models must be registered with NRCan using the designated submission form to be labelled and considered ENERGY STAR certified in Canada.
-   Registered product models must be labelled in accordance with the _Requirements for the labelling and promotion of ENERGY STAR certified windows, doors and skylights sold in Canada_ to be considered ENERGY STAR certified in Canada.

## 6) Installation instructions

Products shall have installation instructions readily available online or packaged with the product. Electronic versions are acceptable on retailer, manufacturer, and/or industry association websites. Disclaimers may limit liability. Generic instructions covering common situations are acceptable.

## 7) Effective date

The effective date for Version 5.0 is **January 1, 2020**, superseding all previous versions. Models must meet the ENERGY STAR specification in effect on their manufacture date (when assembly is complete).

## 8) Future criteria revisions

NRCan reserves the right to change the specification if technological and/or market changes impact its usefulness. Revisions are made through industry discussions. ENERGY STAR certification is not automatically granted for the life of a product model.
