# Property Energy Efficiency Overview: Low-Solar and High-Solar Gain Glazings

This report focuses on the significant impact of window glazing on a property's energy efficiency, particularly in new homes where windows can account for over a quarter of annual space heating needs.

## Key Energy Performance Indicators

**Glazing** is a crucial factor in a window's energy efficiency. While low-emissivity (low-e) coated windows generally improve thermal performance, their solar performance can vary significantly.

### Solar Heat Gain Coefficient (SHGC)

*The SHGC represents the fraction of incident solar radiation that a window admits.*

*   **Range:** Between **0** and **1**.
*   **Low Solar Gain Products:** SHGC of less than **0.30**.
*   **High Solar Gain Products:** SHGC greater than **0.30**.

### Heat Loss Coefficient (U-Factor)

*The U-factor measures how well a window prevents heat from escaping.*

## Canadian Center for Housing Technology (CCHT) Window Glazing Project

A project conducted by the National Research Council of Canada (NRC), Natural Resources Canada (NRCan), and Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation (CMHC), with support from 37 companies, compared high solar gain (HSG) and low solar gain (LSG) glazing systems using twin-house facilities.

### Tested Window Specifications

Thirty-one glazing units were tested in various window types (casement, fixed casement, fixed, and patio door) on all four orientations (north, south, east, and west-facing walls). Both HSG and LSG windows were double-glazed and low-e coated.

| Glazing Type | U-Factor (W/mK) | U-Factor (Btu/hr.ft.F) |
| :----------- | :--------------- | :----------------------- |
| **HSG**      | **1.76**         | **0.31**                 |
| **LSG**      | **1.62**         | **0.285**                |

## Energy Performance Results (Canadian Locations)

Results from 10 Canadian locations demonstrated significant energy cost savings compared to conventional windows:

*   **High Solar Gain (HSG) Windows:**
    *   **Energy Cost Savings:** **13% to 17%**
    *   **Annual Savings:** **$117-$354 CDN**

*   **Low Solar Gain (LSG) Windows:**
    *   **Energy Cost Savings:** **8% to 10%**
    *   **Annual Savings:** **$71-$203 CDN**

## Key Recommendations and Lessons Learned

*   Energy efficiency benefits depend on the **mode of operation**, **orientation**, and **climate**.
*   Combining different glazing types (e.g., **LSG on the north**, **HSG on the south**) can yield optimal results.

## ENERGY STAR Criteria

Using ENERGY STAR certified products is a straightforward way to ensure energy-efficient windows. New specifications (Fall 2010) for ENERGY STAR include:

*   **Minimum SHGC:** Approximately **0.22**
*   **Minimum U-value:** **2.0**
