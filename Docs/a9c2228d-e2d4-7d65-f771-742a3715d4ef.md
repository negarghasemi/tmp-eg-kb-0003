# EnerGuide Information for Manufacturers/Importers

## The EnerGuide Advantage

The **EnerGuide label** provides Canadian consumers with confidence regarding the **energy performance** of products. It applies to:

*   **Major household appliances**
*   **Some heating and cooling equipment**

### Key Information

*   **Government-backed label**: Assures consumers that information on energy consumption or energy efficiency is **tested and verified**.
*   **Comparison shopping**: Enables consumers to easily compare products based on energy performance.
*   **Trademark**: The EnerGuide symbol is a **registered trademark of the Government of Canada** and cannot be reproduced without permission.
*   **Regulations**: Canada's *Energy Efficiency Regulations* establish **minimum energy performance standards** for products.
    *   These regulations specify which products require **mandatory EnerGuide labelling**.
    *   **Voluntary use** of the label is permitted for select products.
*   **Responsibility**: Manufacturers and dealers are responsible for meeting the labelling requirements.
*   **Enforcement**: Natural Resources Canada enforces compliance with the Regulations.

### Administering Bodies for EnerGuide Labels

*   **HVAC Equipment**:
    *   **The Heating, Refrigeration and Air Conditioning Institute of Canada (HRAI)**: Administers labels in all provinces except Quebec.
    *   **Corporation des matres mcaniciens en tuyauterie du Qubec (CMMTQ)**: Delivers the program in Quebec.
*   **Gas Fireplaces**:
    *   **The Hearth, Patio and Barbecue Association of Canada (HPBAC)**: Administers EnerGuide labels for gas fireplaces.
