# Success stories: Oil to Heat Pump Affordability program

## Homeowners who switched from oil heating to heat pumps

Thousands of Canadian homeowners have made the switch from oil home heating to eligible electric heat pump systems with the help of funding provided by the [Oil to Heat Pump Affordability (OHPA) program](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/home-energy-efficiency/canada-greener-homes-initiative/oil-heat-pump-affordability-program).

On this page, youll find some of their stories and discover the financial savings, enhanced comfort and environmental benefits that came from switching to more energy-efficient home heating.

Explore how the OHPA program has transformed the heating and cooling efficiency for these homeowners.

### Video: Cheryl from Ontario

Cheryl replaced oil heating with a heat pump that keeps her family comfortable all year long while reducing heating costs.

*Transcript excerpt:*
> \"Having converted to the heat pump gives me so much peace of mind, not only being energy efficient and the highest efficiency available to my home, but also that I have available funds for my family for typical family adventures, and not having to spend **$1,000 on a fuel source of propane or oil** is just exceptional.\"

**Motivation:** Needed a full furnace and air conditioner installation as both were over 20 years old. Researched and found grant information.

**Financial Impact:**
*   Was eligible for up to **$10,000** against her purchase.
*   Would not have replaced both systems without the grant.
*   Received the grant check within a week before installation.
*   Oil heating would have cost approximately **$1,000 a month** during winter.
*   With the heat pump, pays approximately **$50 to $100 more in hydro**, but saves all fuel source costs.

**Comfort/Health:** Home is comfortably heated and cooled for her and her two children, always on demand.

**Advice:** Examine options, contact a contractor to determine price and needs, consider heating/cooling requirements, age of current equipment, and availability of warranties.

## Important extra tips:

*   **Confirm eligibility**: Your contractor must include the **Air-Conditioning, Heating and Refrigeration Institute (AHRI) number** with the quote(s) for the selected heat pump, brand name, and model numbers of the corresponding indoor and outdoor units.
*   **Verify products**: Search the [eligible product lists](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/home-energy-efficiency/canada-greener-homes-initiative/eligibility-criteria-oil-heat-pump-affordability-program#eligibleList) to verify if the recommended heat pump is eligible under the OHPA program.
*   **Contractor attestation**: Its mandatory that your contractor signs an [attestation form](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/sites/nrcan/files/energy/pdf/AttestationForm-Oil2HeatPump_e.pdf) after your heat pump is installed, confirming it can distribute heat throughout your entire home and is eligible. Ensure your contractor will agree to sign the form before hiring them.
*   **Completion timeline**: Confirm that all the work can be completed within **6 months** of receiving your grant payment.
*   **Ground source heat pumps**: For ground source heat pumps, the contractor must attest that the installation has been completed according to the [CSA C448 standard (latest version)  Design and installation of ground source heat pump systems](https://www.csagroup.org/store/product/2701471/). You must submit this form after the work is complete.
