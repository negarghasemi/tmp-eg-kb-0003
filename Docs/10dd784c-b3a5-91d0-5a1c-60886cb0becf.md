# Room Air ConditionersEnerGuide

## The Mandatory EnerGuide Label

The energy efficiency of a room air conditioner is measured by its **combined energy efficiency ratio (CEER)**: the higher the number, the more efficient the model.

A room air conditioners mandatory EnerGuide label shows the **model type** and its **CEER**. It also uses a pointer to show where that value falls on a scale of energy efficiency for similar models. Use this value and scale to compare room air conditioners.

The EnerGuide label also identifies the model as **louvred** (a window unit) or **non-louvred** (a through-the-wall unit) and displays its **cooling capacity in Btu/h**.

The EnerGuide label may incorporate the **ENERGY STAR symbol** for qualified models.

### EnerGuide label for room air conditioners

There are five main items displayed on the label:

1.  **Combined energy efficiency ratio (CEER)** of the room air conditioner model.
2.  **Range of CEERs** available for similar models (same type and similar cooling capacity)
3.  **CEER indicator**, which positions the model compared with the most efficient and least efficient models in the same class
4.  **Type** (louvred or non-louvred) and **cooling capacity in Btu/h**.
5.  **Model number** of the unit

### EnerGuide label for room air conditioners with an ENERGY STAR qualification

There are six main items displayed on the label:

1.  **Combined energy efficiency ratio (CEER)** of the room air conditioner model.
2.  **Range of CEERs** available for similar models (same type and similar cooling capacity)
3.  **CEER indicator**, which positions the model compared with the most efficient and least efficient models in the same class
4.  **Type** (louvred or non-louvred) and **cooling capacity in Btu/h**.
5.  **Model number** of the unit
6.  **ENERGY STAR symbol**
