# Electric Vehicle Chargers

## Overview

Buying an **ENERGY STAR certified electric vehicle supply equipment** (EV charger) can help you save money. These certified models **use 40% less energy than standard models** in standby mode (when not actively charging a vehicle), which typically accounts for 85% of the product's lifetime.

EV chargers deliver energy to both all-electric vehicles (EVs) and plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs). The ENERGY STAR specification covers both **Level 1 and Level 2 EV chargers**, which provide alternating-current (AC) electricity to the vehicle.

*For more information, refer to the \"Plugging in\" resource.*

## Key Features

ENERGY STAR certified EV chargers come with various features to enhance efficiency and convenience:

*   **Basic Features**: Auto-restart and LED status indicators.
*   **Additional Features**: Programming and timers for manually scheduled delayed starts.
*   **Installation**: Can be installed indoors and outdoors (e.g., home garages, parking lots).
    *   *Over 10,000 Level 1 and Level 2 EV chargers are available in Canada. You can locate the nearest one [here](https://www.nrcan.gc.ca/energy-efficiency/transportation-energy-efficiency/electric-charging-alternative-fuelling-stationslocator-map#/find/nearest).*
*   **Connectivity**: Some models include **Wi-Fi technology** for remote power monitoring.
    *   These 'smart-grid ready' products can help households and property managers take advantage of special energy bill savings programs from local electric utilities.

*You can use the [ENERGY STAR Product Finder tool](https://www.energystar.gov/productfinder/product/certified-evse/results?SetLanguage=English&NRCAN=on) to find and compare certified electric vehicle supply equipment (redirects to the US ENERGY STAR website).*

## Helpful Tips for Consumers

Consider the following when choosing an EV charger to maximize energy savings and convenience:

*   **Incentive Programs**: Contact your local electricity provider to inquire about special EV charger incentive programs. You can start your search using the [ENERGY STAR rebates and incentives finder](http://oee.nrcan.gc.ca/energy/products/energystar/why-buy/programs.cfm?attr=0).
*   **Time-of-Use Plans**: Check if your utility offers time-of-use plans. Charging overnight or on weekends, when electricity costs are lowest, can significantly increase your savings. Look for an EV charger with the ability to control charging times if your car doesn't already have this feature.
*   **Outdoor Rating**: If your parking spot is outdoors (not in a garage), an outdoor rating is essential for the charger.
*   **Charger Levels**: 
    *   Most plug-in vehicles include a **Level 1 charging station** that uses a standard household outlet. This might be sufficient for plug-in hybrids with limited electric range or if you don't drive frequently. Ask your dealer for an ENERGY STAR certified Level 1 EV charger.
    *   **Upgrading to a Level 2 charger** provides faster charging times, which may be necessary for larger battery EVs to charge overnight.
*   **Professional Installation**: Consider having a **certified electrician install your charger**. It's recommended to install one that carries at least **50 amps**; this will only cost slightly more initially but can save significant money in the future if you acquire an electric car that charges at a higher rate.

## The ENERGY STAR Promise

All ENERGY STAR certified products undergo rigorous testing to meet strict efficiency specifications. They are **certified by an independent third party** and are guaranteed to perform as well as or better than standard products without compromising performance.
