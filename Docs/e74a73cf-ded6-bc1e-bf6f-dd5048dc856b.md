# Central air conditioners and heat pumps

## May 2021 Technical Bulletin

### Overview

Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) is amending Canadas *Energy Efficiency Regulations* to align **single-phase** central air conditioners and heat pumps with U.S. standards, while incorporating Canadian climate considerations.

*   **Webinar:** Tuesday, May 18, 1:30-2:30 pm EST.
*   This bulletin initiates the consultation process to gather stakeholder views on proposed requirements.
*   Input will inform the development of Amendment 17. A cost-benefit analysis will be conducted using Canadian market data.
*   A regulatory proposal is expected to be pre-published in the *Canada Gazette, Part I* within approximately one year.

### Background

The Government of Canada is committed to improving energy efficiency standards and reducing regulatory burden through alignment with U.S. standards where appropriate.

*   Central air conditioners and heat pumps manufactured on or after February 3, 1995, are subject to Canada's *Energy Efficiency Regulations*.
*   The most recent changes (2016 and 2018) aligned seasonal Minimum Energy Performance Standards (MEPS) and average off-mode power consumption MEPS with U.S. requirements.
*   On January 6, 2017, the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) published a direct final rule introducing a new test standard and more stringent MEPS for products manufactured on or after January 1, 2023.
*   NRCan is considering aligning its Regulations with the U.S. DOE's test standard requirements.
*   **Canadian Adjustment:** The optional -15C (5F) test point in the U.S. DOE test procedure would become mandatory in Canadian regulations to better reflect Canadian operating conditions.
*   **Climate Region:** Minimum energy performance requirements would be aligned with U.S. DOE, but reporting would incorporate the -15C (5F) test point and be based on Climate Region V (instead of U.S. Climate Region IV).
*   **Through-the-wall (TTW) Products:** NRCan is considering sunsetting the TTW product class. Products previously defined as TTW would be considered space-constrained products and comply with those standards.

### Product Description

**Central air conditioners** and **heat pumps** are air-source units with a rated capacity of less than **19 kW (65,000 Btu/h)**. These include:

*   Single package
*   Split system (single-split, multi-head mini-split, multi-split including variable refrigerant flow (VRF), multi-circuit systems)
*   Ducted
*   Ductless
*   Space-constrained
*   Small-duct, high-velocity air conditioners and heat pumps

### Energy Performance Testing Standard

NRCan is considering referencing the following energy performance testing standard:

*   **Appendix M1 to subpart B, Part 430 of Title 10** to the United States Code of Federal Regulations, entitled *Uniform Test Method for Measuring the Energy Consumption of Central Air Conditioners and Heat Pumps*.
*   **Canadian Adjustments:**
    *   The optional -15C (5F) test point in the U.S. DOE test procedure would become mandatory in Canadian regulations.
    *   The HSPF2 metric used for compliance would be based on **Climate Region V**.

*   The requirements for average off-mode power consumption (*PW,OFF*) remain unchanged.

### Important Dates

*   Modifications to the Regulations would come into force **six months** after the date of publication in the *Canada Gazette, Part II*.
*   NRCan is considering making these regulatory requirements applicable to products manufactured **after January 1, 2023**.

### Labelling Requirements

*   No mandatory regulatory label requirements are proposed.
*   Voluntary labelling requirements will be determined in consultation with the industry.

### Verification Requirements

*   Products must continue to carry a **third-party verification mark**.
*   This mark indicates that the product meets the seasonal cooling and heating MEPS (SEER2 and HSPF2) and the average off-mode power consumption (PW,OFF) MEPS.
*   It also confirms that the reported information has been verified.
*   The verification mark must be from a Standards Council of Canada accredited certification body operating an energy efficiency certification program for the product.

### Reporting Requirements

#### Energy Efficiency Report

The energy efficiency report must include the following product-specific information obtained from the U.S. M1 test procedure, as adjusted for Canada:

*   **SEER2**
*   **Rated cooling capacity**
*   **HSPF2 for Region V** and **rated heating capacity @-8.3C (47F)** for heat pumps
*   **P<sub>W,OFF</sub>**

For heat pumps, the report would also require:

*   **COP @-15C (5F) at rated capacity**
*   **Rated capacity @-15C (5F)**

This report must be submitted by the dealer to NRCan **before** the product is imported into Canada or traded inter-provincially for the first time.

#### Import Report

No change is considered for import reporting.
