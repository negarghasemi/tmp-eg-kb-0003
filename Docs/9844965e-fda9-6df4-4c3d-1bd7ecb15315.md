# Gas Clothes Dryers

**Date**: May 2024

## Technical Bulletin on Amending the Standards

Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) issued a Notice of Intent on April 2, 2022, to harmonize Canada's *Energy Efficiency Regulations* for five major home appliances, including household clothes dryers, with those of the United States Department of Energy (U.S. DOE). In April 2024, NRCan updated its Forward Regulatory Plan, indicating an intention to expand the scope of clothes dryers to include household gas clothes dryers, harmonizing with the United States.

NRCan is considering harmonizing energy efficiency standards, testing standards, and manufacturing dates for household vented gas clothes dryers with the U.S. DOE's recent final rulemaking.

This technical bulletin initiates the consultation process to collect stakeholder views on the proposed requirements. Input will inform the development of future amendments. A cost-benefit analysis will be conducted using Canadian market data to assess economic and environmental impacts.

The Government of Canada intends to pre-publish a regulatory proposal in the *Canada Gazette*, Part I, in 2025.

## Background

*   **Current Regulation**: Household electric clothes dryers manufactured as of February 3, 1995, are subject to the Regulations. Household vented gas clothes dryers are not currently regulated.
*   **Objective**: Efficiency standards for household clothes dryers aim to save energy, reduce overall costs for Canadians, decrease energy-related greenhouse gas emissions, and encourage efficient product designs.
*   **International Cooperation**: In 2021, NRCan and the U.S. DOE signed a Memorandum of Understanding for cooperation on new and updated energy efficiency and testing standards.
*   **U.S. Regulatory Actions**:
    *   **March 12, 2024**: U.S. DOE published a Direct Final Rule for energy efficiency standards for household clothes dryers, including vented gas clothes dryers.
    *   **October 8, 2021**: U.S. DOE published a Final Rule updating testing standards for household clothes dryers.
*   **NRCan's Consideration**: NRCan is considering amending the Regulations to include household vented gas clothes dryers, with harmonized energy efficiency standards, testing standards, and manufacturing dates with the United States.
*   **Scope Extension**: The requirements under consideration would also apply to the clothes dryer component of integrated consumer clothes washer-dryers.

## Product Description

*   **Current Definition**: A clothes dryer is defined as a household tumble-type clothes dryer that is electrically operated.
*   **Proposed Expanded Definition**: A **clothes dryer** means a household tumble-type clothes dryer with forced air circulation. The heat source is electricity or gas, and the drum and blower(s) are driven by an electric motor(s).
*   **Types**: Vented gas clothes dryers exhaust evaporated moisture, while ventless dryers use a closed-loop system with an internal condenser.
*   **Size Categories (based on drum capacity)**:
    *   **Compact**: less than **125 litres (4.4 cubic feet)**
    *   **Standard**: **125 litres (4.4 cubic feet)** or more

### Testing Standard

NRCan is considering referencing the following testing standard for household vented gas clothes dryers, using ambulatory incorporation by reference (as amended from time to time). This standard is already referenced for household electric clothes dryers:

*   **Appendix D2 to Subpart B, Part 430 of Title 10** to the U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, entitled *Uniform Test Method for Measuring the Energy Consumption of Clothes Dryers* (10 C.F.R. Appendix D2).

### Energy Efficiency Standard

The intent is to harmonize with the recent U.S. DOE Direct Final Rule. The energy efficiency standards under consideration are:

*   A minimum **Combined Energy Factor (CEF) of 1.58 kg/kWh (3.48 lb/kWh)** for vented gas clothes dryers with a capacity of **125 L (4.4 cubic feet)** or more, when tested in accordance with 10 C.F.R. Appendix D2.
*   A minimum **Combined Energy Factor (CEF) of 0.92 kg/kWh (2.02 lb/kWh)** for vented gas clothes dryers with a capacity of less than **125 L (4.4 cubic feet)**, when tested in accordance with 10 C.F.R. Appendix D2.

NRCan is considering incorporating the U.S. Code of Federal Regulations by reference, as amended from time to time.

## Important Dates

*   **Coming into Force**: Modifications to the Regulations would come into force **six months** after the date of publication in the *Canada Gazette*, Part II.
*   **Application Date**: NRCan is considering applying the energy efficiency and testing standards to household vented gas clothes dryers manufactured on or after **March 1, 2028**.
*   **Regulatory Pathway**: Governor in Council amendment, with pre-publication in the *Canada Gazette*, Part I, followed by a public comment period. Final publication is typically within a year of the initial public comment period.

## Labelling Requirements

*   No changes are being considered for existing labelling requirements for household clothes dryers.
*   An **EnerGuide label** must be attached to every new household clothes dryer subject to the Regulations.
*   Labelling would be a **new requirement** for household vented gas clothes dryers.

## Size Categories (Administrative Change)

*   NRCan is considering **removing the size categories** from the Regulations.
*   For EnerGuide labels, the size categories would remain in NRCans EnerGuide label guidelines.
*   This is an **administrative change** and has no impact on the scope of the Regulations.

## Verification Requirements

*   No changes are being considered for energy efficiency verification requirements for household clothes dryers.
*   Household clothes dryers will continue to carry a **third-party verification mark**.
*   The verification mark would be a **new requirement** for household vented gas clothes dryers.
*   The verification mark must be from a Standards Council of Canada accredited certification body operating an energy efficiency certification program for the product.

## Reporting Requirements

### Energy Efficiency Report

The energy efficiency report for household gas clothes dryers would include the following information:

*   **Capacity**, in L, of clothes dryers drum
*   **Hourly BTU rating** of the burner
*   **Combined Energy Factor** when tested according to Appendix D2 (CEFD2)
*   **Annual energy consumption**

This report must be submitted by the dealer to NRCan **before** the product is imported into Canada or traded inter-provincially for the first time.

### Import Report

A dealer who imports household vented gas clothes dryers into Canada would be required to complete an import report, similar to other regulated clothes dryers.
