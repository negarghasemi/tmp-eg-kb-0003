## Upgrades requiring a home energy assessment

*Heat pump water heaters, insulation, windows/doors and air sealing*

### Which upgrades require a home energy assessment?
Heat pump water heaters, windows, doors, insulation (attic, wall, foundation and exposed floor) and air sealing require a home energy assessment before beginning any work. You must install at least two of these upgrades to qualify for rebates.

### Why do I need to install at least two upgrades?
Your home works as a system. A home energy assessment will help you identify the best energy-efficiency solutions for your home. We recommend following your registered energy advisors advice on which upgrades will most increase the energy efficiency and comfort of your home.

### What is a home energy assessment and how long does it take?
A home energy assessment can help you understand how your home currently uses energy and identify upgrades to help improve its energy efficiency. A registered energy advisor will assess your home from basement to attic. The assessment will give you an [EnerGuide rating](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/homes/what-energy-efficient-home/welcome-my-energuide/16654) for your home and an energy-efficiency report. An initial home energy assessment takes about **two hours**. A follow-up home energy assessment takes about **an hour**.

### Do I have to pay for the home energy assessment?
Homeowners are responsible for the upfront cost of the initial and follow-up home energy assessments. To offset the cost, you will receive a **$600 rebate** after installing at least two recommended upgrades.

### Why do you work with licensed service organizations to deliver the program?
We work with trusted organizations across Ontario that are licensed with [Natural Resources Canada (NRCan)](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/homes/find-service-provider/find-service-organizations-for-existing-homes/23772). They send NRCan-registered energy advisors to complete home energy assessments.

### Do I need to hire a professional contractor to install my upgrades?
**Air sealing** can be a do-it-yourself approach (minimum air change rate targets are required). However, you must hire a professional contractor for installing new **windows, a heat pump water heater, or adding insulation**.

### I already installed my upgrades or started installation. Am I eligible for the bundled rebates?
**No**. An initial home energy assessment is required *before* starting installation for these upgrades. However, you may be eligible for other upgrades that do not require a home energy assessment (heat pump, solar panels, smart thermostat).

### I already participated in HER, HER+ or other Enbridge Gas or Save on Energy programs. Can I participate in this program?
**Yes**. You may participate provided you install *different* energy-saving upgrades for which you previously received a rebate. *For example, if you replaced windows and received a rebate under the HER+ program, then you will not be eligible to also receive a rebate under this program for the same replacement windows.*

### When will I receive my rebate?
After your follow-up home energy assessment is completed and your registered energy advisor (REA) submits all required documents, your cheque should arrive by mail within **60 days**.

### Why are Enbridge Gas and Save on Energy partnering to deliver this program?
They offer a range of energy conservation programs. By teaming up, they aim to reduce energy use and costs, and make homes more comfortable, whether you heat with natural gas or electricity.

### I have multiple homes. Can I participate in the program multiple times, even if not all homes are my primary residence?
**Yes**. You are able to participate in the program for each property you own.

### Can I apply for the Canada Greener Homes Loan and still be eligible for rebates through this program?
**Yes**. You may be eligible for federal loan funding and rebates together. The Canada Greener Homes Loan is a separate application. More information can be found on the [Canada Greener Homes Loan website](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/home-energy-efficiency/canada-greener-homes-initiative/canada-greener-homes-loan).

### Who do I contact if I have questions about the program and what I qualify for?
Please reach out to a **registered energy advisor**.

### Do Save on Energy or Enbridge Gas solicit participation for the program by phone or door to door?
**No**. Please refer to their [Scams page](https://www.enbridgegas.com/ontario/contact-us/scam-awareness) for more information. If you receive such a call or visit, contact: [saveonenergy@ieso.ca](mailto:saveonenergy@ieso.ca).

### Why are some oil, propane, wood or electrically heated homes not eligible for the Home Renovation Savings program?
To be eligible, these homes must be connected to the IESO-controlled grid via a local electricity utility or distribution company in Ontario. Cornwall Electric customers are not eligible unless converting to/from Enbridge natural gas for home heating.

### I live in a more remote area and am having trouble finding a Service Organization that will come to my home. Can I still participate?
**Yes**. If you have difficulty, contact [info@homerenovationsavings.ca](mailto:info@homerenovationsavings.ca) and the program team will help.

### What is the Multi-Upgrade Bonus?
Participants with a post-renovation assessment date on or after **January 5, 2026** who install **three (3) or more qualified upgrades** under the Assessment program will receive a **$500 bonus rebate**.

### What counts as one (1) qualified upgrade?
Each of the following counts as one qualified upgrade, provided it meets eligibility criteria:

- Air Sealing
- Attic/Cathedral
- Basement Wall/Crawl Space Wall/Foundation Header
- Basement Slab
- Domestic Hot Water Heat Pump
- Exposed Floor
- Exterior Wall
- Crawl Space Ceiling
- Windows/Doors (including skylights and sliding doors)

**Clarifications:**
- **Attic/Cathedral:**
  - Attic only = **1** qualified upgrade
  - Cathedral only = **1** qualified upgrade
  - Attic + Cathedral combined = **1** qualified upgrade
- **Basement Wall/Crawl Space Wall/Foundation Header:**
  - Basement Wall only = **1** qualified upgrade
  - Crawl Space Wall only = **1** qualified upgrade
  - Foundation Header only = *not* a qualified upgrade
  - Any combination of Basement Wall, Crawl Space Wall, Foundation Header = **1** qualified upgrade
- **Windows/Doors (including skylights and sliding doors):**
  - All upgrades combined = **1** qualified upgrade

## Appliances

*Available for appliances*

### How can I apply for a rebate?
1. Purchase an eligible appliance from any retailer.
2. Apply for your rebate online, uploading your receipt within **60 days** of purchase.
3. Install your new appliance and recycle/dispose of your old one.

### What is the purpose of this rebate program?
To encourage energy efficiency by offering financial incentives for purchasing qualified appliances that use less energy, saving on electricity bills and reducing environmental impact.

### Who is eligible to apply for a rebate?
- Residential customer connected to the IESO-grid by a local distribution company (LDC).
- Purchase and install the appliance at a residence connected to the IESO-grid.
- Submit the rebate application within **60 days** of purchase.

### How do I know what appliances qualify?
Qualifying fridges, freezers, heat pump clothes dryers, clothes washers, and induction cooktops/ranges are eligible. Always check the list of eligible models or visit the [ENERGY STAR Most Efficient website](https://www.energystar.gov/products/most_efficient). *Induction stovetops only need to be ENERGY STAR certified.*

### Can I receive multiple rebates?
**Yes**, up to **two rebates per appliance category per home**.

### How long does it take to receive my rebate?
Typically **4-8 weeks** from a complete application. Delays may occur if information is missing.

### Do used or refurbished appliances qualify?
**No**. Only new, unused qualified appliances are eligible.

### What is ENERGY STAR?
The internationally recognized mark of high efficiency for products, homes, and buildings. The **ENERGY STAR Most Efficient** designation recognizes the most efficient ENERGY STAR certified products. Learn more at [ENERGY STAR Canada's website](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/energy-star-canada).

### Why are only select appliances eligible?
The program focuses on top-performing, most energy-efficient products to maximize energy savings and provide the highest return on investment.

### Why are the incentives set at these amounts?
Incentive amounts aim to bridge the cost gap between eligible ENERGY STAR (for induction stoves) or ENERGY STAR Most Efficient appliances and average appliances.

## Attic insulation

**Yes**, as long as all eligibility criteria are met for each application. Where there are tenants, the owner must apply.

### What if I am a tenant, can I participate in the program?
**No**, only the owner of the property is permitted to participate.

### Can I receive additional rebates from other programs for attic insulation (attic/cathedral ceiling/flat roof)?
You can stack incentives from other funding organizations with some limitations. You **may not stack** with other incentives from Enbridge Gas, IESO's Save on Energy, or the Government of Ontario.

## Heat pumps

*Cold climate air source heat pumps and ground source heat pumps*

### What are the eligibility requirements to receive a heat pump rebate?
- **Heating Source:** Enbridge Gas customer primarily heating with natural gas furnace/boiler **OR** connected to Ontario electricity grid and heating with electricity, oil, propane, or wood. *Cornwall Electric customers are not eligible unless primarily heated by Enbridge Gas natural gas.*
- **Home Type:** Single detached, semi-detached, row house, townhome, or mobile home on a permanent foundation. **New builds do not qualify** (must achieve at least six months of occupancy).
- **Ownership:** Must own the home and be responsible for purchase/installation. Owner must apply if there are tenants.
- **First-time Installation:** Must be the first-time installation of a space heating heat pump system. Existing heat pump systems are not eligible.
- **Qualified Products:** Installed heat pump must be on Natural Resources Canadas qualified products list (cold climate air source or ground source).
- **Backup Heating:** Can work standalone or with backup (natural gas furnace, electric resistance). Backup system not eligible for rebate.
- **Natural Gas/Propane Heated Homes:** New or existing furnace can be paired with the heat pump.
- **Electrically Heated Homes:** Existing electrical backup can be kept.
- **Mini/Multi-split Units:** Both indoor and outdoor units must be installed. Replacement of one component or adding a new indoor unit is not eligible.
- **Oil Heated Homes:** Required to size electric heating system (heat pump and electric resistance backup, if needed) to fully offset oil heating load.

*For full eligibility, visit the [Terms and Conditions page](/terms-and-conditions).*

### Can I work with any contractor to complete the installation of my heat pump?
You must work with a **participating contractor**. Fill out [this form](/without-assessment/heat-pumps/heat-pump-form) to receive a list. The contractor must be registered and listed *before* installation.

### Where can I find more information about heat pumps?
[Natural Resources Canada](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/heat-pumps/heat-pump-basics/25589) has information.

### Can I receive additional rebates from other programs for the installation of my heat pump?
You can stack incentives from other funding organizations with some limitations. You **may not stack** with other incentives from Enbridge Gas, IESO's Save on Energy, or the Government of Ontario. *Total incentives must not exceed **100%** of the costs to supply and install the heat pump.*

### I have multiple homes. Can I participate in the program multiple times, even if not all homes are my primary residence?
**Yes**, for each property you own, provided all eligibility criteria are met.

### Im a tenant. Can I participate?
**No**. You must own the home.

### Do Save on Energy or Enbridge Gas solicit participation for the program by phone or door to door?
**No**. Refer to their [Scams page](https://www.enbridgegas.com/ontario/contact-us/scam-awareness). Contact: [saveonenergy@ieso.ca](mailto:saveonenergy@ieso.ca).

### Why are some electricity customers not eligible for the Home Renovation Savings program?
If heating with electricity, you must be connected to Ontarios electricity grid. Cornwall Electric customers are connected to the Hydro-Quebc grid and are only eligible if they are Enbridge Gas customers.

### When will I receive my rebate?
After installation and post-installation application approval, your cheque should arrive by mail within **60 days**.

### How is the heat pump incentive determined?

| Baseline Heating Fuel         | Heat Pump Measures     | Eligible Measure Rebate | Qualifiers   |
| :---------------------------- | :--------------------- | :---------------------- | :----------- |
| Electricity/oil/propane/wood  | ccASHP Full Equipment  | $1,250/ton*             | Up to $7,500 |
| Electricity/oil/propane/wood  | GSHP Full System       | $2,000/ton*             | Up to $12,000 |
| Enbridge Natural Gas          | ccASHP Full Equipment  | $500/ton*               | Up to $2,000 |
| Enbridge Natural Gas          | GSHP Full System       | $3,000                  | flat rate    |
| Rental                        | ccASHP Full Equipment  | $500/ton*               | Up to $2,000 |
| Rental                        | GSHP Full System       | $3,000                  | flat rate    |

**\*Incentives for ccASHPs** will be calculated based on the total rated heating capacity at 8.3 (47). **Incentives for GSHP** will be calculated based on closed loop heating capacity (electric customers only).

### Do I need to remove my existing oil heating system to receive a heat pump rebate?
**No**. You are not required to remove your existing oil heating system, but must transition to an electric heat pump. If required by law, work with a TSSA registered contractor to empty/remove oil storage tanks and piping.

### What if my home is primarily heated with natural gas but I am not an Enbridge Gas customer?
At this time, you are **not eligible**.

### Am I eligible if I heat my home with a natural gas fireplace?
**No**, fireplaces are not considered a primary space heating type.

### Does a home that is replacing a very old heat pump system qualify?
**No**. Heat pump replacements are not eligible.

### What is the rebate amount for 1/2 ton sizes?
Incentives are calculated based on the **unrounded heating tonnage** of the heat pump, multiplied by the $/ton incentive amount.

*Example for ccASHPs (minimum rated heating capacity at 8.3C of 3.2 tons):*
- **$1,600** for an Enbridge natural gas heated home ($500 x 3.2)
- **$4,000** for an oil, propane, wood or electrically heated home ($1250 x 3.2)

*Example for GSHPs (full system):*
- **$3,000** for an Enbridge natural gas heated home (flat rate)
- **$2,000 per ton** for an oil, propane, wood or electrically heated home (calculated per closed loop heating ton)

### How do I become a participating contractor?
**Contractor registrations are currently paused; no new applications are being accepted.** Check back for updates.

### Why was I selected for a Quality Assurance audit?
To maintain high standards, a portion of heat pump installations are randomly chosen for Quality Assurance (QA) audits by third-party evaluators (Ecolighten Energy Solutions and Bowser Technical).

## Solar panels and battery storage

*Rooftop solar systems with or without battery*

### What are the eligibility requirements to receive solar PV and battery storage rebates?
- **Home Type:** Single/semi-detached home or row/townhome.
- **Ownership:** Must own the home and be responsible for purchase/installation. Owner must apply if there are tenants.
- **Electricity Grid:** Home must be connected to Ontarios electricity grid. *Cornwall Electric customers are not eligible.*
- **Permissions/Permits:** All applicable local municipality, LDC, and regulatory body permissions/permits must be completed (Connection Assessment, ESA certificate).
- **Installation Location:** Solar PV panels must be installed on a **rooftop**. Portable or temporary structures are not eligible.
- **Load Displacement:** Solar PV panels must be sized for **load displacement only**. Net metering is not permitted.
- **Battery Storage:** Battery storage systems must be paired with a **new rooftop solar PV panel system**.
- **Equipment:** All equipment must be new, owned by the participant, and purchased in Canada.
- **Standards:** All equipment must be compliant with applicable ANSI/CAN/UL/CSA/IEEE/CEC standards.

### Do I need a home energy assessment to be eligible for solar PV and battery storage rebates?
**No**. A home energy assessment is not required.

### Can I receive a Home Renovation Savings program rebate for my solar PV project and participate in a net-metering agreement?
**No**. Participants who receive an incentive for solar PV and battery storage projects are **not eligible** to participate in a net-metering agreement. These incentives are for load displacement only. You must choose between the program incentive or net-metering.

### Are program participants able to apply for net-metering in the future?
**No**. Participants who receive an incentive for a solar PV system are required to operate the system for load displacement purposes for the life of the system.

### Why is net metering not permitted for solar PV projects that receive a rebate under the Home Renovation Savings program?
The directive from the Minister of Energy and Electrification excludes measures promoted through other programs or initiatives, meaning Home Renovation Savings participants are not eligible for net metering agreements.

### Can I receive rebates for replacing or adding to an existing solar PV or battery storage system?
**No**. The rebate encourages new installations.

### Are solar PV panel rebates allocated in $1,000 increments or a lower amount?
Solar PV panel rebates are calculated at **$1,000 per kilowatt of generation**. They will **not be rounded** to the nearest $1,000. *Example: a 4.84 kW-DC system may receive up to $4,840.*

### Why cant I install a standalone battery storage system?
A standalone battery system charges from the electricity grid. The program aims to reduce energy use and demand on the grid, so battery storage must be paired with solar PV panels.

### Are new builds eligible for solar PV and battery storage rebates?
**Yes**, but must be occupied when submitting the pre-installation application.

### Can I apply more than once, for example, if I have more than one home or move homes? Does the program apply to non-primary homes (i.e., cottages)?
**Yes**. A homeowner can apply more than once. Both primary and non-primary homes are eligible.

### Can I apply for a rebate if I currently have a microFIT project?
**No**. The rebate is for new solar PV and battery storage installations. Expansions to existing systems are not eligible.

### Can I receive additional rebates from other programs for the installation of my solar PV and battery storage project?
You can stack incentives from other funding organizations with some limitations. You **may not stack** with other incentives from Enbridge Gas, IESO's Save on Energy, or the Government of Ontario. *Total incentives must not exceed **100%** of the costs to purchase and install the solar PV and battery storage project.*

### Can I apply for the Canada Greener Homes Loan and still be eligible for rebates through this program?
**Yes**. You may be eligible for federal loan funding and rebates together. The Canada Greener Homes Loan is a separate application. More information can be found on the [Canada Greener Homes Loan website](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/home-energy-efficiency/canada-greener-homes-initiative/canada-greener-homes-loan).

### Is third-party ownership of the solar PV panels and battery storage equipment permitted?
**No**. The program participant must be responsible for paying for the purchase, installation, and ownership.

### Im interested in solar PV panels and battery storage for my off-grid home. Can I receive rebates?
**No**. Your home must be connected to the Ontario electricity grid and distribution system.

### Why are some electricity customers not eligible for the Home Renovation Savings program?
To be eligible, electricity customers must be connected to Ontarios electricity grid. Customers of Cornwall Electric are connected to the Hydro-Qubec electricity grid and are therefore not eligible.

### How big can the solar PV array be?
For load displacement with no net-metering, the size should ideally match the homes typical energy consumption. Consult your LDC for technical requirements.

### What is the typical size of a solar PV project applying for the rebate?
The typical size for load displacement ranges from **5 to 10 kW**, but varies by home load. When paired with storage, the system should primarily be for normal operation, not just backup power.

### Are there any restrictions on where the solar PV systems can be installed?
Must be installed on the **rooftop** of the Eligible Home or a permanent existing structure (e.g., detached garages) connected to the hydro meter. **Ground-mounted solar is not eligible**.

### Why are ground-mounted solar PV systems excluded?
Limiting to rooftop installations addresses concerns about oversizing systems and reduces administrative burden, aligning with the goal of offsetting own consumption.

### When should I install my solar PV and battery storage system?
**Do not purchase or install prior to receiving written pre-approval** from the program team and approval from the relevant LDC.

*If planning other upgrades (hot tub, heat pump, EV charger) that increase energy consumption, complete those upgrades first before sizing solar panels.*

### Can I work with any contractor to complete the installation of the solar PV and battery storage system?
**Yes**, you may work with the contractor of your choice. They must submit a [pre-installation form](/without-assessment/solar/solar-contractor) on your behalf to verify eligibility.

### Can I work with more than one contractor (i.e., a contractor to install solar PV panels and another for battery storage)?
**Yes**. Contact [solar@homerenovationsavings.ca](mailto:solar@homerenovationsavings.ca) for adjusted application process details.

### Where can I find a list of qualified solar PV and battery storage contractors?
The program does not have a list or endorse contractors. Refer to the [Canadian Renewable Energy Association (CanREA) member directory](https://renewablesassociation.ca/member-directory/).

### What is the expected turnaround time for applications?
Applicants can expect to be contacted within a **week** of submitting their pre-installation application.

### Are program participants allowed to inject load back onto the grid and are participants required to install protections to ensure load is not being injected?
The IESO expects the LDC to manage technical connection requirements. Consult your LDC.

### Do Save on Energy or Enbridge Gas solicit participation for the program by phone or door to door?
**No**. Refer to their [Scams page](https://www.enbridgegas.com/ontario/contact-us/scam-awareness). Contact: [saveonenergy@ieso.ca](mailto:saveonenergy@ieso.ca).

## Smart thermostats

*Get $100 off smart thermostats before or after you buy*

### What are the eligibility requirements to receive a smart thermostat rebate?
- **Heating/Cooling Source:** Enbridge Gas customer primarily heating with natural gas furnace/boiler **OR** connected to Ontario electricity grid and heating/cooling electrically. *Cornwall Electric customers are not eligible.*
- **Previous Rebates:** Must not have previously received a smart thermostat rebate, discount, or device from Enbridge Gas, Save on Energy, or the Home Renovation Savings program.
- **Home Type:** Single/semi-detached home, row/townhome, or mobile home on a permanent foundation.

### Whats the difference between the Before you buy rebate and the After you buy rebate?
- **Before you buy:** **$100 off** at the time of checkout from participating retailers.
- **After you buy:** **$100 Enbridge Gas bill credit** (for Enbridge Gas customers) or a **rebate cheque** (for electricity customers). Submit receipt within **60 days** of purchase.

### I already purchased my smart thermostat. Can I still receive a $100 rebate?
**Yes**. If purchased on or after **Jan. 28, 2025**, you can apply through the After you buy option within **60 days** of purchase.

### Where can I purchase my smart thermostat to receive a Before you buy rebate?
Apply online for a discount code, then redeem at participating retailers:

- [bestbuy.ca](https://www.bestbuy.ca/en-ca)
- Home Depot  **in-store purchase only**

### Where can I purchase my smart thermostat to receive an After you buy rebate?
From **any retailer** (online, in-store, or through a contractor). Upload your receipt showing proof of purchase.

### Can I receive a smart thermostat Before you buy rebate from Home Depots website?
**No**. The Before you buy code cannot be redeemed on homedepot.ca. For instant savings, purchase in-store. To purchase online at homedepot.ca, you can apply for the After you buy rebate.

### Can I get both a Before you buy and After you buy smart thermostat rebate?
**No**. You can only receive one type of smart thermostat rebate.

### Can I receive more than one smart thermostat rebate?
**No**. Only one smart thermostat rebate is available per household per lifetime.

### If I have an unredeemed Before you buy rebate code, can I receive an After you buy smart thermostat rebate?

### Can I buy a used or refurbished smart thermostat and receive a rebate?
**No**. Used, refurbished, and open-box smart thermostats are not eligible.

### When will I know if my After you buy smart thermostat rebate application was approved?
You should receive an email response within **two weeks**.

### When will I receive my After you buy smart thermostat rebate?
Paid as a credit on your Enbridge Gas bill or by cheque within up to **60 days** from application approval.

### Im an Enbridge Gas customer. Why are my account number and address not eligible?
- Must have an active Enbridge Gas account.
- Live in an eligible home type.
- If new customer or recently moved, wait 2-3 weeks for system update.
- If you previously received a smart thermostat rebate at your current address, you are ineligible.

### What happens if my After you buy smart thermostat rebate application is denied?
Youll receive an email within **two weeks**. Denial reasons may include:
- Device not purchased within **60 days** of application.
- Cost of device less than total rebate amount.
- Used, refurbished, or open-box device.
- Device not a qualifying model.
- Receipt did not show thermostat cost as its own line item.

### I received a free smart thermostat as part of an HVAC package purchase. Am I eligible for a rebate?
**No**. Free devices are not eligible. Your receipt must clearly show the amount paid for the smart thermostat, and the cost must be more than the rebate amount.

### Will anyone but me control my smart thermostat if I participate in the program?
**No**. Enbridge Gas and Save on Energy do not have access to your smart thermostat settings. However, if you consent and enroll in [Peak Perks program](https://saveonenergy.ca/en/For-Your-Home/Peak-Perks), administrators may control it during peak energy demand (June-September) for a reward.

### Can I receive a smart thermostat rebate if I participate in this program and the Peak Perks program?
**Yes**. This program and [Peak Perks program](https://saveonenergy.ca/en/For-Your-Home/Peak-Perks) are designed to complement each other.

### Can I receive an After you buy rebate if I purchased my thermostat before Jan. 28, 2025?
The After you buy rebate is only for purchases on or after **Jan. 28, 2025**. You must apply within **60 days** of purchase.

### Why are some electricity customers not eligible for the Home Renovation Savings program?
To be eligible, electricity customers must be connected to Ontarios electricity grid. Customers of Cornwall Electric are connected to the Hydro-Qubec electricity grid and are therefore not eligible.
