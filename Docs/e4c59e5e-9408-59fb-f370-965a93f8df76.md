# Pool heaters - Energy Efficiency Regulations

*May 2024*

## Technical Bulletin on Developing the Standards

Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) is updating its Forward Regulatory Plan to amend Canadas *Energy Efficiency Regulations* to introduce **pool heaters** as a new energy-using product. This includes new energy efficiency and testing standards, harmonizing with the United States.

**Webinar**: Thursday, May 30, 2024, 1:00-2:15 pm EDT

Input from this consultation process will inform the development of future amendments. NRCan will conduct a cost-benefit analysis to assess economic and environmental impacts.

## Background

Pool heaters are **not currently defined** as an energy-using product in the Regulations. Introducing efficiency standards aims to:

*   Save energy.
*   Reduce energy costs for Canadians.
*   Reduce energy-related greenhouse gas emissions.
*   Encourage efficient product designs.

NRCan is collaborating with the U.S. Department of Energy (U.S. DOE) on new and updated energy efficiency and testing standards.

*   **May 30, 2023**: U.S. DOE published a final rule introducing energy efficiency standards for electric pool heaters and increasing stringency for gas-fired pool heaters.
*   **January 6, 2015**: U.S. DOE published a final rule for a testing standard to evaluate pool heater energy performance.

NRCan is considering harmonizing with the U.S. DOE rule regarding scope, energy efficiency standards, testing standards, and manufacturing dates.

## Product Description

A **pool heater** is defined as an appliance designed for heating the water contained in a swimming pool.

### Testing Standard

NRCan is considering referencing the following technical standards document from the U.S. Code of Federal Regulations:

*   **Appendix P to subpart B, Part 430 of Title 10**: *Uniform Test Method for Measuring the Energy Consumption of Pool Heaters*.

### Energy Efficiency Standard

NRCan is considering referencing the following energy efficiency standards from the U.S. Code of Federal Regulations:

*   **Gas-fired pool heaters**: Minimum integrated thermal efficiency, in percent, is **84(Q<sub>IN</sub>+491)/(Q<sub>IN</sub>+2536)**
    *   Where **Q<sub>IN</sub>** is the certified input capacity of a gas-fired pool heater basic model, in Btu/h.
*   **Electric pool heaters**: Minimum integrated thermal efficiency, in percent, is **600PE/(PE+1619)**
    *   Where **PE** is the certified active electrical power of an electric pool heater, in Btu/h.

## Important Dates

*   **Effective Date**: Modifications to the Regulations would come into force **six months** after the date of publication in the *Canada Gazette*, Part II.
*   **Manufacturing Date for New Standards**: NRCan is considering applying the new standards to pool heaters manufactured on or after **May 30, 2028**.
*   **Regulatory Proposal Pre-publication**: The Government of Canada intends to pre-publish a regulatory proposal in the *Canada Gazette*, Part I, in **2025**.

### Regulatory Pathway

NRCan is considering amending the Regulations through a Governor in Council amendment. Changes will be pre-published in the *Canada Gazette*, Part I, followed by a public comment period. Final publication timing will depend on comments received.

## Labelling Requirements

NRCan is **not considering** labelling requirements for pool heaters.

## Verification Requirements

Pool heaters would be required to carry a **third-party verification mark** indicating compliance with energy efficiency standards and verification of reported information. The mark must be from a Standards Council of Canada (SCC) accredited certification body operating an energy efficiency certification program for the product.

## Reporting Requirements

### Energy Efficiency Report

This report must be submitted by the dealer to NRCan **before** the product is imported into Canada or traded inter-provincially for the first time. It would include:

*   **Name of product**: Pool heater
*   **Brand name**
*   **Model number**
*   **Name of the manufacturer**
*   **Name of the certification body** whose verification mark would be affixed to the product
*   Whether a **mathematical model** was used to generate any product-specific information
*   **Type of pool heater**: natural gas fired, propane fired, electric resistance, or heat pump
*   Its **heating capacity**, in kW (btu/h)
*   Its **Integrated Thermal Efficiency**, in percent

### Import Report

A dealer importing these products into Canada would include the following information on the customs release document:

*   **Name of product**
*   **Model number**
*   **Brand name**
*   **Address of the dealer** importing the product
*   **Purpose for which the product is being imported** (e.g., for sale/lease without modification, for sale/lease after modification, or as a component for export)

## Comments Invited

NRCan is specifically interested in feedback on:

*   Pool heating products manufactured within Canada.
*   Canada-specific sales or shipment numbers of pool heaters by type.
*   Quantity of pools installed in Canada and pools with heaters installed in Canada.

**Deadline for comments**: **September 13, 2024**.
