# Step 3. Retrofit your home

**Grant Status**: The Canada Greener Homes Grant is **closed**. December 31, 2025, was the last day to submit documents. Requests submitted by the deadline will continue to be processed.

## Retrofit Process Overview

The completed pre-retrofit EnerGuide evaluation, along with the EnerGuide label, Homeowner Information Sheet, and Renovation Upgrade Report, were used by homeowners to decide on recommended retrofits.

## Retrofit Eligibility Criteria

*   Only retrofit work done **after December 1, 2020**, qualified for a grant.
*   **Both a pre- and post-retrofit EnerGuide evaluation were required** for eligibility.
*   Evaluations completed before December 1, 2020, were not eligible.
*   Homeowners had to undertake **at least one retrofit that was both eligible and recommended** by their energy advisor to receive the grant contribution towards evaluations and the grant itself.

### Prioritization of Recommended Retrofits

Recommendations in the Renovation Upgrade Report were prioritized based on:

*   **Potential energy savings**
*   **Life expectancy** of home components
*   **Interactions** among home components
*   **Potential renovation plans**
*   **Cost**

## Considerations Before Starting Retrofits

### 1. Your home operates as a system

*   All elements (walls, roof, ventilation, heating/cooling, external environment, occupant activities) affect each other.
*   Interactions determine overall home performance (e.g., poor insulation/ventilation can negate new windows/doors).
*   _Explore the most common energy-efficiency retrofits_.

### 2. Some retrofits may not be eligible

*   The Canada Greener Homes Grant did not provide grants for all recommended retrofits.
*   Energy advisors might have recommended non-eligible retrofits for overall energy efficiency, indoor air quality, and comfort.
*   **Check eligible retrofits and grant amounts**: [Eligible retrofits and grant amounts](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/home-energy-efficiency/canada-greener-homes-initiative/eligible-retrofits-grant-amounts)
*   **Northern and off-grid communities**: [Grants for Canadian homeowners living in the North and off-grid communities](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/home-energy-efficiency/canada-greener-homes-initiative/grants-canadian-homeowners-living-north-grid-communities)

### 3. To receive a grant for eligible retrofits

*   **Proof of installer's qualifications** (province/territory issued) was highly recommended before starting.
*   **Eligible products**: For windows or heating systems, an eligible product from the list had to be chosen.
*   **Solar photovoltaic systems**: No national/provincial certifications exist. Contractors or DIY were permitted. Off-grid systems had to comply with local building and electrical codes.

### 4. Your budget plays a major role

*   Homeowners were encouraged to consider their investment capacity.
*   **Upfront costs**: Both EnerGuide evaluations and retrofits had to be paid upfront.
*   **Grant limits**: The grant may not fully cover expenses.
    *   Up to **$600** maximum contribution toward total cost of pre- and post-retrofit EnerGuide evaluations (if at least one eligible retrofit was completed).
    *   Between **$125 and $5,000** for eligible home retrofits.
*   **Loan program**: The loan program is now closed to new applicants. Information for existing applicants: [Canada Greener Homes Loan](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/home-energy-efficiency/canada-greener-homes-initiative/canada-greener-homes-loan)
*   _Find other affordable housing and financial support resources_.

### 5. Additional incentives

*   Retrofits might have been eligible for **provincial, territorial, municipal, or non-governmental organization incentives**.
*   Homeowners were encouraged to explore all available incentives to maximize savings, as other programs were considered when determining final grant eligibility.

## Document Requirements

Homeowners were required to document home retrofits and verify expenses, keeping all documents until **March 31, 2028**.

### Mandatory Documents

*   **Receipts** for the pre- and post-retrofit EnerGuide evaluation.
*   All **receipts and invoices**, **marked paid in full**, for products purchased and installation costs. These must include company information, invoice number/date, homeowner's name/address, work description, product eligibility information, cost, and proof of payment.

### Other Recommended Documents (if applicable)

*   For grid-connected solar photovoltaic systems: **Letter of approval or permission for interconnection** from local electrical/building authority.
*   **Attestation forms** (if applicable) confirming:
    *   Mechanical system installed by a trained and licensed professional.
    *   Ground source heat pump system installed according to CSA standards.
    *   Air source heat pump or cold climate air source heat pump system capable of distributing heat to the entire home.
    *   Off-grid systems designed and installed according to local building and electrical codes.

## Planning Your Retrofits

Whenever possible, it's recommended to hire licensed and trained professionals.

### Retrofits Requiring a Licensed and Trained Professional

*   Heat pumps
*   Heat pump water heaters
* Furnaces (northern and off-grid community residents only)
*   Boilers (northern and off-grid community residents only)

### Retrofits for which Professionals are Strongly Recommended (may be required in some jurisdictions)

*   Renewable energy (solar photovoltaic systems)
*   Air sealing
*   Home insulation
*   Windows and doors
*   Thermostats
*   Resiliency measures

### Personal Labour Costs

*   If you implement your own retrofits, **personal labour costs are not eligible for a grant**.

## Do Your Homework

Homeowners are responsible for choosing products/materials and obtaining necessary permits. If permits weren't required, homeowners and contractors were responsible for meeting relevant building codes and standards.

### Hiring a Contractor

*   **Compare contractors**: Get written quotes and contracts.
*   **Ask prospective contractors**: References, proof of license, compliance with codes/bylaws, permit requirements, product legislation compliance, Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS), team experience, challenges encountered, and protection measures for home and family.

### Before You Begin a Retrofit (General Steps)

*   Get estimates from local providers and schedule work.
*   Plan work and get correct permits.
*   Contact your home insurance provider regarding your policy.
*   Obtain proof of licensed professional's license (if applicable).
*   Purchase and install [eligible products](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/home-energy-efficiency/canada-greener-homes-initiative/eligible-products-home-retrofit).

### Doing It Yourself

*   **Health and safety**: Always keep health and safety in mind.
*   Use tools and products according to manufacturer directions.
*   Wear appropriate protective clothing and equipment.
*   Protect home and family from dust, debris, and contaminants.
*   Take precautions in areas with hazardous materials (vermin, mould, lead, asbestos, vermiculite insulation).
*   Purchase and install [eligible products](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/home-energy-efficiency/canada-greener-homes-initiative/eligible-products-home-retrofit).
*   **Personal labour costs are not eligible for the grant**.

### Choosing Products

*   **Purchased in Canada**: All products must be purchased in Canada. Online purchases are eligible only if from a Canadian online distributor.
*   **Product facts**: Get information about products you or your contractor intend to use.
*   **Eligible products**: For some retrofits, you must purchase and install products that are eligible for the grant.
*   **Installation techniques**: Learn and follow proper techniques.
*   **Canadian standards**: Ensure products meet Canadian standards (compliance mark/stamp, CCMC evaluation number).
*   **Health and safety**: Find out about health/safety issues, request MSDS, and follow safety guidelines.

#### Health and Safety Considerations

*   **Air sealing**: Renovations, including air sealing, can cause indoor air quality issues or excessive condensation if ventilation isn't adjusted. Seek advice from an energy advisor and qualified ventilation contractor. _Reference: NRCans Keeping the Heat In_.
*   **Urea-Formaldehyde-Based Foam Insulation (UFFI)**: Prohibited in Canada since 1980 due to formaldehyde gas release. _Reference: Health Canadas Canadian Prohibition of Urea Formaldehyde Foam Insulation_.
*   **Asbestos**: Used before 1990 for insulation and fireproofing. Breathing fibers can cause cancer. Exposure risk during renovation/demolition. _Reference: Health Canadas Health Risks of Asbestos_.
*   **Vermiculite insulation**: Some old vermiculite may contain asbestos. No risk if sealed, but hazardous if inhaled. Blower door tests may not be performed. _Reference: Health Canada asbestos information_.
*   **Radon**: Naturally occurring radioactive gas. Accumulates in enclosed spaces, posing health risks. _Reference: Health Canadas Read more about health risks and safety with radon_.
