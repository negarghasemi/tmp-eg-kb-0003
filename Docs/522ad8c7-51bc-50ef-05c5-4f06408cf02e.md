# Compliance using the Free Trade and Labour Mobility in Canada Act

For over 30 years, Natural Resources Canada (NRCan), industry, and provincial partners have worked to harmonize the energy efficiency regulatory regime in Canada. With the passing of the *Free Trade and Labour Mobility in Canada Act* (FTLMCA), this harmonization is gaining further ground.

## Goods Subject to the FTLMCA

Currently, s.4(1)(b) of the *Energy Efficiency Regulations, 2016* allows regulatees to submit provincial compliance as federal compliance only where the provincial requirement is equivalent or better. The FTLMCA will now allow provincial compliance to be accepted as federal compliance for any comparable requirements without restriction.

**Note:** The FTLMCA impacts products produced domestically and shipped interprovincially. Products imported to Canada must still comply with federal requirements without the FTLMCA compliance option.

FTLMCA will apply when an energy-using product **satisfies all of the following conditions**:

-   **Manufactured or assembled domestically**
-   **Shipped interprovincially for sale**
-   **Subject to both a federal and a provincial energy efficiency standard**
-   **Produced, used, or distributed in accordance with a comparable provincial standard**
-   **The province/territory it was manufactured in or the province/territory it is being shipped to has a comparable provincial standard**

NRCan will accept certified compliance with an applicable provincial standard as compliance with the federal standard. Regulatees can choose whether to utilize the FTLMCA compliance option or continue complying through the *Energy Efficiency Regulations, 2016* via the Compliance Regulatory Energy Efficiency Database (CREED).

## Verification Mark and Labelling Requirements

Products using the FTLMCA compliance pathway must still adhere to federal labelling requirements, including:

-   **Labelling in accordance with the Regulations** (e.g., EnerGuide labels, general service lamp labels, walk-in nameplates).
-   **Labelling with an energy efficiency verification mark**.

**Important:** Compliance with a provincial requirement will **not** allow for the use of the federal Energy Efficiency Verification (EEV) mark. Products using the FTLMCA compliance pathway **must not** bear the federal EEV mark; they will require a provincial verification mark in accordance with section 4(1)(b) of the Regulations.

## FTLMCA Reporting Requirements

Reporting for FTLMCA-authorized provincial compliance will **not** be facilitated through CREED. Regulatees must submit the necessary compliance documents directly to NRCan via email.

To take advantage of the FTLMCA compliance pathway, regulatees must:

-   Report regulated models via an email addressed to **complianceops-opsconformite@nrcan-rncan.gc.ca**.
-   The email subject line must be **FTLMCA Submission**.
-   Attach the completed **FTLMCA form**.
-   Attach the completed **FTLMCA attestation**.
-   Attach the required documents demonstrating compliance with provincial standards (see below).

### Information Required in the FTLMCA Form:

-   **Contact information** for the regulatee
-   **Product type**
-   **Manufacturer name**
-   **Manufacturer location**
-   **Brand name**
-   **Model number**
-   **Provinces/territories** that the product will be shipped to
-   **Name and contact information** of the certification organization or provincial authority issuing supporting documentation
-   **Energy efficiency number** (if any)

### Required Documents for Submission:

Regulatees must submit one of the following documents:

1.  A **certificate of conformity** or similar document stating the energy-using product (specifying brand and model) meets the applicable provincial standard(s), certified by a Standards Council of Canada-accredited certification body.
2.  A document stating the energy-using product (a) meets the applicable provincial standard(s) or (b) is an authorization to use the applicable provincial verification mark. This document must be received from or certified by the provincial ministry or agency that administers the provincial regulations.

**Timing:** The FTLMCA compliance option will apply on and after **January 1, 2026**. Interprovincial shipments of domestically produced products must occur on or after this date to use this option.
