# Grants for Canadian homeowners living in the North and off-grid communities

**Closed: Canada Greener Homes Grant.** _December 31, 2025, was the last day to submit your documents. Requests submitted by the deadline will continue to be processed._

Canadians in northern and off-grid communities faced specific challenges for home retrofits, including higher equipment and labour costs. All retrofit amounts were increased by an additional 30% for these communities. Homeowners in these areas were also eligible for replacements of existing fossil-fuel burning equipment. Northern community homeowners were eligible for additional insulation measures (attic, ceiling, and exposed floor insulation).

## Canada Greener Homes Initiative Offerings

*   **Grants from \$162.50 to \$5,000** for eligible and recommended home retrofits.
*   **Up to \$600** as a maximum contribution toward the total costs of pre- and post-retrofit EnerGuide evaluations (if the grant process, including at least one eligible retrofit, was completed).
*   **Interest-free loans of up to \$40,000** with a 10-year repayment term for major home retrofits.

The maximum grant amount a household could receive was **\$5,600** (up to \$5,000 for retrofits and up to \$600 for evaluations).

## Ineligible Retrofits

The Canada Greener Homes Initiative focused on improving the energy efficiency of *existing* homes. Additions to existing homes were not eligible for incentives. Some retrofits in a rebuild might also not be eligible depending on the extent of the rebuild. It was recommended to contact them for advice on eligibility before starting renovations.

### Retrofits that were not eligible:

*   Retrofits done before a pre-retrofit evaluation.
*   Retrofits done before December 1, 2020.
*   Products installed in new home additions built between the pre- and post-retrofit EnerGuide evaluations:
    *   Home insulation (the portion of new areas do not qualify).
    *   New windows and doors in new home additions do not qualify.
    *   Components requiring 100% upgrades do not qualify (e.g., crawl space wall and header insulation, exposed floor insulation).
*   Leased or rented equipment.
*   Air conditioners.
*   Tankless or storage water heaters (other than heat pump water heaters).
*   Skylights.
*   Roofs.
*   Windows and doors in a garage.

### Home Insulation

Properly insulating your home reduces heating and cooling costs and improves comfort. The effectiveness of insulation is measured by its R-value or RSI value. Higher values indicate slower heat transfer. Ensure selected insulation has a tested R-value supported by documentation.

**All products and equipment must be purchased in Canada.** Online purchases are eligible if ordered from a distributor in Canada.

#### Insulate your roof assemblies (attic/cathedral ceiling/flat roof)

*   **Eligibility criteria:**
    *   Add insulation to at least **20%** of the total area of your roof assembly.
    *   Increase insulation value (R-value or RSI) as per the table below.
    *   Additional insulation must be added to the same location as existing insulation at the time of pre-retrofit evaluation.
*   **Important:**
    *   Grant amount is pro-rated based on the percentage of the total roof assembly insulated.
    *   Maximum grant amounts reflect homes with only one type of roof assembly.
    *   Total grant for any combination of roof insulation cannot exceed **\$2,340**. For northern communities, up to **\$5,000** is available.

#### Basement Insulation

*   **Eligibility criteria:**
    *   Add insulation to at least **20%** of the total foundation wall area (includes basement walls, pony walls, crawl space walls).
    *   Seal and insulate a minimum **80%** of your entire basement header area.
    *   Seal and insulate a minimum **50%** of your entire basement slab area.
*   **Important:**
    *   Combined grants for basement walls, headers, and slabs are possible.
    *   Semi-detached or end unit row houses are eligible for a maximum of **75%** of listed amounts.
    *   Middle unit row houses are eligible for a maximum of **50%** of listed amounts.
    *   No grants for insulating walls and headers between adjacent units.
    *   When both a basement and crawl space are present, all applicable grants are pro-rated to a maximum of **\$1,950** based on total foundation wall area and insulation added.

### Air Sealing

Improving airtightness is a crucial retrofit. Your _Renovation Upgrade Report_ provides a target for air-tightness. Professional assistance is typically needed to achieve these levels.

| Options                                                                       | Grant Amount |
| :---------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------- |
| Meet the target in your _Renovation Upgrade Report_                           | \$715        |
| Reach 10% better than the target (0.9 x target value)                         | \$1,053      |
| Reach 20% better than the target (0.8 x target value)                         | \$1,300      |

_**Note:** Improving the building envelope or air sealing may lead to inadequate ventilation or increased radon levels. A balanced mechanical ventilation system (HRV/ERV) can help. Consult your energy advisor and a qualified ventilation contractor._

### Windows and Doors

Replacing windows and doors with high-performance ENERGY STAR certified models improves comfort, saves energy, and reduces carbon footprint.

*   **Eligibility criteria:**
    *   All equipment must be purchased in Canada (online purchases from Canadian distributors are eligible).
    *   Equipment must be on eligible product lists.
    *   Keep ENERGY STAR labels until after post-retrofit evaluation.
*   **Important:**
    *   New window/door and frame can be inserted into existing frame; glass, sash, or door-only replacements are not eligible.
    *   Each rough opening is eligible for a grant of up to **\$325**, to a maximum of **\$5,000**.

## Canada Greener Homes Initiative Programs

*   **Oil to Heat Pump Affordability program:** Helps low- to median-income homeowners switch from oil heating to an eligible electric heat pump system. Details vary by province and territory.
*   **Canada Greener Homes Affordability Program:** Helps low- to median-income homeowners and tenants reduce energy bills and greenhouse gas emissions through no-cost home retrofits, delivered through provincial and territorial partners, with additional support for Indigenous organizations.
