# Electric Motors

**Publication Date**: May 2024

## Technical Bulletin on Amending the Standards

**Webinar**: Wednesday June 26, 2024, 11:00-12:00 pm EDT

Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) is initiating a consultation process to amend Canadas *Energy Efficiency Regulations* for electric motors. This aims to harmonize Canadian standards with those in the United States Code of Federal Regulations. Input from stakeholders will inform the development of future amendments, and a cost-benefit analysis will be conducted to assess economic and environmental impacts.

## Background

Electric motors manufactured as of February 3, 1995; June 1, 2016; and June 1, 2017, are currently subject to Canadian Regulations. These standards contribute to energy savings, reduced energy costs, and lower greenhouse gas emissions.

In 2021, NRCan and the U.S. Department of Energy (U.S. DOE) signed a Memorandum of Understanding to collaborate on energy efficiency and testing standards.

Key U.S. DOE publications:
- **October 19, 2022**: Published updated testing standard, *Energy Conservation Program: Test Procedure for Electric Motors; Final rule*.
- **June 1, 2023**: Published *Energy Conservation Program: Energy Conservation Standards for Electric Motors, Direct Final Rule*.
- **September 29, 2023**: Confirmed the direct final rule, expanding the scope and strengthening energy efficiency standards for electric motors.

Changes introduced under the U.S. DOE Direct Final Rule include:
- **Medium electric motors, NEMA design A & B, 0.75-373 kW (1500 hp)**:
    1. 0.75-75 kW (1100 hp); NEMA premium efficiency level (IE3)
    2. 75-186 kW (100250 hp); super premium efficiency level (IE4)
    3. 186-373 kW (250500 hp); NEMA premium efficiency level (IE3)
- **Medium electric motors, NEMA design A & B, 374-559 kW (501750 hp)**; NEMA premium efficiency level (IE3)
- **Air over medium electric motors (standard frame size) 0.75-186 kW (1-250 hp)**:
    1. 0.75-75 kW (1100 hp); NEMA premium efficiency level (IE3)
    2. 75-186 kW (100250 hp); super premium efficiency level (IE4)
- **Air over polyphase electric motors (specialized frame size) 0.75-15 kW (1-20 hp)**

NRCan is considering harmonizing its Regulations with these updated scope, energy efficiency standards, testing standards, and compliance dates from the U.S. DOE.

## Product Description

### Current Definition of an Electric Motor (in Canadian Regulations)
An **electric motor** is defined as a machine that converts electrical power into rotational mechanical power, including any motor incorporated into another product, and that:
- is an electric three-phase induction design;
- is one of the following designs:
    1. a NEMA design A, B or C with a three- or four-digit NEMA frame number,
    2. an enclosed NEMA design A, B or C with a NEMA frame number of 56, or
    3. an IEC design N or H with an IEC frame number of 80 or above;
- has a nominal output power of not less than 0.75 kW (1 horsepower) and not more than 375 kW (500 horsepower);
- has a cage or squirrel-cage design;
- is rated for continuous duty or S1 operation;
- is designed to operate at a single speed;
- has a nominal voltage of not more than 600 V AC;
- has a nominal frequency of 50 Hz, 50/60 Hz or 60 Hz;
- has a two-, four-, six- or eight-pole construction;
- has an open or enclosed construction; and
- has an IP code from 00 to 67.

**Exclusions from current definition**:
- a motor that is cooled by air that is forced over the motor by a fan or blower that is not an integral part of the motor;
- a liquid-cooled motor;
- an inverter-only motor; or
- a motor that is designed to operate continuously only while immersed.

### Proposed Expanded Scope for Electric Motors
NRCan is considering expanding the scope to include:
- Medium electric motors, NEMA Design A & B, with a capacity of **374-559 kW (501750 hp)**
- Air over medium electric motors (standard frame size) with a capacity of **0.75-186 kW (1-250 hp)**, and
- Air over polyphase electric motors (specialized frame size) **0.75-15 kW (1-20 hp)** with certain frame sizes.

**Definitions for expanded scope**:
- *Standard frame size*: A motor frame size that aligns with specifications in NEMA MG 12016, section 13.2 for open motors, and NEMA MG 12016, section 13.3 for enclosed motors.
- *Specialized frame size*: An electric motor frame size for which the rated output power of the motor exceeds the motor frame size limits specified for standard frame size.

### NRCan's Proposed New Definition for Electric Motors
An **electric motor** would be defined as a machine that converts electrical power into rotational mechanical power, including any motor that is incorporated into another product, and that:
- is an electric three-phase induction design
- is built in a three-digit or four-digit NEMA frame size (or IEC metric equivalent), including those designs between two consecutive NEMA frame sizes (or IEC metric equivalent), or an enclosed 56 NEMA frame size (or IEC metric equivalent)
- is one of the following designs:
    - a NEMA design A, B or C
    - an IEC design N, NE, NEY, NY or H, HE, HEY, HY motor
- has a nominal output power of not less than **0.75 kW (1 HP)** and not more than **559 kW (750 HP)**
- has a cage or squirrel-cage design
- is rated for continuous duty or S1 operation
- is designed to operate at a single speed
- has a nominal voltage of not more than 600 V AC
- has a nominal frequency of 50 Hz, 50/60 Hz or 60 Hz
- has a two-, four-, six- or eight-pole construction
- has an open or enclosed construction

**Exclusions from proposed new definition**:
- liquid-cooled motor
- component sets of a motor
- inverter-only motor, or
- submersible motor (a motor that is designed to operate continuously only while immersed)

## Testing Standard
NRCan is considering incorporating by reference the U.S. DOE testing standard:
- **Appendix B to Subpart B, Part 431 of Title 10** to the U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, entitled *Uniform Test Method for Measuring the Efficiency of Electric motors*.

## Energy Efficiency Standard
NRCan is considering incorporating by reference the U.S. DOE energy efficiency standards:
- **Section 431.25 to Subpart B, Part 431 of Title 10** to the United States Code of Federal Regulations, entitled *Energy Conservation Standards and Effective Dates, Tables 8-10*.

## Important Dates
- **Coming into force**: Six months after publication in the *Canada Gazette*, Part II.
- **Considered requirements for electric motors apply**: On or after **June 1, 2027**.
- **Voluntary compliance**: Dealers may voluntarily comply upon publication in the *Canada Gazette*, Part II.

## Regulatory Pathway
- NRCan is considering amending the Regulations through **Governor in Council amendment**.
- Changes would be **pre-published in the *Canada Gazette*, Part I**, followed by a public comment period.
- Final publication in the *Canada Gazette*, Part II depends on comments received.

## Labelling Requirements
- NRCan is **not considering a mandatory EnerGuide labelling requirement** for electric motors.

## Verification Requirements
- NRCan is **not considering any changes** to verification requirements.
- Products will continue to carry a **third-party verification mark** from a Standards Council of Canada accredited certification body.

## Reporting Requirements
- **Energy efficiency report**: Minor changes are being considered to cover all motor frequencies (50 Hz, 50/60 Hz, and 60 Hz). This report must be submitted by the dealer to NRCan **before** the product is imported or traded inter-provincially for the first time.
- **Import report**: No change.

## Comments Invited
NRCan invites feedback on the following aspects:
- The period of manufacture and the early compliance option under consideration.
- The capability and capacity of certification bodies to verify electric motors with the U.S. DOE testing standard.
- The ability of manufacturers to procure a third-party energy efficiency verification within the specified timeline.
- Shipment and energy consumption data sets for electric motors.

Stakeholders are encouraged to provide comments by **September 13, 2024**.
