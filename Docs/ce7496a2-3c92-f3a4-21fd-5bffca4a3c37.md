# Electric Vehicles

At **Oakville Hydro**, we are committed to contributing to the sustainability of our community by educating our customers about Electric Vehicles (EVs).

## About Electric Vehicles

### Vehicle Types
There are two main types of EVs on the market:

*   **Battery Electric Vehicle (BEV)**:
    *   Powered solely by a rechargeable battery pack using an electric motor.
    *   Must be plugged in to recharge.
    *   Largest battery capacities among EV types.
    *   Produces **zero tailpipe emissions**.
    *   *Examples: Tesla Model S, Nissan Leaf, Mitsubishi I-Miev, Smart Fortwo, Ford Focus Electric.*

*   **Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicle (PHEV)**:
    *   Uses both a rechargeable battery pack with an electric motor and an internal combustion engine.
    *   The internal combustion engine is used in parallel or after the battery runs out of charge.
    *   *Examples: Chevrolet Volt, Toyota Prius plug-in, BMW i3, Ford C-Max Energi.*

### Charging Equipment
*Sometimes referred to as Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment (EVSE), this is the infrastructure that acts as the supply point to charge EVs.*

*   **Level 1 (120V  15A)**:
    *   Typically included with EV purchase.
    *   Connects to a normal 120V residential receptacle.
    *   **Longest recharge time**: typically **8 to 12 hours** depending on battery size.

*   **Level 2 (208 or 240V  30 to 100A)**:
    *   Requires a 208 or 240Vac supply with a higher current draw.
    *   Available as residential units and commercial charge stations.
    *   **Most popular type** for public and residential locations.
    *   **Typical charge time**: **3 to 6 hours**.

*   **Level 3 (DC Charge 208Vac input up to 120 kW DC output)**:
    *   A commercial charge unit.
    *   Can recharge a battery from **10% to 80% within 30 minutes** (*time varies depending on battery size and vehicle*).

### Environmental Benefits
*   Road transportation in Canada is a large contributor to greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions.
*   Ontarios electricity is mainly generated by hydroelectric and nuclear generation, leading to **substantial offset of carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases** by driving electric.
*   While EV lithium-ion batteries produce some GHG emissions during production, lifecycle analysis shows **significant reductions in emissions** when EVs are on the road compared with internal combustion engines.

### Financial Incentives
*   EVs may have a higher initial purchase price than traditional vehicles.
*   **Long-term financial savings** due to low-cost electricity fuel and higher fuel efficiency.
*   Electric motors convert over **77% of chemical energy** from batteries to power wheels, compared to **12%30%** for internal combustion engines using gasoline.
*   Check the cost comparison calculator on the [CAA website](https://www.caa.ca/maintenance/car-cost-calculator.html#ev_new%EF%BF%BD?%20target=) to understand potential savings.

### Government Incentives
*   The Ministry of Transportation (MTO) promotes EVs with **Ontario's Green Vehicle License Plate Program**.
*   This program allows EVs to use **HOV lanes** on major 400 series highways, even with only one person in the vehicle.
*   For more information, visit the [Ministry of Transportation website](http://www.mto.gov.on.ca/english/vehicles/electric/green-licence-plate.shtml).

### Installation Information (EV Charging Equipment)
*   **Determine required charging level**: Consider driving habits, EV battery size, and desired recharge time.
*   **Check wiring requirements**: See if the charging equipment needs a hardwired circuit or can use a 240V receptacle (like a dryer outlet).
*   **Electrical Panel Space**: If a hardwired circuit is needed (common for Level 2), ensure there is space for an additional breaker on your home's electrical panel.
*   **Panel Upgrade**: If no space is available, a panel upgrade may be required.
    *   Contact **Oakville Hydro** to check if your service size supports a panel upgrade: email [engineering@oakvillehydro.com](mailto:engineering@oakvillehydro.com) or call **905-825-9400, extension 2266**.
*   **Hire Licensed Contractor**: Always use a licensed electrical contractor for any EV charging equipment installation involving wiring to an electrical panel.
    *   Find available contractors on the [Electrical Safety Authority website](http://findacontractor.esasafe.com/).
