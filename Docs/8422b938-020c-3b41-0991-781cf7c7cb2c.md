# Oil to Heat Pump Affordability (OHPA) Program

This page provides information for applicants residing in participating provinces and territories regarding the Oil to Heat Pump Affordability (OHPA) program.

## Program Overview

The OHPA program assists eligible low-to-median income households in switching from oil heating to electric heat pump systems. This includes the purchase and installation of eligible heat pumps and additional related costs, such as the safe removal of oil tanks.

**Benefits of Switching:**
*   Save thousands of dollars annually on heating bills.
*   Reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

## Available Funding and Benefits

### Increased Funding

*   **Federal Funding:** Up to **$15,000**.
*   **Provincial/Territorial Match:** An additional up to **$5,000** is available from participating provinces and territories that have committed to matching the increased federal funding.

### No-Cost Energy-Saving Home Upgrades in Ontario

Eligible Ontario homeowners can receive a free direct installation of an energy-efficient heat pump system valued at up to **$25,000** (up to $15,000 federal funding and up to $10,000 provincial funding). This is facilitated through Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) and its co-delivery partner, the Independent Electricity System Operator (IESO).

*   **Important:** No funds will be paid directly to applicants in Ontario, except for a $250 bonus cheque.
*   **To Apply:** Visit [IESOs Save on Energy program](https://saveonenergy.ca/en/For-Your-Home/Energy-Affordability-Program).

### $250 Bonus Payment

A one-time payment of **$250** is offered to eligible homeowners in co-delivering provinces/territories. This bonus helps participants with the costs associated with switching from oil heating to an eligible electric heat pump.

*   **Eligibility:** Must sign up for the OHPA program through your province/territory and get pre-approved for an eligible heat pump system.
*   **Disbursement:** A $250 cheque will be mailed to the applicant once eligibility has been verified.

## Important Warnings

### Be Wary of High-Pressure Sales Tactics

NRCan has not authorized any third parties to make unsolicited contact with homeowners to register or participate in the Oil to Heat Pump Affordability program. The Government of Canada, NRCan, and its associated brands (ENERGY STAR, EnerGuide) will **never** solicit over the telephone, by email, or go door-to-door to inspect, sell, or rent heating and cooling products. EnerGuide home energy evaluations are performed only by licensed service organizations at the homeowner's request. [Learn how to recognize energy scams](/stories/spotlight-energy-efficiency/recognize-energy-scams).

### Third Parties May Not Apply on Behalf of Homeowners

Homeowners **must** apply to the OHPA program themselves. Third parties (including service organizations, energy advisors, and contractors) are not permitted to apply for the homeowner, or offer to apply, under any circumstances.
