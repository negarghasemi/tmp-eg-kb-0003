## Benefits

### Enjoy better indoor living
ENERGY STAR certified homes are generally **more air-sealed** than a typical new home, which helps to reduce **drafts and cold spots**, making the home more comfortable. It also helps to reduce dust, pollen, and pests. Features like energy-efficient windows and higher insulation levels contribute to more balanced temperatures. These homes often include devices that exchange indoor stale air with outdoor filtered air, improving indoor air quality.

### Save energy
An ENERGY STAR certified home is built to be about **20% more energy efficient** than a typical home. Annually, it saves the same amount of energy as watching **17 hockey seasons on TV** or running a refrigerator for **10 years**. Energy conservation is important as **17% of Canada's energy consumption** is used to operate homes, helping to protect the environment.

### Save money
Homeowners of an ENERGY STAR certified home can save up to **$300 per year** in utility costs. These savings are immediate and continuous, providing extra funds for personal goals.

### Have peace of mind about energy efficiency
ENERGY STAR certified homes are **evaluated, inspected, and labelled by a third-party energy advisor**. They are constructed by builders committed to higher energy-saving standards. This ensures the home meets the ENERGY STAR energy efficiency standard developed by Natural Resources Canada, which is an add-on to regional building codes to specifically improve energy efficiency.

### Help the planet
By operating an ENERGY STAR certified home and conserving energy, you save over **1,000 kilograms of greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions** annually. This is equivalent to the emissions from a **6,000-kilometre road trip** from Toronto to Calgary and back. Lower energy consumption conserves natural resources and protects the environment by reducing emissions contributing to climate change.

### Appreciate the resale potential
An energy-efficient home stands out in the market. Prospective buyers seek homes that offer comfort and cost savings. ENERGY STAR certified homes, with their high-performing windows and efficient heating/cooling systems, provide attractive features for potential buyers.

## Typical Features

### Higher insulation levels
Walls and ceilings are insulated beyond building code requirements, leading to more even temperatures throughout the home.

### Efficient heating and cooling systems
These systems help homeowners save energy and money annually.

### Additional air-sealing
ENERGY STAR certified homes are built to a specific energy target, ensuring they are sufficiently airtight to save energy, provide better comfort, and reduce drafts, cold spots, dust, pollen, pests, and noise.

### High performing windows, doors and skylights
Energy-efficient windows, doors, and skylights (such as those certified ENERGY STAR) help save energy and enhance comfort, especially in colder conditions.

### A whole-home ventilation system
An air-exchanger, such as a heat or energy recovery ventilation system, improves indoor air quality by expelling stale air and replacing it with filtered outdoor air.

### ENERGY STAR certified products
A variety of ENERGY STAR certified products (e.g., lighting, appliances, air conditioners) help save money by using less electricity.
