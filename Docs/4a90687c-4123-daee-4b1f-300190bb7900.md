# Deep Retrofits: Getting to Zero

## What are Net Zero and Net Zero Ready Buildings and Homes?

**Net Zero homes** are regular energy grid-tied homes that are so air-tight, well insulated, and energy efficient that they produce as much renewable energy as they consume over the course of a year. This balances out to zero, or Net Zero energy.

According to Natural Resources Canada, Net Zero homes are expected to be **80% more energy efficient** than new buildings constructed to today's building code minimum. They use on-site (or near-site) renewable energy systems to produce the energy they need.

*Provided by [NAIMA](https://www.naimacanada.ca/what-is-a-net-zero-home/)*

## Why Go Net Zero?

### Savings
Zero energy homes lead to **lowered energy and heating bills** and a carbon-free home. They are built to higher standards than conventional new homes, making them more durable with high-performance and better-insulated walls, roof, and overall envelope.

### Future Proof
The value of Net Zero homes is incredible. They are **future-proof** and built to stand the test of time.

### Comfort and Health
Net Zero homes combine advanced design and superior building systems with energy efficiency and on-site solar panels to produce a **better home**. They are ultra-comfortable, healthy, quiet, and sustainable, making them affordable to live in.

*Thanks to [NAIMA](https://www.naimacanada.ca/basics-of-net-zero-housing/) for Content*

## Key Components for Net Zero

*   ### Building Envelope
    Make sure the home is built or retrofitted with **high-quality, high R-Value insulation** and a **tight building envelope**. The building envelope needs to be highly efficient for the rest of the system to work.

*   ### Efficient Systems
    **High-efficiency HVAC systems** (e.g., Air Source Heat Pumps) and appliances help keep energy use down. Windows can be oriented to take advantage of warmth and light from the sun, known as passive heating.

*   ### Renewables
    Clean, renewable energy is the path forward for achieving a net zero building and reduces carbon emissions. A **Net Zero Ready house** has the ability to easily install renewables.

## Financial Help and Rebates Available to Get to Zero

### Canada Greener Homes Loan
*   The **Canada Greener Homes Loan** (Canada Mortgage and Housing Corp) offers up to **$40,000 in interest-free loans** for terms up to 10 years.
*   Eligible retrofits and application details are available on the [Natural Resource Canada](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/homes/canada-greener-homes-initiative/canada-greener-homes-loan/24286) site.

### Free or Low Cost Income-Qualified Programs
*   **Federal Oil to Heat Pump Affordability Program**
    *   Eligible Canadian homeowners can receive a payment of up to **$10,000** to switch from oil heating to a new energy-efficient heat pump.
    *   **Eligibility:** Home heated by oil, household income at or below median after-tax income level.
    *   **Eligible measures:** Electrical/mechanical upgrades for heat pump, installation of outdoor piping loops for ground source heat pump, safe oil tank removal, backup electrical heating, switching other oil-using systems (e.g., water heater).

*   **100% Free Cold Climate Air Source Heat Pumps (Save on Energy)**
    *   Offers free cold climate air source heat pumps to qualifying Ontario households with electrically heated homes.
    *   Includes a two-year free maintenance warranty and a 10-year equipment and labor warranty.
    *   May also include other free energy efficiency upgrades: professionally installed replacement appliances (ENERGY STAR refrigerators, freezers, window ACs, lighting), weatherstripping, attic/basement insulation, smart thermostats, power strips.

|*   **Home Winterproofing Program (HWP) | Enbridge Gas**
    *   Streamlined with the Energy Affordability Program for free home energy upgrades.
    *   If eligible, you'll receive free insulation, draft proofing, a smart thermostat, Energy Star appliances, and more.
    *   CCET is a community distribution partner with CLEAResult; contact them for a free Energy Saving Kit.

*   **Hydro One Low Income Energy Assistance Program (LEAP)**
    *   Pays up to **$1,000** of the amount owing on your bill (up to $1,200 for electrically heated homes).

*   **Save on Energy  Energy Affordability Program**
    *   Provides expert advice and energy-saving home upgrades completely free of charge for income-eligible households.
    *   Eligibility depends on household income/size or receipt of qualifying government/energy-support benefit.
    *   **Two Types of Support:** Comprehensive upgrades or Energy Saving Kits.

*   **Ontario Electricity Support Program (OESP)**
    *   Lowers electricity bills for lower-income households with a monthly credit based on income and household size.

### Rebates & Incentives
*   **Hydro One - Ontario Electricity Rebate (OER)**
    *   Provides eligible customers with a **13.1% reduction** on their bill before HST (as of November 1, 2024).
    *   **Automatically eligible:** Residential and Small Commercial customers using < 50 kW; Commercial accounts > 50 kW but using < 250,000 kWh annually.

*   **Enbridge Fixed Incentive Program**
    *   Helps offset the cost of upgrading to energy-efficient natural gas technology.

*   **Enbridge Business Programs and Incentives**
    *   Offers rebates and incentives for equipment upgrades, new construction, retrofits, and energy assessments.

*   **Home Renovation Savings Program** (Enbridge Gas and Save on Energy)
    *   Rebates available up to **$5,000** for various upgrades.
    *   **Two or More Upgrades (require Home Energy Assessment):**
        *   **Heat Pump Water Heaters:** $500
        *   **Windows and Doors:** $100 per rough opening
        *   **Insulation (attic wall, foundation, exposed floor):** Up to $8,900
        *   **Air Sealing:** Up to $250
    *   **One or More Upgrades (do not require Home Energy Assessment):**
        *   **Heat Pumps:** Up to $12,000
        *   **ASHP:** Up to $7,500
        *   **Solar Panels and Battery Storage:** Up to $10,000
        *   **Smart Thermostats:** $75
    *   **ASHP Installs Eligibility:** Must be an Enbridge Gas customer with an active account and primarily heat with natural gas furnace/boiler OR connected to Ontario electricity grid and heat electrically. Heat pump must be on Natural Resources Canadas qualified products list.

### Loans & Financing
*   **Canada Greener Homes Loan:** Up to $40,000, interest-free for up to 10 years.
*   **Clean Energy Loan (RBC Energy Saver Loan):** Available for RBC customers for hybrid cars, ENERGY STAR appliances, renewable energy projects. Offers 1% interest rate reduction or $100 rebate on a clean energy audit.
*   **Mortgage Insurance Loan Rebate:** 25% rebate for certified green homes (R-2000, ENERGY STAR, Built Green, LEED) from CMHC, Sagen, and Canada Guaranty.
*   **Canada Green Affordable Housing Program:** Funds up to 100% of eligible retrofit costs to a maximum of $170,000 per unit (forgivable loans of $85,000 per unit or 80% of eligible costs) for homeowners meeting affordability criteria, after pre-retrofit activities like energy audits.

## Steps to Net Zero

1.  **Consult with the Energy Coach**
    *   **Preparation:** Gather information on home size, utility, heating/cooling types, number of occupants, home age, budget/cost concerns.

2.  **Set Your Goals**
    *   Determine priorities and timeline for achieving net zero.

3.  **Book Your EnerGuide Audit**
    *   Required for financing and retrofit recommendations.
    *   Must be booked with a certified EnerGuide Auditor *before* starting any work to be covered by the Greener Homes Loan.
    *   [List of certified professional auditors available here](https://energytransform.ca/resources/what-is-energuide/).

4.  **Contact Your Utility Providers**
    *   Essential for understanding electrical infrastructure upgrades needed for systems like Air Source Heat Pumps (ASHP) and associated costs.

5.  **Contact Contractors**
    *   Seek contractors with specific Net Zero training (e.g., from [Ontario EAs list](https://www.chba.ca/who-to-work-with/)).
    *   CCET's [Resource page](https://energytransform.ca/resources/) lists professional organizations and contractor resources.

6.  **Determine Finance Options**
    *   Explore Federal and Provincial programs, loans, financing, and rebates on CCET's [Financial Help page](https://energytransform.ca/financial-help/).

7.  **Begin Saving**
    *   Initial upgrades and retrofits will immediately reduce monthly energy costs.
    *   Contact the [Energy Coach](https://energytransform.ca/home-energy-retrofits/connect-with-a-coach/) for more information.

## Handy at Home? Check out these DIY Tips!
*   **Water Heater:** Insulate older water heaters with an inexpensive blanket ($30-$40) for significant cost savings.
*   **Curtains:** Use heavy fabric curtains to reduce cold drafts from windows.
*   **Light Bulbs:** Switch to LED light bulbs for energy savings and longevity (up to 100,000 hours or 14 years).
*   **Cold Air:**
    *   Seal electrical outlets on outside walls with foam insulators to stop drafts.
    *   Use weatherstripping around doors and windows.
*   **Air Filters/Maintenance:**
    *   Regularly replace furnace filters per manufacturer's recommendations.
    *   Bleed hot water radiators at least once or twice a year for efficiency.
    *   Oil furnaces need annual maintenance; natural gas and propane furnaces need servicing every two years.
    *   Keep vents and air grills clear of objects and furniture.

## Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Canada has committed to **37% emissions reduction by 2030** and to achieve **net zero by 2050**.

*   Greenhouse gas emissions (GHG) from buildings account for **13% of Canada's total emissions**, making it the third-largest source.
*   **Peel Buildings emit 40%** of the total GHG emissions, and this number is increasing.
*   **Low-rise residential dwellings** contribute **20%** of Peel's GHG emissions.
*   **Electric heat pumps** can reduce carbon emissions by **35%** for the average home (Ontario Clean Air Alliance).
*   Replacing a failing gas furnace with heat pump systems for space and water heating can lead to an estimated **$10,320 in lifetime total savings** (capital plus energy) (Ontario Clean Air Alliance).
*   The region of Peel has **404,000 low-density dwellings**; the aim is an 80% emissions reduction from these by 2050, with Brampton and Caledon targeting all homes to be retrofitted by 2040.
