# Importer's Corner

When importing into Canada or shipping a regulated product between provinces, you need to be aware of the following reporting requirements:

-   **Energy Efficiency Reporting**
-   **Import Reporting**

## File an Energy Efficiency Report

Before importing or shipping an energy-using product between provinces, a dealer must file an energy efficiency report to Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) using the **Compliance Regulatory Energy Efficiency Database (CREED)**.

## Reporting Regulated Energy-Using Products

A dealer who imports a regulated energy-using product into Canada must, at the time of release, include specific information regarding the product and the purpose of its importation on the customs release document (e.g., customs or commercial invoice, bill of sale, price list).

For more information on Canada's Energy Efficiency Regulations, visit [Regulations and Standards](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/energy-efficiency-regulations/6845) or contact [importation@nrcan-rncan.gc.ca](mailto:importation@nrcan-rncan.gc.ca).

### Information Required on Customs Release Document

A dealer importing a regulated energy-using product into Canada must include the following information on the customs release document:

-   **Name of product** (_refer to the [Importers reminder card](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/energy-efficiency/energy-efficiency-regulations/importers-reminder-card)_)
-   **Model number**
    -   *For electric motors, the importer is encouraged to provide the model number instead of a Unique Model Identifier (UMI).*^\[1\]
-   **Brand name** (if any)
-   **Address of the dealer** who is importing the product
-   **The purpose for which the product is being imported**
    -   for sale or lease in Canada without modification
    -   for sale or lease in Canada after modification to comply with the prescribed energy efficiency standard
    -   for use as a component in a product exported from Canada

---
**Warning: Incomplete Documents**

If the customs release document is not complete or if the product does not meet energy efficiency standards, the customs officer may refuse to allow the product to clear customs.
---

The customs release document must be submitted electronically to the Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA). Dealers must always include the required information on the customs release document, regardless of when the product was manufactured.
