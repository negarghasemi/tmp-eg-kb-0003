# Air-source Heat Pumps: Energy Efficiency Overview

## ENERGY STAR Certification
An **ENERGY STAR certified air-source heat pump** uses **5% less energy**, on average, than a standard model. Those with the **ENERGY STAR Most Efficient** designation offer even greater energy savings, contributing to reduced costs and a smaller carbon footprint.

*Find and compare product models using the [searchable product lists for ENERGY STAR air-source heat pumps](https://spl-lpi.nrcan-rncan.gc.ca/en-US/).*

## How an Air-Source Heat Pump Works
Air-source heat pumps operate by **transferring heat** rather than generating it. In cooler months, they extract heat from outside air and transfer it indoors. During warmer periods, they provide cooling by moving warm indoor air outside. This heat transfer mechanism makes them highly energy-efficient for year-round temperature control.

*Learn more in the booklet: [Heating and Cooling with a Heat Pump](/energy-efficiency/energy-star/heating-cooling-heat-pump).*

## Helpful Tips for Heat Pump Management
- **Efficiency Ratings**: When purchasing, look for:
    - **HSPF2 Region V** (Heating Seasonal Performance Factors): Indicates winter efficiency. Higher is better.
    - **SEER2** (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio): Indicates summer efficiency. Higher is better.
- **Sizing & Selection**: Proper sizing is crucial for maximizing energy savings and comfort. Consult a mechanical contractor.
- **Thermostat Setting**: Set your thermostat to one consistent temperature to avoid higher utility costs.
- **Winter Maintenance**: Regularly check for and remove excessive ice or snow build-up on or around the unit to ensure proper operation.
- **Annual Servicing**: The best time for servicing is at the end of summer, before the heating season begins.
- **Filter & Coil Cleaning**: Clean filters and coils monthly, or as per manufacturer instructions, to significantly impact system performance and lifespan.
- **Professional Inspection**: Hire a qualified service contractor for an annual inspection.

## Energy Efficiency Regulations
Air-source, ground-source, and water-source heat pumps sold in Canada are subject to **Canada's Energy Efficiency Regulations**, which establish minimum performance standards for energy efficiency.

*Details can be found in the [Guide to the Regulations](/energy-efficiency/energy-efficiency-regulations/guide-canada-s-energy-efficiency-regulations).*
