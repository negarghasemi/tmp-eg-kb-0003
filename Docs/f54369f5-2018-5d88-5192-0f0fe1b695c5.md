# Save on Energy Program Information

*Supporting information for Save on Energy programs under the 2021-2024 CDM Framework.*

The **2021-2024 Conservation and Demand Management (CDM) Framework** focuses on cost-effectively meeting the needs of customers and the electricity system. The framework is on track to achieve its original energy and demand savings target. Enhancements will be made to programs to reflect changing market conditions and customer feedback.

## Business and Industry Programs

### Retrofit program

**Description**: Offers businesses a customer-friendly experience through an expanded list of energy-efficient measures, faster application review processes, and quicker incentive payments. Will accept custom applications from businesses, institutional, and industrial customers in Spring 2023.

**Measures**: Available under three streams: lighting, HVAC, and manufacturing and other equipment. Focuses on commonly used products and technologies, including popular measures from the previous custom track.

**Updates**: Incentives and measures will be updated throughout the four-year framework based on marketplace feedback. Suggestions for new measures can be submitted.

**More Information**: [Complete list of measures and incentives](/For-Business-and-Industry/Programs-and-incentives/Retrofit-Program/Retrofit-Program-Update). Contact a Save on Energy representative via the [Contact us page](/Contact).

### Industrial Energy Efficiency Program (IEEP)

**Description**: Replaced the Process and Systems Upgrade (PSU) program (discontinued Dec 31, 2020). Focuses on supporting participants with larger, more complex projects. Reduces administrative burden and addresses unique needs of industrial consumers.

**Contact**: IESO representative for more information.

### Energy Performance Program (EPP)

**Description**: Continues to be available for businesses to achieve continuous whole-building energy improvements under the pay-for-performance model. Available for commercial, institutional, and now industrial facilities.

**Enhancements**: Provides stability for participants and ensures sustained energy savings. Includes new payments for achieving annual peak demand savings. Contract term extended to three years, with upfront incentive payments for initial capital investments. Technical requirements streamlined.

**Incentive Information**:
-   **Energy Incentive**: **$0.04/kWh** paid annually over the contract term for metered savings compared against the facility baseline energy model.
-   **Peak Demand Incentive**: An additional **$50/kW** paid annually over the three-year term for metered savings compared against the facilitys average peak demand over summer months in the baseline year.

**Contact**: Speak with a Save on Energy representative via [Contact us](/Contact).

### Energy Manager program (Evolving to Strategic Energy Management program)

**Description**: Has built a skilled network of energy professionals. Nearly 200 Energy Managers supported since its introduction.

**Evolution in 2023**: Will evolve from the Embedded Energy Manager model to a **Strategic Energy Management (SEM) model**. Shifts incentive from direct salary funding to enhanced technical support and resources for companies with a dedicated energy management professional.

**SEM Program Benefits**: Greater flexibility, additional cost savings through increased training, access to industry tools and resources. Will launch in January 2023 with training opportunities (hands-on instruction, guidance to small cohorts by sector). Participants eligible for enabling incentives for energy management tools and services upon reaching milestones.

**Engagement**: IESO will engage with the marketplace for a smooth transition and feedback.

**Contact**: Speak with a Save on Energy representative via [Contact us](/Contact).

### Small Business Program

**Description**: Replaced the Small Business Lighting program. Designed to help small businesses reduce energy consumption at no cost. Offers direct-install incentives on an expanded range of equipment, including both lighting and non-lighting.

**Contact**: Save on Energy representative via the [Contact us page](/Contact).

### Foodservice Distributor Discount Program

**Description**: Offers instant discounts to commercial businesses purchasing eligible foodservice equipment from participating distributors/dealers for installation. Delivered in partnership with Enbridge.

**More Information**: [Foodservice Distributor Discount program web page](/Error-404?item=web%3a%7b33C27115-E8C0-46D7-8D6C-F63B15C9A291%7d%40en).

### Training and support opportunities

**Description**: Courses and workshops available for energy professionals. Training incentives will be available throughout the four-year framework for:
-   Energy management
-   Specialized skills training for systems and technologies
-   Professional development through industry certifications

**Resources**: Foundational energy-efficiency educational content, sector-specific information guides, and project-based case studies will be made available.

**More Information**: [Training and support web page](/Error-404?item=web%3a%7b3334F592-1950-4E63-AA33-5A6C6F1C5FBF%7d%40en).

## Programs for First Nations

**Description**: Programs assist on-reserve First Nations communities with energy-saving upgrades to improve homes and buildings and help residents save on energy costs.

### First Nations Community Building Retrofit Program

**Description**: For on-reserve First Nations communities in Ontario connected to the grid. Offers up to **$100,000** in funding and technical support for projects improving energy efficiency of band-owned commercial and institutional facilities.

### Remote First Nations Energy-Efficiency Program

**Description**: Provides funding support to remote First Nations communities to implement energy-efficient projects for better energy management and increased comfort in homes and businesses.

### Energy Affordability Program

**Description**: Also available to eligible grid-connected First Nation residents. Offers energy-saving upgrades tailored to specific home needs at no cost. Brochures in Ojibwe, Cree, and Oji-Cree are available.

**More Information**: Visit the [IESO's Ministerial Directives web page](https://ieso.ca/en/Corporate-IESO/Ministerial-Directives) for the directive. Contact a Save on Energy representative via the [Contact us page](/Contact).

## Residential Programs and Resources

### Energy Affordability Program

**Description**: Offers expanded eligibility from the previous Home Assistance Program. Supports income-eligible electricity consumers in lowering monthly electricity costs and increasing home comfort.

**Benefits**: Participants may qualify for:
-   Free energy needs assessment by a trained energy professional, identifying upgrades like replacement appliances and weather stripping.
-   Free energy-saving kits customized with items like LED lighting, timers, faucet aerators, and/or a clothes drying line.

**Contact**: Call **1-844-770-3148** to discuss eligibility and opportunities.

### My Energy Action Plan

**Description**: Online tool launched in March 2022. Provides residential consumers with low-cost, no-cost ways to improve home energy efficiency.

**Features**: Online questionnaire offering tailored advice and tips to manage electricity use, improve home comfort, and save on energy costs.

### Tips and information

**Description**: Continues to offer residential consumers information and tips on saving energy and managing electricity costs.

**Resources**: [Resources](/For-Your-Home/Advice-and-Tips) on the Save on Energy website for smart buying decisions on energy-efficient equipment and tips for improving living/working spaces. Content will be refreshed to reflect seasonality and societal needs.

## Additional Resources and Opportunities

### Local program opportunities

**Description**: Targeted local programming to maximize value to ratepayers and customers. Uses energy efficiency to manage local electricity demand and avoid/defer infrastructure investments. Initiatives will be deployed in targeted areas based on IESO's regional planning process. Leverages competitive mechanisms for targeted energy and demand savings, innovation, and cost savings.

**More Information**: [Learn more about local initiatives](/For-Business-and-Industry/Programs-and-incentives/Local-Initiatives).

## Coming in 2023

Save on Energy is developing new programs based on customer feedback. The following are planned to launch in 2023:

-   **Strategic Energy Management program**: Evolution of the Energy Manager program, providing training, resources, and enhanced technical support to companies with a dedicated energy manager.
-   **Existing Building Commissioning program**: Helps optimize operations and improve energy efficiency based on current facility requirements.
-   **Commercial Midstream Lighting program**: Lighting incentives for distributors to increase sales of energy-efficient lighting through point-of-sale discounts.
-   **Additional local initiatives**: In targeted areas of the province with electricity constraints.

**Announced on October 4, 2022, to launch by Spring 2023**:

-   New **residential demand response program**: For homes with existing central air conditioning and smart thermostats to lower energy use at peak times.
-   **Targeted support for greenhouse growers** in Southwest Ontario: Incentives for LED lighting, advanced controls, or behind-the-meter distributed energy resources (DER) like combined solar generation and battery storage.
-   Enhancements to the **Save on Energy Business Retrofit Program**: To include custom energy-efficiency projects for businesses, institutional, and industrial customers.
-   Enhancements to the **Save on Energy Local Initiatives Program**: To reduce participation barriers and add flexibility for incentives for DER solutions.

*Stay tuned for more information on these initiatives as they become available.*
